## Authority Tier
Court Rule / Official System (Administrative Office of the U.S. Courts)

## Currency Warning
Most federal courts have migrated to "NextGen CM/ECF," which requires a unified PACER account. Always check individual district court websites for local e-filing rules (Local Rules / General Orders).

## Key Facts
1. **PACER:** Public Access to Court Electronic Records. Used for searching, tracking, and downloading federal court documents.
2. **CM/ECF:** Case Management/Electronic Case Files. The system used by attorneys to file documents with the court.
3. **Registration:** Attorneys must register for a PACER account and then apply for e-filing privileges in each specific district or circuit court where they practice.
4. **Fees:** PACER charges $0.10 per page ($3.00 max per document), but fees are waived if they total less than $30.00 in a quarter.
5. **Electronic Service:** Filing a document through CM/ECF generally constitutes service on other registered users in the case (Fed. R. Civ. P. 5(b)(2)(E)).
6. **Notice of Electronic Filing (NEF):** Automatically generated upon filing; serves as proof of service and provides one "free look" at the document to all parties.
7. **Redaction Requirements:** Attorneys are responsible for redacting sensitive information (SSNs, DOBs, minor names, financial account numbers) under Fed. R. Civ. P. 5.2.
8. **Technical Failures:** Most courts have rules providing relief if a deadline is missed due to a system-wide CM/ECF outage, but personal technical issues (internet down) are usually not excused.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Case Management/Electronic Case Files (CM/ECF) system is the Federal Judiciary's comprehensive case management system for all appellate, district, and bankruptcy courts." — USCourts.gov.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must master the CM/ECF interface to avoid procedural dismissals. "E-filing at the eleventh hour" is a high-risk activity; most practitioners recommend filing at least two hours before the midnight deadline to account for system lag or upload errors. Redaction is a critical ethical and procedural duty; failure to redact can lead to sanctions.
