## Currency Warning
Cybersecurity standards for lawyers are shifting from "recommended" to "mandatory." Several states (e.g., NY) have introduced specific "CLE" requirements for cybersecurity.

## Key Facts
1. **Duty to Protect (Rule 1.6(c)):** Lawyers must make "reasonable efforts" to prevent unauthorized access to client data. What is "reasonable" depends on the sensitivity of the data and the current state of technology.
2. **ABA Formal Opinion 477R:** Lawyers must use "proportionate" security measures. For highly sensitive data, standard unencrypted email may be insufficient; encrypted portals or end-to-end encryption may be required.
3. **ABA Formal Opinion 483 (Data Breach):** Lawyers have an ethical duty to:
   - **Monitor:** Actively monitor for breaches.
   - **Stop:** Take immediate action to stop the breach and mitigate damage.
   - **Investigate:** Conduct a post-breach investigation to determine what was lost.
   - **Notify:** Promptly notify current clients (and potentially former clients) if their "protected information" was compromised.
4. **Encryption:** Is now considered a de facto requirement for mobile devices and cloud storage in a law firm.
5. **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Essential for all firm accounts (email, practice management, bank).
6. **State Breach Notification Laws:** Every U.S. state has its own statute (e.g., Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.82) requiring notification to individuals (and often the Attorney General) if personal information is compromised.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"When a data breach occurs, a lawyer has a duty to notify current clients... to the extent reasonably necessary to permit the client to make informed decisions regarding the representation." — ABA Formal Opinion 483.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates are "Chief Information Security Officers" by default. They must implement a written "Incident Response Plan" (IRP) and ensure all firm data is encrypted and backed up. Cyber-liability insurance is becoming as critical as malpractice insurance to cover the costs of forensic investigation and client notification after a breach.
