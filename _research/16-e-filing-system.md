## Currency Warning
The USPTO and Copyright Office are in the middle of major "IT Modernization" efforts. The USPTO's **TEAS** system is being replaced by the **Trademark Center**, and the Copyright Office is launching the **Enterprise Copyright System** (ECS).

## Key Facts
1. **USPTO (Trademarks):** TEAS (Trademark Electronic Application System). Requires a verified "USPTO.gov" account. Almost all trademark filings must be electronic.
2. **USPTO (Patents):** Patent Center. Used for filing and managing patent applications. Replaced the older EFS-Web system.
3. **U.S. Copyright Office:** eCO (Electronic Copyright Office). Used for registering literary, visual, and performing arts works.
4. **SEC (Securities):** EDGAR (Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval). Used by public companies for mandatory filings (10-K, 10-Q).
5. **Mandatory Filing:** For many agencies (including USPTO), paper filings are now either prohibited or subject to a significant "paper filing surcharge."
6. **Electronic Signatures:** Most agencies accept "S-signatures" (e.g., /John Doe/) in lieu of wet-ink signatures for electronic filings.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates practicing Intellectual Property (IP) must maintain active and secure accounts with the USPTO and Copyright Office. Because these systems are distinct from the court's CM/ECF, practitioners must manage multiple sets of credentials and "docketing" deadlines. Failure to respond to a USPTO "Office Action" via the e-filing portal results in the "abandonment" of the trademark application.
