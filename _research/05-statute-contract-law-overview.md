# Contract Law Overview: UCC & Restatement — USA

2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (UCC — adopted by states) / Secondary (Restatement Second)

## Currency Warning
The UCC is periodically updated; notably, the 2022 Amendments (Article 12) address "Controllable Electronic Records" (digital assets/crypto). Always check if the state legislature has adopted the latest version.

## Key Facts
1. **UCC Article 2:** Governs the sale of "goods" (moveable objects). Does not apply to service contracts or real estate.
2. **Restatement (Second) of Contracts:** A highly persuasive synthesis of common law used for non-goods contracts (services, real estate, employment).
3. **Formation:** Requires Offer, Acceptance, and Consideration. The UCC is more flexible regarding "open terms" (price, delivery) than common law.
4. **Statute of Frauds:** Certain contracts must be in writing (e.g., sale of goods >$500 under UCC 2-201; real estate; contracts over 1 year).
5. **Parol Evidence Rule:** Generally prohibits using outside evidence to contradict a "fully integrated" written contract.
6. **Breach and Remedies:**
   - **Expectation Damages:** Putting the non-breaching party in the position they would have been in if the contract was performed (the standard remedy).
   - **Specific Performance:** Only available when the subject matter is "unique" (e.g., real estate).
7. **Unconscionability:** Courts may refuse to enforce contracts that are fundamentally unfair in their formation or terms.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A contract for sale of goods may be made in any manner sufficient to show agreement, including conduct by both parties which recognizes the existence of such a contract." — UCC § 2-204(1).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must first identify if a dispute involves "goods" (UCC) or "services" (Common Law/Restatement) as the rules for formation and breach differ. Mastery of the Statute of Frauds is a primary defense in many contract disputes. For digital economy clients, understanding the new UCC Article 12 is becoming critical.
