# Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP) — USA

## Source URL
https://www.uscourts.gov/rules-policies/current-rules-practice-procedure/federal-rules-civil-procedure

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Court Rule (Supreme Court Promulgated)

## Currency Warning
The FRCP are amended annually (typically effective Dec 1). Ensure checking the latest House Document for Advisory Committee Notes. Significant 2015 amendments focused on proportionality in discovery (Rule 26).

## Key Facts
1. **Scope:** These rules govern the procedure in all civil actions and proceedings in the United States district courts (Rule 1).
2. **Commencing Action:** A civil action is commenced by filing a complaint with the court (Rule 3).
3. **Pleading Standards:** Rule 8 requires a "short and plain statement of the claim showing that the pleader is entitled to relief" (Twombly/Iqbal standard).
4. **Service:** Rule 4 governs summons and service of process.
5. **Motions to Dismiss:** Rule 12(b) provides for defenses including lack of jurisdiction (1-3), insufficient process (4-5), and failure to state a claim (6).
6. **Discovery:** Rules 26–37 govern discovery. Rule 26(b)(1) limits discovery to nonprivileged matter that is relevant and proportional to the needs of the case.
7. **Summary Judgment:** Rule 56 allows for judgment as a matter of law if there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact.
8. **E-Discovery:** Rule 37(e) addresses the failure to preserve electronically stored information (ESI).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"These rules... should be construed, administered, and employed by the court and the parties to secure the just, speedy, and inexpensive determination of every action and proceeding." — FRCP Rule 1.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must strictly adhere to the timelines in Rule 12 (21 days to respond to a complaint) and Rule 26 (initial disclosures). Proportionality is a key defense against "discovery abuse" by larger firms with more resources. Mastery of Rule 56 is essential for resolving cases without the high cost of trial.
