## Currency Warning
This is a rapidly evolving area. ABA Formal Opinion 512 (released 2024) is the current definitive guide. Multiple state bars (CA, FL, MI, NJ, NY) have issued their own specific guidelines on Generative AI.

## Key Facts
1. **Duty of Competence (Rule 1.1):** Lawyers must keep abreast of the "benefits and risks associated with relevant technology," which now explicitly includes AI.
2. **Confidentiality (Rule 1.6):** Lawyers must ensure AI tools do not disclose confidential client information to third parties. Specifically, inputting client data into "public" or "open" AI models that use inputs for training is generally an ethics violation.
3. **Duty to Supervise (Rules 5.1 & 5.3):** Lawyers are responsible for the work product of AI, just as they are for a junior associate or paralegal. "Hallucinations" (fake cases cited by AI) are the lawyer's responsibility.
4. **Candor Toward the Tribunal (Rule 3.3):** Filing documents with AI-generated fake citations can lead to sanctions (e.g., *Mata v. Avianca*).
5. **Billing (Rule 1.5):** Lawyers generally cannot bill "billable hours" for time saved by AI. They must bill for actual time spent or use flat fees. They cannot mark up the cost of an AI subscription as a "disbursement" beyond actual cost.
6. **Communication (Rule 1.4):** Lawyers may have a duty to inform clients if AI is being used for significant tasks, depending on the client agreement and the nature of the task.
7. **Judicial Orders:** Many federal and state judges have issued "Standing Orders" requiring lawyers to certify that any AI-generated filings have been verified by a human.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A lawyer may use GAI tools... provided the lawyer's use of the GAI tool is consistent with the lawyer's professional obligations, including the duties of competence, confidentiality, meritorious claims and contentions, candor toward the tribunal, and supervision." — ABA Formal Opinion 512.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must choose "Enterprise" or "Legal-Specific" AI tools that guarantee data privacy (Opt-out of training). Using free, public versions of ChatGPT for client work is a high-risk ethical violation. Every AI-generated draft must be "shepardized" (verified) manually to ensure citations are real and accurate.
