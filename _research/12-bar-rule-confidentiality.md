# Bar Rules: Confidentiality of Information — USA

## Source URL
https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/rule_1_6_confidentiality_of_information/

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule (ABA Model Rules)

## Currency Warning
Rule 1.6(c) was added/strengthened recently to mandate "reasonable efforts" to prevent the inadvertent or unauthorized disclosure of client information, specifically addressing the use of cloud storage and email.

## Key Facts
1. **The Core Duty:** A lawyer shall not reveal information relating to the representation of a client unless the client gives informed consent, the disclosure is impliedly authorized to carry out the representation, or a specific exception applies.
2. **Breadth of Duty:** The duty of confidentiality is much broader than the Attorney-Client Privilege. It covers ALL information related to the representation, regardless of the source.
3. **Exceptions (Permissive Disclosure):** A lawyer *may* reveal information to the extent the lawyer reasonably believes necessary:
   - To prevent reasonably certain death or substantial bodily harm.
   - To prevent the client from committing a crime or fraud that is reasonably certain to result in substantial injury to the financial interests or property of another.
   - To secure legal advice about the lawyer’s compliance with these Rules (ethics counsel).
   - To establish a claim or defense in a controversy between the lawyer and the client (the "self-defense" exception).
4. **Technology Safeguards (Rule 1.6(c)):** A lawyer shall make reasonable efforts to prevent the inadvertent or unauthorized disclosure of, or unauthorized access to, information relating to the representation of a client.
5. **Persistence:** The duty of confidentiality continues even after the client-lawyer relationship has terminated (Rule 1.9(c)).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A lawyer shall not reveal information relating to the representation of a client unless the client gives informed consent, the disclosure is impliedly authorized in order to carry out the representation or the disclosure is permitted by paragraph (b)." — ABA Model Rule 1.6(a).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates are personally responsible for their firm's "data hygiene." Using unencrypted public Wi-Fi or losing an unencrypted laptop containing client files can be an ethics violation under Rule 1.6(c). When using AI tools or cloud storage, the lawyer must ensure the provider has "reasonable" security and privacy policies.
