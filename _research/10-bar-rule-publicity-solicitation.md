## Currency Warning
The ABA significantly streamlined Rules 7.1–7.5 in 2018. Most states have adopted or are in the process of adopting these simplified rules. Always check the specific state's rules (e.g., California Rule 7.1-7.5, NY Rule 7.1-7.5).

## Key Facts
1. **Rule 7.1 (General Rule):** A lawyer shall not make a false or misleading communication about the lawyer or the lawyer’s services. A communication is false or misleading if it contains a material misrepresentation of fact or law, or omits a fact necessary to make the statement as a whole not materially misleading.
2. **Rule 7.2 (Advertising):**
   - A lawyer may communicate information regarding the lawyer’s services through any media.
   - A lawyer shall not give anything of value to a person for recommending the lawyer’s services (with limited exceptions like referral services).
   - Any communication must include the name and contact information of at least one lawyer or law firm responsible for its content.
3. **Rule 7.3 (Solicitation):**
   - **In-person/Live Telephone/Real-time Electronic Contact:** Prohibited when a significant motive is the lawyer's pecuniary gain, unless the person contacted is a lawyer, or has a family, close personal, or prior business relationship with the lawyer.
   - **Targeted Mailings:** Generally permitted but must not involve coercion, duress, or harassment.
4. **Specialization:** A lawyer may communicate that they do or do not practice in particular fields of law, but shall not state or imply they are "certified as a specialist" unless certified by an approved organization and the name of that organization is clearly identified.
5. **Firm Names:** Must not be misleading. Trade names are permitted if they do not imply a connection with a government agency or that they are a public or charitable legal services organization.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A lawyer shall not by in-person, live telephone or real-time electronic contact solicit professional employment when a significant motive for the lawyer’s doing so is the lawyer’s pecuniary gain..." — ABA Model Rule 7.3(b).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must be extremely careful with "ambulance chasing" tactics. While website advertising and social media posts are generally fine (Rule 7.2), reaching out directly to a victim of an accident via DM or text for the purpose of getting hired is likely a violation of Rule 7.3. Testimonials and "success rates" must be carefully phrased to avoid being "misleading" under Rule 7.1.
