## Authority Tier
Secondary / Industry Standard (The Bluebook)

## Currency Warning
The 21st Edition (2020) is the current standard. Note that some states have their own citation manuals (e.g., *California Style Manual*, *New York Law Reports Style Guide*).

## Key Facts
1. **Federal Cases:**
   - **Supreme Court:** *Name v. Name*, [Volume] U.S. [Page] (Year). Example: *Brown v. Bd. of Educ.*, 347 U.S. 483 (1954).
   - **Courts of Appeals:** *Name v. Name*, [Volume] F.[Series] [Page] ([Circuit] Year). Example: *United States v. Caseres*, 122 F.3d 1274 (9th Cir. 1997).
   - **District Courts:** *Name v. Name*, [Volume] F. Supp. [Series] [Page] ([District] Year). Example: *Abbott v. United States*, 111 F. Supp. 2d 111 (D. Mass. 2000).
2. **Statutes:**
   - **Federal:** [Title] U.S.C. § [Section]. Example: 18 U.S.C. § 3282.
   - **State (e.g., California):** Cal. [Code] Code § [Section]. Example: Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.100.
3. **Constitution:**
   - **Federal:** U.S. Const. art. [Article], § [Section]. Example: U.S. Const. art. III, § 1.
   - **Amendments:** U.S. Const. amend. [Number]. Example: U.S. Const. amend. XIV.
4. **Regulations:**
   - **Federal:** [Title] C.F.R. § [Section] (Year). Example: 21 C.F.R. § 101.9 (2020).
5. **Short Form:** Used after the full citation has been provided. Example: *Brown*, 347 U.S. at 485; *Id.* at 486.
6. **Bluepages:** The section of The Bluebook specifically for practitioners (non-academic citation).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must ensure every brief filed in court follows the jurisdiction's preferred citation style. While Bluebook is the gold standard, some local judges are sticklers for local manuals (e.g., California Style Manual). Incorrect citation can signal a lack of professionalism or "sloppiness" to the court.
