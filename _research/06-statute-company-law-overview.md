## Currency Warning
The DGCL is amended annually. Recent amendments (2023/2024) addressed officer exculpation and the use of blockchain for corporate records.

## Key Facts
1. **Dominance of Delaware:** Over 60% of Fortune 500 companies and the majority of startups are incorporated in Delaware due to its flexible law and specialized Court of Chancery.
2. **Internal Affairs Doctrine:** The law of the state of incorporation (e.g., Delaware) governs the internal relationships between shareholders, directors, and officers, regardless of where the company operates.
3. **Fiduciary Duties:** Directors and officers owe duties of **Care** and **Loyalty** (including Good Faith) to the corporation and its shareholders.
4. **Business Judgment Rule (BJR):** A presumption that in making a business decision, directors acted on an informed basis, in good faith, and in the honest belief that the action taken was in the best interests of the company.
5. **Formation:** Requires filing a Certificate of Incorporation with the Secretary of State.
6. **Stock Issuance:** DGCL Section 151 governs the classes and series of stock.
7. **Mergers and Acquisitions:** DGCL Section 251 governs the process for domestic mergers.
8. **Shareholder Derivative Suits:** Mechanisms for shareholders to sue on behalf of the corporation for breaches of fiduciary duty, subject to "demand" requirements.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The business and affairs of every corporation organized under this chapter shall be managed by or under the direction of a board of directors..." — DGCL § 141(a).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates advising startups must be familiar with the DGCL even if they are not located in Delaware. "Foreign qualification" is required if a Delaware corp is "doing business" in another state (e.g., a Delaware corp with an office in NY must register as a foreign corp in NY). The Court of Chancery's extensive caselaw provides high predictability for corporate disputes.
