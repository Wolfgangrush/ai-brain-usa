## Currency Warning
The Corporate Transparency Act (CTA) took effect January 1, 2024. Reporting deadlines for existing companies (those formed before 2024) was January 1, 2025. This is a massive new compliance burden for small businesses and their lawyers.

## Key Facts
1. **The Mandate:** "Reporting companies" (mostly small LLCs and corporations) must report Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN).
2. **Beneficial Owner:** Any individual who, directly or indirectly: (1) exercises substantial control over the company; or (2) owns or controls at least 25% of the ownership interests.
3. **Company Applicant:** For entities formed after Jan 1, 2024, the person who filed the formation document (and the person primarily responsible for directing the filing) must also be reported. **This often includes the lawyer or paralegal.**
4. **Exemptions:** 23 categories of entities are exempt, including "Large Operating Companies" (>20 full-time employees and >$5M gross receipts) and highly regulated entities (banks, insurance).
5. **Reporting Deadlines:**
   - Entities formed before 2024: By Jan 1, 2025.
   - Entities formed in 2024: Within 90 days.
   - Entities formed in 2025+: Within 30 days.
6. **Penalties:** Civil penalties up to $500 per day; criminal penalties up to $10,000 and 2 years in prison for "willful" failure to report.
7. **AML for Lawyers Generally:** While lawyers are not currently "Financial Institutions" under the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), the CTA brings them into the AML net as "Company Applicants."

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates forming entities for clients MUST decide if they will handle CTA filings. If acting as the "Company Applicant," the lawyer's own info (name, DOB, address, ID number) is permanently stored in the FinCEN database for that entity. Many firms are using "Engagement Letter" language to explicitly disclaim responsibility for ongoing CTA compliance (e.g., reporting changes in ownership).
