# Mandatory Continuing Legal Education (MCLE) — USA

2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Bar / Regulator Rule (State Bar MCLE Boards)

## Currency Warning
Many states have recently increased requirements for "Technology" and "Diversity/Inclusion" (EDI) credits. Always check the current year's requirements on the state bar's MCLE portal.

## Key Facts
1. **General Requirement:** 46 states require Mandatory CLE (MCLE).
2. **Standard Hours:** Typically 12–15 hours per year or 24–30 hours every 2 years.
3. **Core Subject Areas:**
   - **Ethics:** Almost all states require 1–2 hours per year of Ethics/Professional Responsibility.
   - **Technology:** States like Florida and North Carolina specifically require "Technology" credits.
   - **Mental Health/Substance Abuse:** Increasing number of states require periodic credits in "Well-being."
4. **Reporting Cycles:** Vary by state (e.g., California is on a 3-year cycle, New York on a 2-year cycle).
5. **Credit Types:** "Live" vs. "On-Demand/Self-Study." Most states now allow a significant portion (or 100%) to be completed via webinars or recorded videos.
6. **Non-Compliance:** Failure to report credits or pay the reporting fee can lead to "Administrative Suspension" of the law license.
7. **Pro Bono Credit:** Some states allow lawyers to earn CLE credits by performing pro bono service (e.g., 1 credit for every 3 hours of pro bono).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must track their own credits as they don't have a firm "CLE Coordinator." Missing a deadline leads to late fees and the risk of suspension. Utilizing free CLEs offered by local bar associations or low-cost bundles (PLI, Lawline) is the most efficient way to maintain compliance.
