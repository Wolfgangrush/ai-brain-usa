## Currency Warning
The FAA was amended by the **Ending Forced Arbitration of Sexual Assault and Sexual Harassment Act of 2021**, which makes pre-dispute arbitration agreements unenforceable in these specific cases.

## Key Facts
1. **Federal Arbitration Act (FAA):** The primary federal statute governing arbitration. It mandates that arbitration agreements in contracts involving interstate commerce are "valid, irrevocable, and enforceable."
2. **Supreme Court Stance:** The SCOTUS has consistently upheld a "liberal federal policy favoring arbitration agreements," often preempting state laws that attempt to restrict arbitration (e.g., *Epic Systems Corp. v. Lewis*).
3. **Enforcement:** Courts must stay litigation if an issue is referable to arbitration under a written agreement (9 U.S.C. § 3).
4. **Awards:** Arbitrators' awards can be confirmed by a court and turned into a judgment.
5. **Vacating an Award:** Extremely difficult. Under 9 U.S.C. § 10, an award can only be vacated for fraud, corruption, evident partiality, or where arbitrators exceeded their powers. "Manifest disregard of the law" is a narrow, common-law ground in some circuits.
6. **Mediation:** Unlike arbitration, mediation is non-binding. Most federal and state courts now have mandatory "Alternative Dispute Resolution" (ADR) programs requiring parties to mediate before going to trial.
7. **Confidentiality:** Mediation communications are generally privileged and inadmissible in subsequent proceedings under FRE 408 and various state "Mediation Confidentiality" statutes (e.g., Cal. Evid. Code § 1119).

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"A written provision in... a contract evidencing a transaction involving commerce to settle by arbitration a controversy thereafter arising out of such contract or transaction... shall be valid, irrevocable, and enforceable..." — 9 U.S.C. § 2.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates representing consumers or employees often view arbitration as a "forced" and unfavorable forum. Conversely, for business clients, it is a tool for confidentiality and speed. Understanding the narrow grounds for vacating an award is critical; once an arbitrator rules, the "case is over" for all practical purposes.
