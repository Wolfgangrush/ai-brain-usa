## Currency Warning
The Department of Labor (DOL) recently increased the salary threshold for "exempt" employees under the FLSA (2024/2025). Many state laws (e.g., California's AB5) have significantly changed the criteria for classifying workers as "Independent Contractors" vs. "Employees."

## Key Facts
1. **At-Will Employment:** The default rule in almost all U.S. states (except Montana); an employer can fire an employee for any reason or no reason, as long as it is not an *illegal* reason.
2. **Title VII (1964 Civil Rights Act):** Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), and national origin. Applies to employers with 15+ employees.
3. **ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act):** Prohibits discrimination against qualified individuals with disabilities and requires "reasonable accommodations."
4. **ADEA (Age Discrimination in Employment Act):** Protects workers aged 40 and older.
5. **FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act):** Governs the federal minimum wage, overtime pay (1.5x for hours > 40), and child labor. Distinguishes between "Exempt" and "Non-Exempt" employees.
6. **FMLA (Family and Medical Leave Act):** Provides up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave for specific family and medical reasons.
7. **Worker Classification:** The "ABC Test" (in some states) or the DOL "Economic Realities Test" is used to determine if a worker is an independent contractor. Misclassification can lead to massive liability for unpaid taxes and overtime.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"It shall be an unlawful employment practice for an employer... to fail or refuse to hire or to discharge any individual... because of such individual’s race, color, religion, sex, or national origin." — 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-2(a).

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates acting as "outside GC" for small businesses must perform "Worker Classification Audits" to ensure clients aren't misclassifying employees as contractors to save on taxes. For plaintiff-side solos, "Wage and Hour" class actions (unpaid overtime) remain a highly lucrative, though complex, niche.
