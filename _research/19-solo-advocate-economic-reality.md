## Currency Warning
Inflation and the rise of "alternative legal service providers" (ALSPs) are putting downward pressure on some solo rates, while specialized niches (e.g., AI Law, High-Stakes Litigation) see rate growth.

## Key Facts
1. **Average Hourly Rates:** Solos average approximately $288/hour, though this varies significantly by geography (e.g., $400+ in NYC/SF vs. $200 in rural areas).
2. **Collection Rates:** A major "leak" in solo economics. On average, solos only collect about 85-90% of what they bill.
3. **Overhead:** Typically ranges from 30% to 50% of gross revenue. Major costs include malpractice insurance, research databases (Westlaw/Lexis), and office/software subscriptions.
4. **Revenue Patterns:** Often "feast or famine" due to the difficulty of consistent marketing while simultaneously handling active cases.
5. **Billing Models:** 
   - **Hourly:** Still the dominant model.
   - **Flat Fee:** Increasing in popularity for predictable matters (uncontested divorce, basic estate planning, trademark filing).
   - **Contingency:** Common in personal injury; 33%–40% of the final recovery.
6. **Self-Employment Tax:** Solos must account for the full 15.3% SECA tax (employer + employee share), often managed via an S-Corp election for tax efficiency.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
The "Collection Gap" is the single biggest threat to solo profitability. Implementing automated credit card payments (LawPay) and "evergreen" retainers (where the client must replenish the trust account once it hits a certain floor) is essential for economic survival.
