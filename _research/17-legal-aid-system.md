# Legal Aid System — USA

## Source URL
https://www.lsc.gov/

## Last Verified
2026-05-18

## Authority Tier
Statute (Legal Services Corporation Act of 1974) / Regulator (LSC)

## Currency Warning
Federal funding for legal aid (LSC) is a perennial budget battle in Congress. Recent years have seen increased focus on "pro bono" requirements and "civil Gideon" movements in specific cities/states.

## Key Facts
1. **Legal Services Corporation (LSC):** A publicly funded, 501(c)(3) non-profit established by Congress to provide financial support for civil legal aid to low-income Americans.
2. **Eligibility:** Generally limited to individuals with incomes at or below 125% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines (approx. $38,000 for a family of four in 2026).
3. **Scope of Services:** Focuses on "bread and butter" civil issues: housing (eviction defense), family law (domestic violence), consumer protection, and access to government benefits.
4. **Prohibitions:** LSC-funded organizations are strictly prohibited from handling "fee-generating" cases (like personal injury), class actions, or political lobbying.
5. **Pro Bono Requirements:** Many state bars strongly "encourage" 50 hours of pro bono service per year from every licensed attorney. Some states (like NY) require 50 hours of pro bono for admission to the bar.
6. **Judicial Council / Access to Justice Commissions:** Most states have established commissions to improve access to the courts for self-represented (pro se) litigants.

## Verbatim Quote (if applicable)
"The Corporation shall provide financial assistance to qualified programs furnishing legal assistance to eligible clients..." — 42 U.S.C. § 2996f.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates often accept "modest means" cases or work as "contract attorneys" for legal aid organizations to supplement their practice. Many solos also use pro bono service as a way to gain trial experience in areas of law they are looking to expand into.
