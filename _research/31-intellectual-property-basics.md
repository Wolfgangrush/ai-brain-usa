## Currency Warning
The **CASE Act** (2020) created a small-claims board for copyright disputes, providing a lower-cost alternative to federal court. The **Trademark Modernization Act** (2020) introduced new procedures for expunging unused trademark registrations.

## Key Facts
1. **Copyright (17 U.S.C.):** Protects "original works of authorship fixed in any tangible medium of expression." Protection is automatic upon creation, but registration is required before filing an infringement suit.
2. **Trademarks (15 U.S.C. - Lanham Act):** Protects words, names, symbols used in commerce to identify the source of goods/services. Rights can be "common law" (use-based) or federal (registration-based).
3. **Patents (35 U.S.C.):** Protects new and useful processes, machines, manufactures, or compositions of matter. Requires a rigorous examination by the USPTO.
4. **Trade Secrets:** Protected primarily by the **Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA)** (18 U.S.C. § 1836) and state-level Uniform Trade Secrets Acts (UTSA). Requires "reasonable measures" to keep the information secret.
5. **Fair Use (Copyright):** An equitable doctrine (17 U.S.C. § 107) allowing limited use of copyrighted material for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, or research.
6. **Work Made for Hire:** Under Rule 17 U.S.C. § 101, the employer or commissioning party is the "author" of a work created by an employee within the scope of employment or a specifically commissioned work with a written agreement.

## Practical Implication for Solo Advocate
Solo advocates must ensure that for any creative work (software code, marketing materials) produced by a contractor, there is a written "Work Made for Hire" or "Assignment of Rights" agreement. Without it, the contractor may still own the copyright, even if they were paid in full.
