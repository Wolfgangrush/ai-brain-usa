## Federal Courts — CITED:01-court-hierarchy.md

- **SCOTUS:** Supreme Court of the United States, Washington DC. Apex court. Discretionary appellate jurisdiction via writ of certiorari.
- **13 Circuit Courts:** U.S. Courts of Appeals. Mandatory appellate jurisdiction over district courts. 12 regional + Federal Circuit.
- **94 District Courts:** U.S. District Courts. Original jurisdiction over federal questions and diversity of citizenship (>$75,000).
- **Specialized:** Bankruptcy Courts (11 U.S.C.), Tax Court, Court of Federal Claims, Court of International Trade.
- **Dual Court System:** Federal + 50 independent state court systems. Concurrent jurisdiction in some matters. Removal from state to federal possible.

## Federal Procedural Rules — CITED:02,03-court-rules-*.md

- **FRCP:** Federal Rules of Civil Procedure. Governs civil actions in US District Courts.
- **FRCrP:** Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure. Governs criminal proceedings.
- **FRE:** Federal Rules of Evidence. Governs admissibility.
- **FRAP:** Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure. Governs appeals to circuit courts.

## Federal Statutes — CITED:04-08 statute files

- **US Constitution:** Article III establishes judicial branch. Article I § 8 enumerates federal powers.
- **Bankruptcy Code (11 U.S.C.):** Chapters 7 (liquidation), 11 (reorganization), 13 (individual adjustment).
- **Patent Act (35 U.S.C.):** Federal patent protection. Federal Circuit exclusive appellate jurisdiction.
- **Copyright Act (17 U.S.C.):** Copyright protection. Includes DMCA.
- **Sherman Act (15 U.S.C. §§ 1-7) + Clayton Act:** Federal antitrust.
- **Securities Act 1933 + Securities Exchange Act 1934:** SEC enforcement. Federal court jurisdiction.
- **IRC (26 U.S.C.):** Internal Revenue Code. Tax Court jurisdiction.
- **ECPA + CFAA (18 U.S.C.):** Electronic communications privacy + computer fraud.
- **BSA + CTA:** Bank Secrecy Act + Corporate Transparency Act (FinCEN). BOI reporting. CURRENCY: CTA enforcement rolling out.
- **FAA (9 U.S.C.):** Federal Arbitration Act. Preempts state arbitration law.

## State Statutes (Sample) — CITED:04-statute-data-protection.md

- **CCPA 2018 + CPRA 2020 (California):** Strongest US data privacy law. Applies to CA residents.
- **NY SHIELD Act:** New York data breach notification.
- **DGCL:** Delaware General Corporation Law. Dominant for US incorporation.

## Bar Rules — CITED:10,11,12 bar-rule files

- **ABA Model Rule 7 (formerly 7.1-7.5):** Streamlined 2018. Governs lawyer advertising, solicitation, specialization claims, firm names. No false/misleading communications.
- **ABA Model Rule 5.5:** Unauthorized practice of law. Multijurisdictional practice restrictions. Pro hac vice admission.
- **ABA Model Rule 1.6:** Confidentiality of information. Attorney-client privilege.
- **ABA Formal Opinion 512:** AI ethics floor. CURRENCY: state-specific AI opinions emerging monthly.
- **ABA Formal Opinion 483:** Lawyers' obligations after data breach.
- **ABA Formal Opinion 477R:** Securing communication of protected client information.

## Citation Format — CITED:13,14-citation-format-*.md

## AI Regulatory Stance — CITED:23-ai-law-firm-regulatory-stance.md

ABA Formal Opinion 512 is the current floor for AI use in legal practice. Key principles:
- Competence (MR 1.1): Lawyers must understand AI tool capabilities and limitations.
- Confidentiality (MR 1.6): AI tools must not expose client confidences.
- Supervision (MR 5.1/5.3): Lawyers must supervise AI outputs; cannot delegate legal judgment.
- Communication (MR 1.4): Clients must be informed about AI use in their matters.
