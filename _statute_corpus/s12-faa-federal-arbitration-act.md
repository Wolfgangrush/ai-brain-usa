## Section-by-Section Digest

### § 2 — Validity, Irrevocability, and Enforcement of Agreements to Arbitrate

**Plain-English summary:** A written provision in any maritime transaction or contract evidencing a transaction involving commerce to settle by arbitration a controversy thereafter arising shall be valid, irrevocable, and enforceable, save upon such grounds as exist at law or in equity for the revocation of any contract.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> A written provision in any maritime transaction or a contract evidencing a transaction involving commerce to settle by arbitration a controversy thereafter arising out of such contract or transaction… or an agreement in writing to submit to arbitration an existing controversy arising out of such a contract, transaction, or refusal, shall be valid, irrevocable, and enforceable, save upon such grounds as exist at law or in equity for the revocation of any contract.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. § 2.

**Practical use:** The "savings clause" permits invalidation only on generally applicable contract-law grounds — *AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion*, 563 U.S. 333 (2011), preempted state rules disfavoring class-action waivers. *Epic Sys. Corp. v. Lewis*, 584 U.S. 497 (2018), confirmed enforceability of employment class-action waivers.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 3 — Stay of Proceedings

**Plain-English summary:** When any issue in a suit is referable to arbitration under a written agreement, the court shall on application stay the trial of the action until arbitration has been had.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> If any suit or proceeding be brought in any of the courts of the United States upon any issue referable to arbitration under an agreement in writing for such arbitration, the court in which such suit is pending, upon being satisfied that the issue involved in such suit or proceeding is referable to arbitration under such an agreement, shall on application of one of the parties stay the trial of the action until such arbitration has been had in accordance with the terms of the agreement, providing the applicant for the stay is not in default in proceeding with such arbitration.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. § 3.

**Practical use:** Stay is mandatory once arbitrability is found. *Smith v. Spizzirri*, 601 U.S. 472 (2024) — § 3 requires a stay; courts may not dismiss in lieu of staying.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 4 — Failure to Arbitrate; Petition to Compel

**Plain-English summary:** A party aggrieved by the failure of another to arbitrate under a written agreement may petition the U.S. district court for an order directing that arbitration proceed as provided in the agreement.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. § 4.

**Practical use:** Vehicle for motions to compel arbitration. The court determines arbitrability unless the parties have "clearly and unmistakably" delegated that question to the arbitrator (*Henry Schein, Inc. v. Archer & White Sales, Inc.*, 586 U.S. 63 (2019)).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 9 — Confirmation of Arbitration Award

**Plain-English summary:** If the parties have agreed that judgment shall be entered upon the award, then at any time within one year after the award the parties may apply to the court specified in the agreement for an order confirming the award, and thereupon the court must grant such an order unless the award is vacated, modified, or corrected.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> If the parties in their agreement have agreed that a judgment of the court shall be entered upon the award made pursuant to the arbitration… then at any time within one year after the award is made any party to the arbitration may apply to the court so specified for an order confirming the award, and thereupon the court must grant such an order unless the award is vacated, modified, or corrected as prescribed in sections 10 and 11 of this title.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. § 9.

**Practical use:** Confirmation is summary and mandatory unless statutory vacatur grounds exist.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 10 — Vacatur of Award

**Plain-English summary:** A court may vacate an arbitration award only on four narrow grounds: (1) corruption, fraud, undue means; (2) evident partiality or corruption of arbitrators; (3) misconduct in refusing postponement / refusing to hear pertinent evidence / other prejudicial misbehavior; (4) arbitrators exceeded powers or so imperfectly executed them that a mutual, final, and definite award was not made.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> In any of the following cases the United States court in and for the district wherein the award was made may make an order vacating the award upon the application of any party to the arbitration — (1) where the award was procured by corruption, fraud, or undue means; (2) where there was evident partiality or corruption in the arbitrators, or either of them; (3) where the arbitrators were guilty of misconduct in refusing to postpone the hearing, upon sufficient cause shown, or in refusing to hear evidence pertinent and material to the controversy; or of any other misbehavior by which the rights of any party have been prejudiced; or (4) where the arbitrators exceeded their powers, or so imperfectly executed them that a mutual, final, and definite award upon the subject matter submitted was not made.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. § 10(a).

**Practical use:** § 10 grounds are the exclusive vacatur grounds for FAA awards — *Hall Street Assocs. v. Mattel, Inc.*, 552 U.S. 576 (2008), foreclosed contractual expansion of judicial review.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### §§ 201–208 — New York Convention (International)

**Plain-English summary:** Chapter 2 implements the Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards (New York Convention, 1958). Federal courts have jurisdiction over actions under the Convention; awards are enforceable subject to seven exclusive defenses in Article V.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. §§ 201–208; New York Convention art. V.

**Practical use:** Foundation of international-arbitration enforcement in U.S. courts. *GE Energy Power Conversion France SAS v. Outokumpu Stainless USA, LLC*, 590 U.S. 432 (2020), confirmed non-signatory equitable-estoppel doctrines may apply.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### §§ 401–402 — Sexual Harassment Carve-Out (EFAA)

**Plain-English summary:** Notwithstanding any predispute arbitration agreement, at the election of the person alleging sexual-harassment or sexual-assault conduct, the predispute agreement is unenforceable with respect to a case relating to that conduct.

**Citation format:** 9 U.S.C. § 402(a).

**Practical use:** *Famuyide v. Chipotle Mexican Grill, Inc.* (and following decisions) construe the EFAA broadly: once invoked, the entire case (not just the harassment count) is removed from arbitration.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every motion to compel arbitration: § 2 validity → § 4 compel → § 3 stay.
- Every vacatur petition lives or dies on § 10(a)(1)–(4) framing.
- International cases: route through Chapter 2 (NY Convention) — 9 U.S.C. §§ 201–208.
- Sexual-harassment plaintiffs: invoke § 402(a) election at earliest opportunity.
