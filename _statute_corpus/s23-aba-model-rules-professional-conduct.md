# ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct

## Citation Block
- **Source:** American Bar Association, Center for Professional Responsibility.
- **Status:** Model Rules — adopted (in varying form) by 49 states + D.C. + U.S. Virgin Islands as the basis for binding rules of professional conduct. California adopted its own Rules of Professional Conduct in 2018, modeled on the Model Rules.
- **Official URL:** [https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct)
- **Bluebook Citation:** *Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. [N.N] (Am. Bar Ass'n [year])*; for state-enacted versions, cite the state rule (e.g., *N.Y. Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 1.6*).

## Authority Tier
Tier 1 — Legal Ethics Backbone.

## Currency Block
- **Original Adoption:** ABA House of Delegates, August 1983 (replacing the 1969 Model Code of Professional Responsibility).
- **Major Revisions:** Ethics 2000 Commission (2002); Commission on Ethics 20/20 (2012).
- **Latest amendments:** ABA Formal Opinion 512 (29 July 2024) on use of generative AI tools (competence under Rule 1.1; confidentiality under Rule 1.6; supervision under Rules 5.1, 5.3; reasonable fees under Rule 1.5; candor under Rule 3.3). ABA Formal Opinion 506 (May 2023) on responding to charges of professional misconduct.

## Rule-by-Rule Digest

### Rule 1.1 — Competence

**Plain-English summary:** A lawyer shall provide competent representation to a client. Competent representation requires the legal knowledge, skill, thoroughness, and preparation reasonably necessary for the representation.

**Text (verbatim):**
> A lawyer shall provide competent representation to a client. Competent representation requires the legal knowledge, skill, thoroughness and preparation reasonably necessary for the representation.

**Comment [8] (post-2012 Ethics 20/20):**
> To maintain the requisite knowledge and skill, a lawyer should keep abreast of changes in the law and its practice, including the benefits and risks associated with relevant technology…

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 1.1.

**Practical use:** Comment [8] tech-competence is foundation of ABA Formal Opinion 512 (2024) guidance on generative AI tools.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 1.6 — Confidentiality of Information

**Plain-English summary:** A lawyer shall not reveal information relating to the representation of a client unless the client gives informed consent, the disclosure is impliedly authorized to carry out the representation, or the disclosure is permitted by enumerated exceptions in (b) (including to prevent reasonably certain death/substantial bodily harm, to prevent crime/fraud, to comply with court order).

**Text (verbatim, partial):**
> (a) A lawyer shall not reveal information relating to the representation of a client unless the client gives informed consent, the disclosure is impliedly authorized in order to carry out the representation or the disclosure is permitted by paragraph (b).
> (c) A lawyer shall make reasonable efforts to prevent the inadvertent or unauthorized disclosure of, or unauthorized access to, information relating to the representation of a client.

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 1.6.

**Practical use:** Generative-AI use is permissible only with appropriate safeguards per ABA Formal Opinion 512 — self-learning tools that train on client data raise § 1.6(c) "reasonable efforts" issues.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 1.7 — Conflict of Interest: Current Clients

**Plain-English summary:** A lawyer shall not represent a client if the representation involves a concurrent conflict. A concurrent conflict exists if: (1) the representation will be directly adverse to another current client; or (2) there is a significant risk that the representation will be materially limited. Notwithstanding, representation is permitted if (1) the lawyer reasonably believes she can provide competent and diligent representation, (2) the representation is not prohibited by law, (3) it does not involve assertion of a claim by one client against another client in the same proceeding, and (4) each affected client gives informed consent confirmed in writing.

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 1.7(a)–(b).

**Practical use:** Conflict checks at intake and at lateral hiring. Some conflicts cannot be cured by consent (1.7(b)(2)–(3)).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 3.3 — Candor Toward the Tribunal

**Plain-English summary:** A lawyer shall not knowingly: (1) make a false statement of fact or law to a tribunal or fail to correct a false statement of material fact or law previously made; (2) fail to disclose to the tribunal legal authority in the controlling jurisdiction known to the lawyer to be directly adverse to the position of the client and not disclosed by opposing counsel; or (3) offer evidence the lawyer knows to be false.

**Text (verbatim, partial):**
> (a) A lawyer shall not knowingly: (1) make a false statement of fact or law to a tribunal or fail to correct a false statement of material fact or law previously made to the tribunal by the lawyer; (2) fail to disclose to the tribunal legal authority in the controlling jurisdiction known to the lawyer to be directly adverse to the position of the client and not disclosed by opposing counsel; or (3) offer evidence that the lawyer knows to be false…

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 3.3.

**Practical use:** AI-hallucinated citations — *Mata v. Avianca, Inc.*, 678 F. Supp. 3d 443 (S.D.N.Y. 2023), is the watershed sanctions decision. ABA Formal Opinion 512 ties Rule 3.3 verification duty to all AI-assisted filings.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 4.2 — Communication with Person Represented by Counsel

**Plain-English summary:** In representing a client, a lawyer shall not communicate about the subject of the representation with a person the lawyer knows to be represented by another lawyer in the matter, unless the lawyer has the consent of the other lawyer or is authorized to do so by law or a court order.

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 4.2.

**Practical use:** Workhorse "no-contact" rule. Comment [7] addresses communications with constituents of represented organizations — high-level personnel, persons whose acts may be imputed, and persons whose statements may constitute admissions.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 5.5 — Unauthorized Practice of Law; Multijurisdictional Practice

**Plain-English summary:** A lawyer shall not practice law in a jurisdiction in violation of the regulation of the legal profession in that jurisdiction or assist another in doing so. Subsection (c) permits limited temporary multijurisdictional practice under enumerated safe harbors (association with local lawyer, pending or potential ADR, reasonably-related-to-practice).

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 5.5.

**Practical use:** Foundation of foreign-lawyer/out-of-state practice analysis. Several jurisdictions (Utah, Arizona) have adopted alternative business structures permitting non-lawyer ownership.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 7.1 — Communications Concerning a Lawyer's Services

**Plain-English summary:** A lawyer shall not make a false or misleading communication about the lawyer or the lawyer's services. A communication is false or misleading if it contains a material misrepresentation of fact or law, or omits a fact necessary to make the statement considered as a whole not materially misleading.

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 7.1.

**Practical use:** 2018 amendments consolidated Rules 7.1–7.5 and removed many in-person solicitation prohibitions; specifics vary materially by state.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 8.4 — Misconduct

**Plain-English summary:** It is professional misconduct for a lawyer to: (a) violate or attempt to violate the Rules, knowingly assist or induce another to do so, or do so through the acts of another; (b) commit a criminal act that reflects adversely on the lawyer's honesty, trustworthiness, or fitness; (c) engage in conduct involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or misrepresentation; (d) engage in conduct that is prejudicial to the administration of justice; (g) engage in conduct that the lawyer knows or reasonably should know is harassment or discrimination based on protected characteristics in connection with the practice of law.

**Citation format:** Model Rules of Pro. Conduct r. 8.4.

**Practical use:** 2016 amendment added Rule 8.4(g) (anti-discrimination/harassment) — adoption is mixed across the states.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every engagement letter addresses scope (Rule 1.2), fees (Rule 1.5), and confidentiality (Rule 1.6).
- Every AI-assisted filing has documented verification of citations to satisfy Rule 3.3 candor.
- Every multijurisdictional engagement passes the Rule 5.5 safe-harbor test.
- Every conflict-of-interest waiver is informed and confirmed in writing under Rule 1.7(b)(4).
