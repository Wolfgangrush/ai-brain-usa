## Section-by-Section Digest

### § 1331 — Federal Question Jurisdiction

**Plain-English summary:** District courts have original jurisdiction over all civil actions arising under the Constitution, laws, or treaties of the United States.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> The district courts shall have original jurisdiction of all civil actions arising under the Constitution, laws, or treaties of the United States.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 1331.

**Practical use:** Pleaded in every federal-question complaint. Read with the "well-pleaded complaint" rule — *Louisville & Nashville R.R. v. Mottley*, 211 U.S. 149 (1908) — and *Grable & Sons Metal Prods. v. Darue Eng'g & Mfg.*, 545 U.S. 308 (2005), for substantial federal issue jurisdiction.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 1332(a) — Diversity Jurisdiction

**Plain-English summary:** District courts have jurisdiction over civil actions where the amount in controversy exceeds $75,000 exclusive of interest and costs and the parties are citizens of different states (or foreign citizens).

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> The district courts shall have original jurisdiction of all civil actions where the matter in controversy exceeds the sum or value of $75,000, exclusive of interest and costs, and is between — (1) citizens of different States; (2) citizens of a State and citizens or subjects of a foreign state…; (3) citizens of different States and in which citizens or subjects of a foreign state are additional parties; and (4) a foreign state… as plaintiff and citizens of a State or of different States.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 1332(a).

**Practical use:** Requires complete diversity under *Strawbridge v. Curtiss*, 7 U.S. (3 Cranch) 267 (1806). For corporations, citizenship is place of incorporation and principal place of business under *Hertz Corp. v. Friend*, 559 U.S. 77 (2010).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 1391(b) — Venue in Civil Actions

**Plain-English summary:** A civil action may be brought in (1) any district where any defendant resides, if all defendants reside in the same state; (2) any district where a substantial part of the events giving rise to the claim occurred or where a substantial part of property is situated; or (3) if no other district fits, any district where any defendant is subject to personal jurisdiction.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> A civil action may be brought in — (1) a judicial district in which any defendant resides, if all defendants are residents of the State in which the district is located; (2) a judicial district in which a substantial part of the events or omissions giving rise to the claim occurred, or a substantial part of property that is the subject of the action is situated; or (3) if there is no district in which an action may otherwise be brought as provided in this section, any judicial district in which any defendant is subject to the court's personal jurisdiction with respect to such action.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b).

**Practical use:** Anchor for venue analysis; pair with § 1404(a) transfer motions and § 1406 cure-or-dismiss for improper venue.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 1404(a) — Change of Venue

**Plain-English summary:** For the convenience of parties and witnesses, in the interest of justice, a district court may transfer a civil action to any other district where it might have been brought or to which all parties have consented.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> For the convenience of parties and witnesses, in the interest of justice, a district court may transfer any civil action to any other district or division where it might have been brought or to any district or division to which all parties have consented.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 1404(a).

**Practical use:** Read with *Atl. Marine Constr. Co. v. U.S. Dist. Court*, 571 U.S. 49 (2013) — forum-selection clauses control absent extraordinary circumstances.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 1441(a) — Removal of Civil Actions

**Plain-English summary:** Any civil action in a State court of which the district courts have original jurisdiction may be removed by the defendant to the district court embracing the place where the action is pending.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> Except as otherwise expressly provided by Act of Congress, any civil action brought in a State court of which the district courts of the United States have original jurisdiction, may be removed by the defendant or the defendants, to the district court of the United States for the district and division embracing the place where such action is pending.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 1441(a).

**Practical use:** Removal mechanics in § 1446; remand for defects or lack of jurisdiction in § 1447. Removal time limit is 30 days after defendant receives the initial pleading (§ 1446(b)).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 1746 — Unsworn Declarations Under Penalty of Perjury

**Plain-English summary:** Wherever any rule, regulation, or law requires a sworn declaration, an unsworn declaration in writing signed and dated by the declarant under penalty of perjury has the same effect.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Wherever, under any law of the United States or under any rule, regulation, order, or requirement made pursuant to law, any matter is required or permitted to be supported, evidenced, established, or proved by the sworn declaration, verification, certificate, statement, oath, or affidavit, in writing of the person making the same…, such matter may, with like force and effect, be supported, evidenced, established, or proved by the unsworn declaration, certificate, verification, or statement, in writing of such person which is subscribed by him, as true under penalty of perjury, and dated, in substantially the following form: "I declare (or certify, verify, or state) under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct. Executed on (date). (Signature)."

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 1746.

**Practical use:** Foundation for declarations used in motions under FRCP 56, 12, 65 — no notary required.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 2201 — Declaratory Judgments

**Plain-English summary:** In a case of actual controversy within its jurisdiction, any court of the United States may declare the rights and other legal relations of any interested party seeking such declaration, whether or not further relief is or could be sought.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> In a case of actual controversy within its jurisdiction… any court of the United States, upon the filing of an appropriate pleading, may declare the rights and other legal relations of any interested party seeking such declaration, whether or not further relief is or could be sought. Any such declaration shall have the force and effect of a final judgment or decree and shall be reviewable as such.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. § 2201(a).

**Practical use:** Read with *MedImmune, Inc. v. Genentech, Inc.*, 549 U.S. 118 (2007), for the case-or-controversy threshold in declaratory actions.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 2241–§ 2255 — Habeas Corpus

**Plain-English summary:** § 2241 grants federal courts general habeas jurisdiction; § 2254 governs state-prisoner habeas (subject to AEDPA deference); § 2255 is the exclusive remedy for federal prisoners challenging the legality of detention.

**Citation format:** 28 U.S.C. §§ 2241, 2254, 2255.

**Practical use:** AEDPA (Antiterrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act, 1996) imposes one-year limitation period and deferential review under § 2254(d). Successive petitions barred under § 2244(b).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every complaint identifies jurisdiction under § 1331 or § 1332 plus venue under § 1391.
- Every removal petition follows § 1446 timing and § 1441 scope.
- Every declaration cites § 1746 to avoid notarisation.
