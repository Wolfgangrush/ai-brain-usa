# Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)

## Citation Block
- **Statute:** Pub. L. 95-213, codified at 15 U.S.C. §§ 78dd-1, 78dd-2, 78dd-3 (anti-bribery) and 78m(b)(2) (accounting provisions).
- **Official URL:** [https://www.justice.gov/criminal/criminal-fraud/foreign-corrupt-practices-act](https://www.justice.gov/criminal/criminal-fraud/foreign-corrupt-practices-act)
- **Bluebook Citation:** *15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1* (issuers); *15 U.S.C. § 78dd-2* (domestic concerns); *15 U.S.C. § 78dd-3* (territorial jurisdiction).

## Authority Tier
Tier 1 — Corporate Compliance Backbone.

## Currency Block
- **Original Enactment:** 19 December 1977.
- **Major Amendments:** 1988 Trade Act (added "knowing" standard, affirmative defenses); 1998 International Anti-Bribery Act (implementing OECD Anti-Bribery Convention; added § 78dd-3 territorial jurisdiction).
- **Doctrinal currents:** *United States v. Hoskins*, 902 F.3d 69 (2d Cir. 2018) — foreign nationals not within enumerated FCPA categories cannot be liable under conspiracy/accomplice theory. *Kelly v. United States*, 590 U.S. 391 (2020) (wire-fraud "object of fraud" — affects parallel FCPA-adjacent prosecutions).
- **Enforcement Policy:** DOJ FCPA Corporate Enforcement Policy (Justice Manual § 9-47.120) presumes declination for voluntary self-disclosure plus full cooperation plus timely remediation, absent aggravating circumstances. SEC enforcement runs in parallel via Exchange Act § 21C.

## Language
English (Authoritative).

## Scope of Digest
- **Provisions covered:** Anti-bribery (15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1, -2, -3); accounting provisions (15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(2)(A)–(B)); affirmative defenses (local-law and reasonable-expenses); penalties.

## Section-by-Section Digest

### Anti-Bribery — Elements

**Plain-English summary:** It is unlawful for any (a) issuer (§ 78dd-1), (b) domestic concern (§ 78dd-2), or (c) any person while in the territory of the United States (§ 78dd-3), or any officer, director, employee, agent, or stockholder acting on their behalf, to make use of interstate commerce corruptly in furtherance of any offer, payment, promise to pay, or authorization of the payment of anything of value to any foreign official, foreign political party, or candidate for foreign political office, for the purpose of influencing any act or decision in their official capacity, securing an improper advantage, or inducing them to use their influence with a foreign government to assist in obtaining or retaining business.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial — § 78dd-1(a)):**
> It shall be unlawful for any issuer which has a class of securities registered pursuant to section 78l of this title or which is required to file reports under section 78o(d) of this title, or for any officer, director, employee, or agent of such issuer or any stockholder thereof acting on behalf of such issuer, to make use of the mails or any means or instrumentality of interstate commerce corruptly in furtherance of an offer, payment, promise to pay, or authorization of the payment of any money, or offer, gift, promise to give, or authorization of the giving of anything of value to — (1) any foreign official…

**Citation format:** 15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1(a), § 78dd-2(a), § 78dd-3(a).

**Practical use:** Five elements: (1) covered person, (2) corrupt intent, (3) use of interstate commerce (jurisdictional element; for territorial cases under § 78dd-3, presence in the U.S. suffices), (4) thing of value to a foreign official, (5) for an improper business purpose.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Accounting Provisions (15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(2))

**Plain-English summary:** Every issuer with a registered class of securities shall: (A) make and keep books, records, and accounts that accurately and fairly reflect the transactions and dispositions of assets; and (B) devise and maintain a system of internal accounting controls sufficient to provide reasonable assurances that transactions are executed in accordance with management's authorization, recorded as necessary to permit preparation of financial statements in conformity with GAAP, access to assets is permitted only in accordance with management's authorization, and recorded accountability for assets is compared with existing assets at reasonable intervals.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Every issuer which has a class of securities registered pursuant to section 78l of this title and every issuer which is required to file reports pursuant to section 78o(d) of this title shall — (A) make and keep books, records, and accounts, which, in reasonable detail, accurately and fairly reflect the transactions and dispositions of the assets of the issuer; and (B) devise and maintain a system of internal accounting controls sufficient to provide reasonable assurances that — (i) transactions are executed in accordance with management's general or specific authorization; (ii) transactions are recorded as necessary…

**Citation format:** 15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(2)(A)–(B).

**Practical use:** No corrupt-intent requirement; strict-liability bookkeeping. Penalties under § 78m(b)(4) (knowing falsification) — up to $5M / 20 years for individuals; up to $25M for entities.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Affirmative Defenses (15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1(c))

**Plain-English summary:** Two affirmative defenses: (1) the payment, gift, offer, or promise was lawful under the written laws and regulations of the foreign official's country; or (2) the payment was a reasonable and bona fide expenditure (e.g., travel and lodging) directly related to (A) the promotion, demonstration, or explanation of products or services, or (B) the execution or performance of a contract with a foreign government or agency thereof.

**Citation format:** 15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1(c), § 78dd-2(c).

**Practical use:** Local-law defense narrowly construed — must be a written law. Reasonable-expenses defense — DOJ Opinion Procedure Releases provide guidance.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Penalties

**Plain-English summary:** Anti-bribery: individuals — up to $250,000 (or twice the gross gain/loss under 18 U.S.C. § 3571(d)) and 5 years' imprisonment; entities — up to $2,000,000 (or twice the gross gain/loss). Accounting provisions: individuals — up to $5,000,000 and 20 years; entities — up to $25,000,000. Civil penalties under §§ 78ff and 78u-2.

**Citation format:** 15 U.S.C. § 78dd-2(g), § 78ff.

**Practical use:** DOJ/SEC parallel resolutions typical for issuer cases. Disgorgement under SEC remedies (subject to *Liu v. SEC*, 591 U.S. 71 (2020), and *Kokesh v. SEC*, 581 U.S. 455 (2017)).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.
