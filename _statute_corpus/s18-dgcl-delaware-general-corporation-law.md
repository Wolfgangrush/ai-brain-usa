# Delaware General Corporation Law (DGCL)

## Citation Block
- **Statute:** Title 8, Chapter 1 of the Delaware Code.
- **Official URL:** [https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/index.html](https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/index.html)
- **Bluebook Citation:** *Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § [section]* (e.g., Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 141(a)).

## Authority Tier
Tier 1 — Corporate Backbone (state law; controls majority of U.S. public companies and a large share of private companies through choice-of-incorporation).

## Currency Block
- **Original Code:** 1899 (modernized 1967; multiple cycles since).
- **2024 Amendments:** Pub. Act enacted summer 2024 in response to *West Palm Beach Firefighters' Pension Fund v. Moelis & Co.* (Del. Ch. 2024) — added § 122(18) explicitly authorizing stockholder agreements with corporate governance terms.
- **Doctrinal currents:** *Tornetta v. Musk*, 310 A.3d 430 (Del. Ch. 2024) (controller-conflict cleansing under *MFW*); *Sears Hometown & Outlet Stores, Inc. v. Lampert*, 309 A.3d 474 (Del. Ch. 2024) (advance-notice bylaws).

## Language
English (Authoritative).

## Scope of Digest
- **Sections covered:** § 102 (certificate of incorporation), § 109 (bylaws), § 141 (board of directors), § 144 (interested-director transactions), § 151 (classes/series of stock), § 211 (annual meetings), § 220 (books and records inspection), § 251 (mergers), § 262 (appraisal rights), § 271 (sale of assets).

## Section-by-Section Digest

### § 102(b)(7) — Director Exculpation; Officer Exculpation (2022 Amendment)

**Plain-English summary:** The certificate of incorporation may eliminate or limit director liability for breach of fiduciary duty, except for: (i) duty of loyalty breaches; (ii) acts not in good faith or involving intentional misconduct or knowing violation of law; (iii) § 174 violations (unlawful dividends); (iv) transactions from which the director derived an improper personal benefit. 2022 amendment extended exculpation to certain senior officers, except in claims by or in the right of the corporation.

**Citation format:** Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 102(b)(7).

**Practical use:** Standard provision in Delaware certificates of incorporation. Director claims surviving § 102(b)(7) typically allege bad-faith *Caremark* failure or duty-of-loyalty conflict.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 141(a) — Board of Directors; Powers

**Plain-English summary:** The business and affairs of every corporation organized under this chapter shall be managed by or under the direction of a board of directors, except as may be otherwise provided in this chapter or in its certificate of incorporation.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> The business and affairs of every corporation organized under this chapter shall be managed by or under the direction of a board of directors, except as may be otherwise provided in this chapter or in its certificate of incorporation.

**Citation format:** Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 141(a).

**Practical use:** Foundation of board centrality. Read with § 122(18) (2024) — stockholder agreements may bind board action where authorized.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 144 — Interested Directors and Officers; Quorum

**Plain-English summary:** A contract or transaction between a corporation and one or more of its directors or officers, or between a corporation and any other entity in which one or more of its directors or officers are directors or officers, or have a financial interest, shall not be void or voidable solely for this reason if: (1) the material facts are disclosed and the board or committee in good faith authorizes by majority of disinterested directors; (2) the material facts are disclosed and shareholders approve; or (3) the contract or transaction is fair to the corporation as of the time it is authorized.

**Citation format:** Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 144.

**Practical use:** Statutory safe harbor for conflicted transactions. Read with *Kahn v. M&F Worldwide Corp.* (MFW), 88 A.3d 635 (Del. 2014), six-step cleansing for controller transactions to obtain business-judgment review.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 220 — Books and Records Inspection

**Plain-English summary:** Any stockholder, in person or by attorney, upon written demand under oath stating the purpose, shall have the right to inspect for any proper purpose the corporation's stock ledger, list of stockholders, and other books and records.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Any stockholder, in person or by attorney or other agent, shall, upon written demand under oath stating the purpose thereof, have the right during the usual hours for business to inspect for any proper purpose, and to make copies and extracts from: (1) The corporation's stock ledger, a list of its stockholders, and its other books and records; and (2) A subsidiary's books and records…

**Citation format:** Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 220(b).

**Practical use:** Plaintiff's tool to investigate before filing derivative complaint. Recent doctrinal current emphasises proper purpose plus credible-basis ("infer wrongdoing") standard.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 251 — Merger or Consolidation of Domestic Corporations

**Plain-English summary:** Two or more domestic corporations may merge into a single corporation pursuant to an agreement of merger approved by the board and (with limited exceptions) the stockholders. Section 251(h) permits a back-end merger without stockholder vote if the tender-offer acquirer holds the requisite majority of outstanding shares.

**Citation format:** Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 251.

**Practical use:** Foundation of M&A. § 251(h) medium-form merger is the standard public-company two-step. Appraisal rights under § 262 available for objecting stockholders.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 262 — Appraisal Rights

**Plain-English summary:** A stockholder who properly perfects appraisal rights in a merger or consolidation receives the "fair value" of the shares as determined by the Court of Chancery in a judicial proceeding, exclusive of any element of value arising from the accomplishment or expectation of the merger.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Any stockholder of a corporation of this State who holds shares of stock on the date of the making of a demand pursuant to subsection (d) of this section with respect to such shares, who continuously holds such shares through the effective date of the merger or consolidation, who has otherwise complied with subsection (d) of this section and who has neither voted in favor of the merger or consolidation nor consented thereto in writing… shall be entitled to an appraisal by the Court of Chancery of the fair value of the stockholder's shares of stock under the circumstances described in subsections (b) and (c) of this section.

**Citation format:** Del. Code Ann. tit. 8, § 262.

**Practical use:** Post-*DFC Global Corp. v. Muirfield Value Partners, L.P.*, 172 A.3d 346 (Del. 2017), and *Dell, Inc. v. Magnetar Global Event Driven Master Fund*, 177 A.3d 1 (Del. 2017), merger price has heightened evidentiary weight in arm's-length deals.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every certificate of incorporation contains § 102(b)(7) director (and now officer) exculpation.
- Every M&A agreement addresses § 251 vote requirements and § 262 appraisal-rights notices.
- Every books-and-records demand satisfies § 220 form-of-demand and proper-purpose requirements.
- Every controller transaction is structured under *MFW* / § 144 to obtain business-judgment review.
