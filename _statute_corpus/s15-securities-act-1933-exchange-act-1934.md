## Section-by-Section Digest

### Securities Act § 5 — Registration Requirement

**Plain-English summary:** Unless a registration statement is in effect, it is unlawful to sell or deliver any security in interstate commerce. § 5(c) prohibits offers before filing; § 5(a) prohibits sales before effectiveness; § 5(b) governs prospectus form.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Unless a registration statement is in effect as to a security, it shall be unlawful for any person, directly or indirectly — (1) to make use of any means or instruments of transportation or communication in interstate commerce or of the mails to sell such security through the use or medium of any prospectus or otherwise; or (2) to carry or cause to be carried through the mails or in interstate commerce, by any means or instruments of transportation, any such security for the purpose of sale or for delivery after sale.

**Citation format:** Securities Act § 5(a), 15 U.S.C. § 77e(a).

**Practical use:** Foundation of every offering analysis. Exemptions live in §§ 3 and 4 (private placement under § 4(a)(2) and Regulation D Rule 506); resale exemption under § 4(a)(1) (no longer "underwriter, issuer, dealer") and Rule 144.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Securities Act § 11 — Civil Liability on Registration Statement

**Plain-English summary:** If any part of the registration statement, when it became effective, contained an untrue statement of a material fact or omitted to state a material fact required or necessary to make the statements not misleading, any person acquiring the security may sue the issuer, every signer, every director, the underwriter, and the experts (with due-diligence defenses).

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> In case any part of the registration statement, when such part became effective, contained an untrue statement of a material fact or omitted to state a material fact required to be stated therein or necessary to make the statements therein not misleading, any person acquiring such security… may, either at law or in equity, in any court of competent jurisdiction, sue…

**Citation format:** Securities Act § 11(a), 15 U.S.C. § 77k(a).

**Practical use:** Strict-liability for issuers (defendants other than the issuer have due-diligence defenses under § 11(b)). Post-*Slack*, plaintiff must trace shares to the registration statement.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Exchange Act § 10(b) and Rule 10b-5

**Plain-English summary:** § 10(b) prohibits the use of any manipulative or deceptive device or contrivance in connection with the purchase or sale of any security. Rule 10b-5 implements the prohibition with three subparagraphs: (a) device, scheme, or artifice to defraud; (b) untrue statements of material fact or omissions; (c) acts, practices, or courses of business which operate as a fraud or deceit.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial — § 10(b)):**
> It shall be unlawful for any person, directly or indirectly, by the use of any means or instrumentality of interstate commerce or of the mails, or of any facility of any national securities exchange — (b) To use or employ, in connection with the purchase or sale of any security registered on a national securities exchange or any security not so registered… any manipulative or deceptive device or contrivance in contravention of such rules and regulations as the Commission may prescribe…

**Citation format:** Exchange Act § 10(b), 15 U.S.C. § 78j(b); 17 C.F.R. § 240.10b-5.

**Practical use:** Workhorse anti-fraud provision. Six elements for private cause of action: (1) material misrepresentation/omission, (2) scienter, (3) connection with purchase/sale, (4) reliance, (5) economic loss, (6) loss causation (*Dura Pharm., Inc. v. Broudo*, 544 U.S. 336 (2005)). PSLRA heightened-pleading at § 21D(b).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

### Exchange Act § 13(a) — Periodic Reporting

**Plain-English summary:** Every issuer of registered securities must file periodic reports as the SEC prescribes — Form 10-K (annual), Form 10-Q (quarterly), Form 8-K (current).

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Every issuer of a security registered pursuant to section 78l of this title shall file with the Commission, in accordance with such rules and regulations as the Commission may prescribe as necessary or appropriate for the proper protection of investors and to insure fair dealing in the security — (1) such information and documents… as the Commission shall require to keep reasonably current the information and documents required to be included in or filed with an application or registration statement…

**Citation format:** Exchange Act § 13(a), 15 U.S.C. § 78m(a).

**Practical use:** Foundation of public-company disclosure regime. Item 105 risk factors; Item 303 MD&A; Item 1.05 cybersecurity incidents (added July 2023 final rule).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Exchange Act § 16(b) — Short-Swing Profit Recovery

**Plain-English summary:** Any profit realized by an officer, director, or 10% beneficial owner from any purchase and sale (or sale and purchase) of equity securities of the issuer within any six-month period shall be recoverable by the issuer.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> For the purpose of preventing the unfair use of information which may have been obtained by such beneficial owner, director, or officer by reason of his relationship to the issuer, any profit realized by him from any purchase and sale, or any sale and purchase, of any equity security of such issuer… within any period of less than six months… shall inure to and be recoverable by the issuer…

**Citation format:** Exchange Act § 16(b), 15 U.S.C. § 78p(b).

**Practical use:** Strict-liability provision; no scienter required. Two-year statute of limitations from realization.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Exchange Act § 20(a) — Control-Person Liability

**Plain-English summary:** Every person who, directly or indirectly, controls any person liable under any provision of the chapter shall also be liable jointly and severally with and to the same extent, unless the controlling person had no knowledge of or reasonable ground to believe in the existence of the facts giving rise to the liability.

**Citation format:** Exchange Act § 20(a), 15 U.S.C. § 78t(a).

**Practical use:** Joint-and-several extension of primary 10(b) liability to officers/directors with control. Read with PSLRA's strong-inference standard for scienter.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.
