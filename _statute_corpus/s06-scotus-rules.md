## Section-by-Section Digest

### Rule 10 — Considerations Governing Review on Certiorari

**Plain-English summary:** Review on writ of certiorari is not a matter of right but of judicial discretion. A petition will be granted only for compelling reasons — typically a circuit split, conflict with state court of last resort, or a federal question of overriding importance.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Review on a writ of certiorari is not a matter of right, but of judicial discretion. A petition for a writ of certiorari will be granted only for compelling reasons. The following, although neither controlling nor fully measuring the Court's discretion, indicate the character of the reasons the Court considers:
> (a) a United States court of appeals has entered a decision in conflict with the decision of another United States court of appeals on the same important matter;
> (b) a United States court of appeals has decided an important federal question in a way that conflicts with a decision by a state court of last resort;
> (c) a state court of last resort has decided an important federal question in a way that conflicts with the decision of another state court of last resort or of a United States court of appeals;
> (d) a state court or a United States court of appeals has decided an important question of federal law that has not been, but should be, settled by this Court, or has decided an important federal question in a way that conflicts with relevant decisions of this Court.

**Citation format:** Sup. Ct. R. 10.

**Practical use:** Every cert petition's "Reasons for Granting" section maps to Rule 10(a)–(d). Vehicle-defect arguments live or die at this step.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

### Rule 13 — Review on Certiorari: Time for Petitioning

**Plain-English summary:** A petition for certiorari to review a judgment in any case must be filed within 90 days after entry of the judgment or after denial of a timely petition for rehearing. The time may be extended once by a Justice for up to 60 days.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Unless otherwise provided by law, a petition for a writ of certiorari to review a judgment in any case, civil or criminal, entered by a state court of last resort or a United States court of appeals (including the United States Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces) is timely when it is filed with the Clerk of this Court within 90 days after entry of the judgment.

**Citation format:** Sup. Ct. R. 13.1.

**Practical use:** 90-day clock is jurisdictional in civil cases. Rule 13.5 allows extension on application to a Justice.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 14 — Content of a Petition for a Writ of Certiorari

**Plain-English summary:** A cert petition must contain (i) Questions Presented, (ii) parties list and corporate disclosure, (iii) related cases, (iv) table of contents and authorities, (v) cited opinions, (vi) jurisdictional statement, (vii) constitutional, statutory, and regulatory provisions, (viii) statement of the case, (ix) Reasons for Granting the Petition, (x) appendix.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> A petition for a writ of certiorari shall contain, in the order indicated:
> (a) The questions presented for review, expressed concisely in relation to the circumstances of the case, without unnecessary detail. The questions should be short and should not be argumentative or repetitive.

**Citation format:** Sup. Ct. R. 14.1.

**Practical use:** Questions Presented are the first thing the Justices read. Argumentative framing is fatal. The Reasons-for-Granting section (Rule 14.1(h)) must map directly to Rule 10 factors.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

### Rule 20 — Procedure on a Petition for an Extraordinary Writ

**Plain-English summary:** Issuance of extraordinary writs (mandamus, prohibition, habeas) is not a matter of right but of discretion sparingly exercised, only when no adequate alternative remedy exists and the writ will serve as a means of correcting palpable error.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Issuance by the Court of an extraordinary writ authorized by 28 U.S.C. § 1651(a) is not a matter of right, but of discretion sparingly exercised. To justify the granting of any such writ, the petition must show that the writ will be in aid of the Court's appellate jurisdiction, that there are present exceptional circumstances warranting the exercise of the Court's discretionary powers, and that adequate relief cannot be obtained in any other form or from any other court.

**Citation format:** Sup. Ct. R. 20.1.

**Practical use:** Vehicle for emergency-docket relief and original-action petitions. Read with *Cheney v. U.S. Dist. Court for D.C.*, 542 U.S. 367 (2004), for the mandamus standard.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 22 — Applications to Individual Justices

**Plain-English summary:** A stay or injunction application must first be presented to the appropriate Justice (the Circuit Justice). The Justice may refer the application to the full Court.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> An application addressed to an individual Justice shall be filed with the Clerk, who will transmit it promptly to the Justice concerned if an individual Justice has jurisdiction to grant the relief sought.

**Citation format:** Sup. Ct. R. 22.

**Practical use:** Emergency-docket stay applications under *Nken* (or *South Bay United Pentecostal Church*-style equitable balancing) flow through the Circuit Justice first.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Rule 33 — Document Preparation: Booklet Format; 8½ × 11-Inch Paper Format

**Plain-English summary:** Documents must be printed in booklet format on opaque, unglazed paper, 6⅛ × 9¼ inches, with specific page limits and a covers-of-particular-colours scheme for each document type.

**Practical use:** Cert petitions are limited to 9,000 words (Rule 33.1(g)(ii)). Briefs on the merits are limited to 13,000 words (Rule 33.1(g)(i)). Reply briefs at cert stage: 3,000 words.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every cert petition opens with a tight Question Presented and maps Reasons for Granting to Rule 10(a)–(d).
- Every stay application routes through the Circuit Justice under Rule 22.
- Every brief tracks the booklet-format requirements under Rule 33.
