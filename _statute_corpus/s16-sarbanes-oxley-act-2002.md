- **Enacted:** 30 July 2002.

## Scope of Digest
- **Sections covered:** § 302 (officer certifications), § 304 (clawback), § 404 (internal-control reporting), § 802 (document destruction), § 806 (whistleblower retaliation — codified at 18 U.S.C. § 1514A), § 906 (criminal certification).

## Section-by-Section Digest

### § 302 — Corporate Responsibility for Financial Reports

**Plain-English summary:** Principal executive officer(s) and principal financial officer(s) must certify in each annual and quarterly report that: (1) the officer reviewed the report; (2) based on the officer's knowledge, the report does not contain material misstatements; (3) the financial information fairly presents in all material respects; (4) the signing officers are responsible for and have designed disclosure controls and internal controls.

**Codified at:** 15 U.S.C. § 7241; implementing rule at 17 C.F.R. § 240.13a-14.

**Practical use:** Every Form 10-K and 10-Q includes the § 302 certifications as exhibits 31.1 and 31.2.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 304 — Forfeiture of Certain Bonuses and Profits

**Plain-English summary:** If an issuer is required to prepare an accounting restatement due to material noncompliance, as a result of misconduct, with any financial reporting requirement, the CEO and CFO shall reimburse the issuer for (1) any bonus or other incentive-based or equity-based compensation received during the 12 months following the issuance of the noncompliant financial document, and (2) any profits realized from the sale of securities during that 12-month period.

**Codified at:** 15 U.S.C. § 7243.

**Practical use:** Clawback liability is strict — does not require officer personally engaged in the misconduct. Read with Dodd-Frank § 954 (15 U.S.C. § 78j-4) broader clawback (effective via SEC final rules October 2022).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 404 — Management Assessment of Internal Controls

**Plain-English summary:** Each annual report must contain (1) a statement of management's responsibility for establishing and maintaining adequate internal control over financial reporting (ICFR), and (2) management's assessment of the effectiveness of the ICFR. § 404(b) requires registered public accounting firm to attest (smaller reporting companies and emerging growth companies exempt).

**Codified at:** 15 U.S.C. § 7262.

**Practical use:** ICFR framework typically follows COSO 2013. Material weaknesses must be disclosed.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 802 — Criminal Penalties for Altering Documents

**Plain-English summary:** Whoever knowingly alters, destroys, mutilates, conceals, covers up, falsifies, or makes a false entry in any record, document, or tangible object with the intent to impede, obstruct, or influence the investigation or proper administration of any matter within the jurisdiction of any department or agency of the United States, or in relation to or contemplation of any such matter, shall be fined and/or imprisoned not more than 20 years.

**Codified at:** 18 U.S.C. § 1519.

**Practical use:** Broad anti-shredding provision; the *Yates v. United States*, 574 U.S. 528 (2015), Court limited "tangible object" to objects used to record or preserve information.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 806 — Whistleblower Protection (18 U.S.C. § 1514A)

**Plain-English summary:** No publicly traded company, or any officer, employee, contractor, or agent of such company, may discharge, demote, suspend, threaten, harass, or in any other manner discriminate against an employee in the terms and conditions of employment because of any lawful act done by the employee to provide information regarding any conduct which the employee reasonably believes constitutes a violation of federal securities laws, SEC rules, or any provision of federal law relating to fraud against shareholders.

**Codified at:** 18 U.S.C. § 1514A.

**Practical use:** Post-*Murray v. UBS* (2024), plaintiff need only show whistleblowing was a contributing factor. Employer's burden then to show by clear-and-convincing evidence it would have taken the same action absent the protected activity. 180-day OSHA filing window; subsequent de novo district-court action available.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 906 — Criminal Certification (18 U.S.C. § 1350)

**Plain-English summary:** CEO and CFO must certify with each periodic report containing financial statements that the report fully complies with Exchange Act § 13(a) or 15(d) and fairly presents in all material respects the financial condition. Knowing violation: up to $1M and 10 years' imprisonment; willful: $5M and 20 years.

**Codified at:** 18 U.S.C. § 1350.

**Practical use:** Filed as exhibit 32.1/32.2 to every 10-K and 10-Q. Strict-liability for certification accuracy.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every 10-K/10-Q includes both § 302 and § 906 certifications.
- Every whistleblower complaint pleads contributing-factor causation post-*Murray*.
- Every restatement triggers § 304 clawback analysis plus Dodd-Frank § 954 review.
