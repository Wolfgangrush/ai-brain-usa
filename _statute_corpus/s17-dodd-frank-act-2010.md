- **Sections covered:** Title I (Financial Stability Oversight Council; SIFI designation), Title II (Orderly Liquidation Authority), Title VII (OTC derivatives — Commodity Exchange Act amendments), Title IX (investor protection — including § 922 whistleblower), Title X (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau), Title XIV (mortgage reform).

## Section-by-Section Digest

### § 113 — FSOC Designation of Nonbank Financial Companies

**Plain-English summary:** The Financial Stability Oversight Council may determine that a U.S. nonbank financial company shall be supervised by the Federal Reserve and subject to enhanced prudential standards if the company's material financial distress, or the nature, scope, size, scale, concentration, interconnectedness, or mix of the activities, could pose a threat to U.S. financial stability.

**Codified at:** 12 U.S.C. § 5323.

**Practical use:** FSOC SIFI-designation analysis. *MetLife, Inc. v. FSOC*, 177 F. Supp. 3d 219 (D.D.C. 2016), rescinded MetLife's designation. November 2023 FSOC guidance restored an activities-based-plus-entity-based framework.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 619 — "Volcker Rule" (12 U.S.C. § 1851)

**Plain-English summary:** A banking entity shall not engage in proprietary trading or acquire or retain any equity, partnership, or other ownership interest in or sponsor a hedge fund or private equity fund (covered fund), subject to enumerated exemptions and permitted activities.

**Codified at:** 12 U.S.C. § 1851.

**Practical use:** 2020 final amendments (Reg VV) substantially relaxed covered-fund restrictions and clarified "trading account" definitions. Compliance program required for banking entities above $20B in trading assets and liabilities.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 922 — SEC Whistleblower Program (15 U.S.C. § 78u-6)

**Plain-English summary:** The SEC shall pay an award to whistleblowers who voluntarily provide original information leading to a successful enforcement action resulting in monetary sanctions exceeding $1 million. Award amount: 10–30% of collected sanctions.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> In any covered judicial or administrative action, or related action, the Commission, under regulations prescribed by the Commission and subject to subsection (c), shall pay an award or awards to 1 or more whistleblowers who voluntarily provided original information to the Commission that led to the successful enforcement of the covered judicial or administrative action…

**Citation format:** 15 U.S.C. § 78u-6.

**Practical use:** Anti-retaliation provision at § 78u-6(h). *Digital Realty Trust, Inc. v. Somers*, 583 U.S. 149 (2018), held § 78u-6(h) anti-retaliation protection applies only to whistleblowers who report to the SEC (internal-only complaints rely on SOX § 1514A instead).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 954 — Clawback of Erroneously Awarded Compensation (15 U.S.C. § 78j-4)

**Plain-English summary:** Each exchange must require a listed issuer to develop and implement a clawback policy providing for recovery from any current or former executive officer of incentive-based compensation (including stock options) received during the three-year period preceding the date on which the issuer is required to prepare an accounting restatement, in excess of what would have been paid based on the restated amounts.

**Codified at:** 15 U.S.C. § 78j-4.

**Practical use:** SEC final rule released 26 October 2022; exchange listing standards effective 27 November 2023. Broader than SOX § 304 — applies to all current and former officers (not just CEO/CFO), and does not require misconduct.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 1011 — CFPB Establishment (Title X)

**Plain-English summary:** The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau is established as an independent bureau within the Federal Reserve System, with rulemaking, supervision, and enforcement authority over a broad range of consumer financial products and services.

**Codified at:** 12 U.S.C. § 5491.

**Practical use:** Authority extends to UDAAP (unfair, deceptive, abusive acts or practices) under § 1031, codified at 12 U.S.C. § 5531. *CFPB v. CFSA*, 601 U.S. 416 (2024), settled the funding question; agency remains active in supervisory and enforcement work in mortgage, payments, credit card, and consumer lending.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### Title VII — OTC Derivatives Reforms

**Plain-English summary:** Title VII divides OTC derivatives regulatory authority between the CFTC (swaps) and SEC (security-based swaps), requires central clearing for standardized swaps, exchange or SEF trading, and Swap Data Repository reporting.

**Codified across:** Commodity Exchange Act amendments (7 U.S.C.) and Exchange Act amendments (15 U.S.C.).

**Practical use:** Margin requirements for uncleared swaps phased in 2016–2022. Cross-border substituted compliance regime.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every clawback policy must satisfy both SOX § 304 and Dodd-Frank § 954 standards.
- Every whistleblower complaint must distinguish § 78u-6 (SEC reporter) from § 1514A (SOX — internal also covered).
- Every consumer-finance regulatory engagement begins with UDAAP analysis under 12 U.S.C. § 5531.
