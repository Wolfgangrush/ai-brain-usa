## Section-by-Section Digest

### Title VII § 2000e-2(a) — Unlawful Employment Practices

**Plain-English summary:** It shall be unlawful for an employer (1) to fail or refuse to hire or to discharge any individual, or otherwise to discriminate against any individual with respect to compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment, because of race, color, religion, sex, or national origin; or (2) to limit, segregate, or classify employees in any way that would deprive or tend to deprive any individual of employment opportunities because of those protected characteristics.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> It shall be an unlawful employment practice for an employer — (1) to fail or refuse to hire or to discharge any individual, or otherwise to discriminate against any individual with respect to his compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment, because of such individual's race, color, religion, sex, or national origin; or (2) to limit, segregate, or classify his employees or applicants for employment in any way which would deprive or tend to deprive any individual of employment opportunities or otherwise adversely affect his status as an employee, because of such individual's race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.

**Citation format:** 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-2(a).

**Practical use:** *McDonnell Douglas Corp. v. Green*, 411 U.S. 792 (1973), burden-shifting framework for disparate-treatment claims; *Griggs v. Duke Power Co.*, 401 U.S. 424 (1971), disparate-impact framework codified at § 2000e-2(k). *Bostock* extends "sex" to sexual orientation and gender identity.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

### Title VII § 2000e-3(a) — Retaliation

**Plain-English summary:** It shall be unlawful for an employer to discriminate against any individual because he has opposed any practice made an unlawful employment practice by Title VII, or because he has made a charge, testified, assisted, or participated in any manner in an investigation, proceeding, or hearing under Title VII.

**Citation format:** 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-3(a).

**Practical use:** *Burlington N. & Santa Fe Ry. v. White*, 548 U.S. 53 (2006), "materially adverse" standard (objective). *Univ. of Tex. Sw. Med. Ctr. v. Nassar*, 570 U.S. 338 (2013), but-for causation.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### ADA § 12112 — Discrimination Prohibited

**Plain-English summary:** No covered entity shall discriminate against a qualified individual on the basis of disability in regard to job application procedures, the hiring, advancement, or discharge of employees, employee compensation, job training, and other terms, conditions, and privileges of employment. "Discriminate" includes not making reasonable accommodations to the known physical or mental limitations of an otherwise qualified individual with a disability, unless the entity can demonstrate that the accommodation would impose an undue hardship.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> No covered entity shall discriminate against a qualified individual on the basis of disability in regard to job application procedures, the hiring, advancement, or discharge of employees, employee compensation, job training, and other terms, conditions, and privileges of employment.

**Citation format:** 42 U.S.C. § 12112(a).

**Practical use:** Post-ADAAA, "disability" liberally construed (§ 12102(4)(A)). *US Airways, Inc. v. Barnett*, 535 U.S. 391 (2002), reasonable-accommodation framework. Interactive-process obligations through EEOC enforcement guidance.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### ADEA § 623(a) — Age Discrimination

**Plain-English summary:** It shall be unlawful for an employer to fail or refuse to hire or to discharge any individual or otherwise discriminate with respect to compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment, because of such individual's age (40 or older).

**Citation format:** 29 U.S.C. § 623(a).

**Practical use:** *Gross v. FBL Fin. Servs.*, 557 U.S. 167 (2009), but-for causation in ADEA cases — no mixed-motive analysis. *Babb v. Wilkie*, 589 U.S. 399 (2020), federal-sector ADEA does not require but-for causation.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### FMLA § 2612 — Entitlement to Leave

**Plain-English summary:** An eligible employee is entitled to a total of 12 workweeks of leave during any 12-month period for: (A) birth and care of newborn; (B) placement of child for adoption or foster care; (C) care for spouse/child/parent with serious health condition; (D) employee's own serious health condition; (E) any qualifying exigency arising from family member's covered active duty. Military caregiver leave: 26 workweeks.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> Subject to section 2613 of this title, an eligible employee shall be entitled to a total of 12 workweeks of leave during any 12-month period for one or more of the following: (A) Because of the birth of a son or daughter of the employee and in order to care for such son or daughter. (B) Because of the placement of a son or daughter with the employee for adoption or foster care. (C) In order to care for the spouse, or a son, daughter, or parent, of the employee, if such spouse, son, daughter, or parent has a serious health condition. (D) Because of a serious health condition that makes the employee unable to perform the functions of the position of such employee. (E) Because of any qualifying exigency…

**Citation format:** 29 U.S.C. § 2612(a)(1).

**Practical use:** Eligibility requires 12 months of service + 1,250 hours in prior 12 months + employer with 50+ employees within 75 miles. Restoration rights under § 2614.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### PWFA — Reasonable Accommodation for Pregnancy

**Plain-English summary:** Effective 27 June 2023, covered entities must provide reasonable accommodations to known limitations related to the pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions of a qualified employee, unless the accommodation would impose an undue hardship.

**Citation format:** 42 U.S.C. § 2000gg-1.

**Practical use:** EEOC final regulations effective 18 June 2024. Distinct from ADA in that PWFA reaches conditions that would not meet ADA disability threshold.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every Title VII complaint maps to *McDonnell Douglas* prima-facie elements or direct-evidence allegations; PSLRA-style particularity is not required.
- Every ADA accommodation request triggers documented interactive process.
- Every FMLA decision documents eligibility, qualifying-event, and intermittent-leave certification.
- Every adverse-action analysis post-*Muldrow* applies "some harm" baseline.
