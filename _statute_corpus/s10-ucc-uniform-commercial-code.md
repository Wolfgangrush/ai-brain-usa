# Uniform Commercial Code (UCC) — Articles 1, 2, 9

## Citation Block
- **Source:** Uniform Commercial Code, drafted by the Uniform Law Commission (ULC) and the American Law Institute (ALI).
- **Adoption:** Adopted in some form by all 50 States, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. Louisiana has not adopted Articles 2 and 2A.
- **Official URL:** [https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/ucc](https://www.uniformlaws.org/acts/ucc) and the LII project text: [https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc](https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc).
- **Bluebook Citation:** *U.C.C. § [section]* (e.g., U.C.C. § 2-207) — but always cite the enacting state's commercial code in practice (e.g., *N.Y. U.C.C. § 2-207*, *Cal. Com. Code § 2207*, *Del. Code tit. 6, § 2-207*).

## Authority Tier
Tier 1 — Commercial Backbone (state-enacted; varies by jurisdiction).

## Currency Block
- **Article 1 (General Provisions):** Revised 2001; widely enacted.
- **Article 2 (Sales):** 1951 / 1962 official text; the 2003 amendments to Article 2 were never enacted by any state and have been withdrawn.
- **Article 9 (Secured Transactions):** Revised 1998 (effective 2001 in most states); 2010 amendments effective 2013 across most states.
- **Article 12 (Controllable Electronic Records):** 2022 amendments add CER framework for digital assets — enacted in a growing minority of states as of 2026.

## Language
English (Authoritative).

## Scope of Digest
- **Articles covered:** Article 1 (General Provisions and Definitions), Article 2 (Sales of Goods), Article 9 (Secured Transactions).
- **Articles deferred:** Article 2A (leases), Article 3 (negotiable instruments), Article 4/4A (bank deposits, funds transfers), Article 5 (letters of credit), Article 7 (documents of title), Article 8 (investment securities), Article 12 (CER).

## Section-by-Section Digest

### § 1-201(b)(20) — "Good Faith"

**Plain-English summary:** "Good faith" means honesty in fact and the observance of reasonable commercial standards of fair dealing.

**Statutory text (verbatim):**
> "Good faith," except as otherwise provided in Article 5, means honesty in fact and the observance of reasonable commercial standards of fair dealing.

**Citation format:** U.C.C. § 1-201(b)(20).

**Practical use:** Implied in every contract under § 1-304. Cited in cure and bad-faith arguments in sales disputes.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 2-207 — Additional Terms in Acceptance or Confirmation ("Battle of the Forms")

**Plain-English summary:** A definite expression of acceptance operates as an acceptance even though it states additional or different terms. Between merchants, additional terms become part of the contract unless (a) the offer expressly limits acceptance to its terms, (b) they materially alter it, or (c) notification of objection is given within a reasonable time.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> (1) A definite and seasonable expression of acceptance or a written confirmation which is sent within a reasonable time operates as an acceptance even though it states terms additional to or different from those offered or agreed upon, unless acceptance is expressly made conditional on assent to the additional or different terms.
> (2) The additional terms are to be construed as proposals for addition to the contract. Between merchants such terms become part of the contract unless: (a) the offer expressly limits acceptance to the terms of the offer; (b) they materially alter it; or (c) notification of objection to them has already been given or is given within a reasonable time after notice of them is received.

**Citation format:** U.C.C. § 2-207.

**Practical use:** Anchor of every battle-of-the-forms analysis in commercial drafting (purchase orders vs. acknowledgements).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 2-314 — Implied Warranty of Merchantability

**Plain-English summary:** Unless excluded or modified, a warranty that goods are merchantable is implied in a contract for their sale if the seller is a merchant with respect to goods of that kind. Goods must, at minimum, pass without objection in the trade, be of fair average quality, fit for ordinary purposes, run of even kind and quality, be adequately packaged and labeled, and conform to label promises.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> (1) Unless excluded or modified (Section 2-316), a warranty that the goods shall be merchantable is implied in a contract for their sale if the seller is a merchant with respect to goods of that kind…
> (2) Goods to be merchantable must be at least such as — (a) pass without objection in the trade under the contract description; and (b) in the case of fungible goods, are of fair average quality within the description; and (c) are fit for the ordinary purposes for which such goods are used; and (d) run, within the variations permitted by the agreement, of even kind, quality and quantity within each unit and among all units involved; and (e) are adequately contained, packaged, and labeled as the agreement may require; and (f) conform to the promises or affirmations of fact made on the container or label if any.

**Citation format:** U.C.C. § 2-314.

**Practical use:** Foundation of product-defect claims. Disclaimer must comply with § 2-316 (conspicuous; "merchantability" must be mentioned).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 2-615 — Excuse by Failure of Presupposed Conditions (Commercial Impracticability)

**Plain-English summary:** Delay in delivery or non-delivery is not a breach if performance has been made impracticable by the occurrence of a contingency the non-occurrence of which was a basic assumption of the contract, or by compliance in good faith with applicable foreign or domestic governmental regulation or order.

**Citation format:** U.C.C. § 2-615.

**Practical use:** Force-majeure cousin; raised in supply-chain and pandemic litigation. Allocation to customers required under § 2-615(b).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 9-203 — Attachment of a Security Interest

**Plain-English summary:** A security interest attaches when (1) value has been given, (2) the debtor has rights in the collateral, and (3) the debtor has authenticated a security agreement that provides a description of the collateral (or the collateral is in the secured party's possession or control).

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> (b) Except as otherwise provided in subsections (c) through (i), a security interest is enforceable against the debtor and third parties with respect to the collateral only if: (1) value has been given; (2) the debtor has rights in the collateral or the power to transfer rights in the collateral to a secured party; and (3) one of the following conditions is met: (A) the debtor has authenticated a security agreement that provides a description of the collateral…

**Citation format:** U.C.C. § 9-203(b).

**Practical use:** Foundation of every secured-lending due-diligence exercise. Pair with § 9-310 (perfection by filing) and § 9-322 (priority among conflicting security interests).

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

---

### § 9-322 — Priorities Among Conflicting Security Interests in and Agricultural Liens on Same Collateral

**Plain-English summary:** First-to-file-or-perfect rule: conflicting perfected security interests rank by priority in time of filing or perfection. Unperfected interests lose to perfected ones.

**Statutory text (verbatim, partial):**
> (a) Except as otherwise provided in this section, priority among conflicting security interests and agricultural liens in the same collateral is determined according to the following rules: (1) Conflicting perfected security interests and agricultural liens rank according to priority in time of filing or perfection…

**Citation format:** U.C.C. § 9-322(a).

**Practical use:** Anchor of priority memoranda in commercial finance transactions.

**STATUS:** VERIFIED.

## Drafting Hooks
- Every sale-of-goods contract addresses warranties under § 2-314 / § 2-315 and disclaimers under § 2-316.
- Every commercial battle of the forms is analysed under § 2-207.
- Every security agreement satisfies § 9-203 attachment elements and § 9-310 perfection requirements.
