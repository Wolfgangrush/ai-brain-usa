# Preliminary Injunction Brief — Skeleton (*Winter* Factors)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 65](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_65)
- *Winter v. NRDC*, 555 U.S. 7 (2008).

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Skeleton — drafter's outline.

## Currency Warning
Sliding-scale variants surviving in some circuits (Ninth Circuit's "serious questions" formulation in *Alliance for the Wild Rockies v. Cottrell*, 632 F.3d 1127 (9th Cir. 2011)) — but *Winter*'s "likely to succeed" standard governs.

## Template Type
Brief Skeleton.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction** — preview of relief and urgency.
2. **Statement of Facts** — supported by declarations + verified complaint.
3. **Legal Standard** — Rule 65 + *Winter* four factors:
   - Likelihood of success on merits.
   - Irreparable harm absent relief.
   - Balance of equities tipping in movant's favor.
   - Public interest favoring relief.
4. **Argument**:
   - **A. Movant Is Likely to Succeed on the Merits.**
     - Element-by-element claim analysis.
     - Cite controlling precedent.
   - **B. Movant Will Suffer Irreparable Harm Absent Relief.**
     - Distinguish from monetary harm (which is generally not irreparable).
     - Loss of goodwill, trade secrets, privacy, constitutional rights — categorically irreparable.
     - Specific facts (declarations).
   - **C. The Balance of Equities Tips in Movant's Favor.**
     - Relative hardships.
     - Status quo preservation.
   - **D. The Public Interest Favors Relief.**
     - For government defendants, public interest analysis primary.
     - Regulatory framework / statutory purpose.
5. **Conclusion.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Verified complaint or supporting declarations are critical for factual allegations.
- For First Amendment cases, loss of First Amendment freedoms = per se irreparable injury (*Elrod v. Burns*).
- For trademark/IP cases (post-Trademark Modernization Act 2020), 15 U.S.C. § 1116(a) statutory presumption.
- Bond under Rule 65(c) — propose modest amount; argue for nominal where harm to opposing party speculative.
- Pair with proposed order satisfying Rule 65(d) specificity requirements.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A plaintiff seeking a preliminary injunction must establish that he is likely to succeed on the merits, that he is likely to suffer irreparable harm in the absence of preliminary relief, that the balance of equities tips in his favor, and that an injunction is in the public interest." — *Winter v. NRDC*, 555 U.S. 7, 20 (2008).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
High-frequency emergency-motion brief. Pair with TRO motion under separate brief if ex parte relief sought.
