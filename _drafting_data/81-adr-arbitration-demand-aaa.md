# Arbitration Demand (AAA / JAMS)

## Source URLs

- [JAMS Comprehensive Arbitration Rules](https://www.jamsadr.com/rules-comprehensive-arbitration)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Federal Arbitration Act + selected arbitration-provider rules.

## Currency Warning
AAA Consumer Due Process Protocol and Employment Due Process Protocol layer on top of commercial rules where applicable. EFAA 2022 (sexual harassment / assault) — confirm at intake that claimant has not invoked carve-out election (9 U.S.C. § 402).

## Template Type
Initiating Demand for Arbitration.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — "AMERICAN ARBITRATION ASSOCIATION [or JAMS] DEMAND FOR ARBITRATION."
2. **Parties** — Claimant + Respondent; addresses.
3. **Arbitration Provision** — quote contractual arbitration clause; identify provider; rules invoked; seat.
4. **Nature of Dispute** — brief statement of claims.
5. **Claims** — by count, identifying contract/statute violated and remedy sought.
6. **Amount in Controversy** — for fee calculation.
7. **Relief Sought** — monetary; declaratory; injunctive.
8. **Hearing Locale** — preference; seat per contract typically controls.
9. **Arbitrator Selection** — number; method (per provider's rules); qualifications.
10. **Counsel** — name; firm; contact.
11. **Filing Fee** — paid to provider per fee schedule.
12. **Service** — on Respondent; on provider.

## Drafting Conventions
- Match the procedural rules invoked in the arbitration clause (Commercial / Consumer / Employment / Construction / International).
- Pre-arbitration steps in clause (negotiation, mediation prerequisite) — confirm compliance before filing.
- For class-action waivers, address enforceability under controlling state law (*Concepcion*).
- For EFAA / sexual-harassment claims, confirm claimant has not elected to opt out under 9 U.S.C. § 402.
- For international arbitration, ICDR (AAA's international arm) rules + New York Convention enforcement framework.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A written provision in any maritime transaction or a contract evidencing a transaction involving commerce to settle by arbitration a controversy thereafter arising out of such contract or transaction… shall be valid, irrevocable, and enforceable, save upon such grounds as exist at law or in equity for the revocation of any contract." — 9 U.S.C. § 2.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard initiating instrument for commercial arbitration. Coordinate with provider's case management system and arbitrator-selection process.
