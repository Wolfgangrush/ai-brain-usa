# Motion in Limine

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Evid. 103 (rulings on evidence)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/fre/rule_103)
- [Fed. R. Evid. 401 / 403 / 404](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/fre/rule_401)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Federal Rules of Evidence + court's inherent authority to manage trial.

## Currency Warning
Motion in limine is not a substitute for summary judgment. Court may defer ruling until trial context. Failure to renew objection at trial may waive issue on appeal unless the in limine ruling is definitive (*Luce v. United States*, 469 U.S. 38 (1984)).

## Template Type
Pre-Trial Motion + Supporting Memorandum.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — case caption + "MOTION IN LIMINE NO. [N] TO [EXCLUDE / ADMIT / DETERMINE ADMISSIBILITY OF]."
2. **Introduction** — evidence at issue; relief sought.
3. **Factual Background** — what the evidence is; how opposing party intends to use it.
4. **Legal Standard** — applicable Rule(s) of Evidence; balancing under Rule 403 (probative value vs. unfair prejudice / confusion / waste of time).
5. **Argument**:
   - **Rule 401 relevance** — if no, exclude.
   - **Rule 403 balancing** — even if relevant, probative value substantially outweighed by danger of unfair prejudice / confusing the issues / misleading the jury / undue delay / wasting time / needlessly presenting cumulative evidence.
   - **Rule 404(b) prior acts** — character vs. permissible non-character (motive, opportunity, intent, preparation, plan, knowledge, identity, absence of mistake, lack of accident) + notice requirement in criminal cases.
   - **Rule 702 expert testimony** — Daubert challenge if applicable.
   - **Rules 801–807 hearsay** — exclude hearsay or admit under exception.
   - **Rule 901 / 902 authentication** — exclude unauthenticated evidence.
6. **Specific Relief Requested** — exclude / limit / require pre-admission proffer.
7. **Proposed Order.**
8. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- File well in advance of trial — Local Rules and pretrial orders typically set deadlines (often 14-30 days before trial).
- For each piece of evidence, specify the precise basis for exclusion.
- *Luce v. United States*: in criminal cases, defendant must testify at trial to preserve appellate review of in limine ruling on impeachment under Rule 609; civil analog generally not as strict.
- Bundle related motions in limine into single document or file as separate numbered motions per Local Rule.
- Anticipate Rule 403 balancing in every motion: probative value of the evidence vs. risk of unfair prejudice.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The court may exclude relevant evidence if its probative value is substantially outweighed by a danger of one or more of the following: unfair prejudice, confusing the issues, misleading the jury, undue delay, wasting time, or needlessly presenting cumulative evidence." — Fed. R. Evid. 403.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Routine trial-prep filing. Build a numbered set of motions in limine targeting each anticipated evidence-admissibility dispute. Coordinate with opening statement and witness order.
