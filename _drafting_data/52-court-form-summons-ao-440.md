# Summons (Form AO 440)

## Authority Tier
Primary — Federal Rules of Civil Procedure Rule 4 + Administrative Office forms.

## Currency Warning
Rule 4(m) — service of summons and complaint must occur within 90 days after filing of the complaint or court must dismiss without prejudice (extension permitted for good cause). Defendant who waives service under Rule 4(d) has 60 days (90 if defendant outside U.S.) to answer instead of 21 days.

## Template Type
Court Form (issued by Clerk).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — court · case number · plaintiff · defendant.
2. **To: [Defendant Name and Address]** — service target.
3. **Notice of Action** — "A lawsuit has been filed against you."
4. **Time to Answer** — 21 days after service (60 if waived under Rule 4(d); 60 if U.S. government as defendant under Rule 12(a)(2)).
5. **Consequence of Default** — judgment by default for relief demanded in complaint.
6. **Signature** — Clerk of Court.
7. **Proof of Service** — separate form (AO 440 reverse) completed by process server.
8. **Service Methods** — personal · substituted service (place of dwelling, age 18+ resident) · agent · waiver of service (Rule 4(d)).

## Drafting Conventions
- For corporations: serve registered agent (Rule 4(h)(1)) or any officer / managing or general agent / agent authorized to receive process under state law.
- For LLCs: serve as state law of formation directs (often manager or authorized member).
- For U.S. government defendants: Rule 4(i) — special service rules (deliver to U.S. Attorney, mail to Attorney General).
- For foreign defendants: Rule 4(f) — Hague Convention or other international service mechanism.
- Waiver of service (Rule 4(d)) avoids personal service; failure to waive without good cause exposes defendant to service expenses.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "If a defendant is not served within 90 days after the complaint is filed, the court — on motion or on its own after notice to the plaintiff — must dismiss the action without prejudice against that defendant or order that service be made within a specified time. But if the plaintiff shows good cause for the failure, the court must extend the time for service for an appropriate period." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 4(m).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Required first-instance court issuance. Coordinate with process server immediately on complaint filing.
