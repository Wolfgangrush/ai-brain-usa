## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Bankruptcy Code Chapter 11 + Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure + Official Forms.

## Currency Warning
Subchapter V (added 2019) for small-business debtors — streamlined Chapter 11. Debt-limit threshold periodically adjusted; verify current cap. *Harrington v. Purdue Pharma L.P.*, 603 U.S. ___ (2024) — non-consensual third-party releases not authorized.

## Template Type
Petition + First-Day Motions Package.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Voluntary Petition (Form 201).**
2. **Resolutions** — authorizing chapter 11 filing by board.
3. **List of 20 Largest Unsecured Creditors** + List of Equity Security Holders.
4. **Corporate Ownership Statement** (FRBP 7007.1).
5. **Schedules A-J** + Statement of Financial Affairs (Form 207).
6. **First-Day Motions Package**:
   - Cash management.
   - DIP financing.
   - Wages and benefits.
   - Critical vendors.
   - Customer programs.
   - Insurance.
   - Tax payments.
   - Utility deposits (§ 366).
   - 341(a) meeting timing.
   - Bar date for proofs of claim.
   - Retention of professionals (debtor's counsel, financial advisor, investment banker, claims agent).
7. **Disclosure Statement and Plan** — to be filed within 120 days of petition (or 90 days for Subchapter V); exclusivity period (§ 1121).
8. **Section 341(a) Meeting of Creditors** — set by U.S. Trustee within 21-40 days of petition.

## Drafting Conventions
- File First-Day Motions concurrently with petition for emergency relief.
- DIP financing: pre-negotiated; lender concessions documented.
- Subchapter V eligibility check — if debt under threshold, simplified path.
- Plan confirmation under § 1129 — 16 elements; cramdown under § 1129(b) requires fair-and-equitable treatment + absolute priority rule.
- Disclosure statement under § 1125 — adequate information for reasonable hypothetical investor.
- Post-*Purdue Pharma*, third-party releases require consensual structure.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Only the debtor may file a plan until after 120 days after the date of the order for relief under this chapter." — 11 U.S.C. § 1121(b).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Complex restructuring practice. Subchapter V provides simpler path for small businesses; engage specialized counsel for traditional Chapter 11.
