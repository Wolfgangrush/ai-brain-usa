# EEOC Charge of Discrimination

## Source URLs
- [42 U.S.C. § 2000e-5 (Title VII enforcement)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/2000e-5)
- [EEOC Filing Page](https://www.eeoc.gov/filing-charge-discrimination)
- [EEOC Public Portal](https://publicportal.eeoc.gov)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Title VII / ADA / ADEA / GINA / EPA + state-FEP work-sharing arrangements.

## Currency Warning
180-day filing deadline from alleged discriminatory act; extended to 300 days where state or local FEP agency has work-sharing arrangement with EEOC. Right-to-sue letter required before Title VII / ADA / GINA / ADEA federal court action; 90 days from receipt of right-to-sue letter to file complaint.

## Template Type
Administrative Charge.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Charging Party Information** — name; address; phone; email.
2. **Respondent Information** — employer name; address; number of employees; whether federal contractor.
3. **Basis of Discrimination** — race · color · religion · sex (incl. sexual orientation and gender identity per *Bostock*) · national origin · age · disability · genetic information · pregnancy · retaliation.
4. **Date(s) of Discrimination** — earliest to most recent.
5. **Discrimination Statement / "Particulars"**:
   - Identify employer's actions.
   - Identify specific individuals (decision-makers and witnesses).
   - Identify comparator employees (similarly situated; different treatment).
   - Identify damages (lost wages, demoted, terminated, denied promotion, harassment).
   - Identify protected activity (if retaliation claim).
6. **Continuing-Violation Allegations** (if applicable) for hostile-work-environment.
7. **Signature + Verification** under penalty of perjury.
8. **Filing Receipt** — EEOC will provide Charge Number.

## Drafting Conventions
- Write in plain language; the charge is a non-technical administrative pleading.
- Detail dates, decision-makers, comparators; vagueness invites dismissal.
- Include retaliation as separate basis if applicable; retaliation often outlasts underlying-conduct case.
- Continuing-violation doctrine — *Nat'l R.R. Passenger Corp. v. Morgan*, 536 U.S. 101 (2002) — discrete-act claims must be filed within 180/300 days of each act; hostile-environment claims may include earlier acts if part of same actionable practice.
- Right-to-sue letter request: may request after 180 days of charge filing even before EEOC concludes investigation.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A charge under this section shall be filed within one hundred and eighty days after the alleged unlawful employment practice occurred… except that in a case of an unlawful employment practice with respect to which the person aggrieved has initially instituted proceedings with a State or local agency with authority to grant or seek relief from such practice…, such charge shall be filed by or on behalf of the person aggrieved within three hundred days after the alleged unlawful employment practice occurred…" — 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-5(e)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foundation of employment-discrimination practice. File charge with EEOC and concurrently with state FEP agency where work-share applies.
