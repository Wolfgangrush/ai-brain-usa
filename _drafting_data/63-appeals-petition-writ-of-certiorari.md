# Petition for a Writ of Certiorari (SCOTUS Rule 14)

- [28 U.S.C. § 1254](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1254) (certiorari from courts of appeals)
- [28 U.S.C. § 1257](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1257) (certiorari from state courts of last resort)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Supreme Court Rules + 28 U.S.C. §§ 1254, 1257.

## Currency Warning
Filing deadline: 90 days after entry of judgment of court of appeals or denial of timely rehearing petition (Sup. Ct. R. 13.1). Extension up to 60 days under Rule 13.5. Word limit: 9,000 words (Rule 33.1(g)(ii)). Booklet format required unless IFP.

## Template Type
Appellate / Apex-Court Petition.

## Structural Skeleton (Sup. Ct. R. 14.1)
1. **Questions Presented** — first page; concise; one or two; not argumentative.
2. **Parties to the Proceeding** — list each party in the court below.
3. **Corporate Disclosure Statement** — Sup. Ct. R. 29.6.
4. **Related Cases** statement.
5. **Table of Contents.**
6. **Table of Authorities.**
7. **Opinions Below** — citation to court of appeals opinion (e.g., "Pet. App. 1a") + court of appeals citation + lower court(s).
8. **Jurisdiction** — date of court of appeals judgment; date of rehearing denial (if any); 28 U.S.C. § 1254(1) basis.
9. **Constitutional and Statutory Provisions Involved.**
10. **Statement of the Case** — concise; record-cited; non-argumentative.
11. **Reasons for Granting the Petition** — map to Sup. Ct. R. 10:
    - **Circuit split** (Rule 10(a)) — identify split, name circuit cases, show the split is genuine and entrenched.
    - **Conflict with state court of last resort** (Rule 10(b)).
    - **Exceptional importance** (Rule 10(c)) — federal question that should be settled; reach analysis (number of affected parties, recurrence of issue).
    - **Conflict with Supreme Court precedent** (Rule 10(c)).
12. **Conclusion** — "The petition should be granted."
13. **Counsel Information** — Counsel of Record + Bar admission to SCOTUS + address + phone + email.
14. **Appendix** — court of appeals opinion · district court opinion · order denying rehearing · relevant constitutional/statutory provisions.

## Drafting Conventions
- Question Presented framing is the single most important draft decision — neutral framing, not argumentative.
- Reasons for Granting must map directly to Rule 10 factors; "vehicle defects" (factual idiosyncrasies, mootness, alternative grounds) are the most-cited denial reasons.
- For state-court petitions, address adequate-and-independent-state-grounds carefully.
- Pair with companion application for stay if needed (Rule 22).
- Booklet format (Rule 33.1) — 6⅛ × 9¼ inches, off-white paper, cover colors per Rule (cert petition cover: white).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Review on a writ of certiorari is not a matter of right, but of judicial discretion. A petition for a writ of certiorari will be granted only for compelling reasons." — Sup. Ct. R. 10.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Apex appellate vehicle. Specialist practice; many petitions filed by Supreme Court bar specialists. Pair early with Bar admission process if not already a member of the SCOTUS bar.
