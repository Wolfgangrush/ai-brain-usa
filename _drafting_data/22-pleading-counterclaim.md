# Counterclaim (Rule 13)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 13](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_13)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 13.

## Currency Warning
Compulsory counterclaims under Rule 13(a) — arising out of the same transaction or occurrence — are forever barred if not raised. Permissive counterclaims under Rule 13(b) require independent jurisdictional basis (supplemental jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1367 may suffice).

## Template Type
Pleading — Defendant's Counterclaim (filed within Answer or as standalone).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — match case; "ANSWER, AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES, AND COUNTERCLAIM" (combined) OR standalone "COUNTERCLAIM."
2. **Parties to the Counterclaim** — Counterclaim-Plaintiff (defendant) v. Counterclaim-Defendant (plaintiff); add new parties under Rule 13(h) and Rules 19/20.
3. **Jurisdiction and Venue** — restate basis for original jurisdiction; for permissive counterclaims, demonstrate § 1367 supplemental jurisdiction or independent basis.
4. **Factual Allegations** — numbered (continue numbering or restart).
5. **Counts** — separate count per cause of action.
6. **Prayer for Relief** — independent prayer for damages and other relief.
7. **Jury Demand** (if applicable) — separate demand for counterclaim claims.
8. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- File counterclaim with the Answer or as soon after as practicable; do not delay (waiver risk for compulsory).
- For compulsory counterclaims (arising out of same transaction/occurrence): same-transaction test from *Moore v. New York Cotton Exchange*, 270 U.S. 593 (1926), and modern formulations.
- Joining new counterclaim-defendants under Rule 13(h) requires Rule 19 (required parties) or Rule 20 (permissive joinder) compliance.
- Service: counterclaim served via Rule 5 (later pleading) for parties already in case; Rule 4 (summons) for new counterclaim-defendants.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A pleading must state as a counterclaim any claim that — at the time of its service — the pleader has against an opposing party if the claim: (A) arises out of the transaction or occurrence that is the subject matter of the opposing party's claim; and (B) does not require adding another party over whom the court cannot acquire jurisdiction." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 13(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard companion to Answer where defendant has independent claims. Always conduct Rule 13(a) compulsory analysis early — failure to plead is forever waiver.
