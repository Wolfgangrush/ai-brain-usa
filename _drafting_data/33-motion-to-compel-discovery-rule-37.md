# Motion to Compel Discovery (Rule 37)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 37](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_37)
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 26](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_26)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rules 26, 33, 34, 36, 37.

## Currency Warning
Meet-and-confer prerequisite (Rule 37(a)(1)) must be documented with detail. Many districts have additional "letter motion" requirement, page limits on discovery motions, and specific local rule procedures for discovery disputes. Failure to comply with Rule 37(e) (ESI loss) requires intent-to-deprive finding for severe sanctions (adverse inference, dismissal, default).

## Template Type
Motion + Memorandum + Joint Statement (where required) + Exhibits.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Notice of Motion** — relief sought; hearing date per Local Rule.
2. **Certification of Meet-and-Confer Efforts** — pursuant to Rule 37(a)(1); detail dates, communications, attempts to resolve.
3. **Memorandum in Support**:
   a. **Introduction** — discovery sought; reason opposing party's response is deficient.
   b. **Procedural History** — date discovery served; date response served; objections asserted.
   c. **Legal Standard** — Rule 26(b)(1) scope (relevant + proportional); Rules 33/34/36 specific obligations.
   d. **Argument** — request-by-request:
      - Relevance and proportionality.
      - Inadequacy of objection (boilerplate · over-broad · privilege not properly logged · "subject to and without waiving objections" responses insufficient).
      - For privilege claims, demand for proper privilege log; in-camera review request if needed.
      - For ESI, address Rule 26(f) ESI protocol and any agreed search terms.
   e. **Relief Requested** — order compelling production / responses; specific deadlines; attorney's fees under Rule 37(a)(5).
4. **Exhibits** — discovery requests; responses; meet-and-confer correspondence.
5. **Proposed Order.**
6. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Rule 26(b)(1) scope: "relevant to any party's claim or defense and proportional to the needs of the case."
- Rule 37(a)(5)(A) fee-shifting: court MUST require party whose conduct necessitated the motion to pay reasonable expenses UNLESS (i) movant did not in good faith try to obtain disclosure without court action, (ii) opposing position substantially justified, or (iii) other circumstances make award unjust.
- Boilerplate objections insufficient — *Heller v. City of Dallas*, 303 F.R.D. 466 (N.D. Tex. 2014).
- Privilege log requirement: Rule 26(b)(5)(A) demands description sufficient to enable other parties to assess.
- ESI sanctions under Rule 37(e): if ESI lost despite reasonable steps to preserve, two paths — (1) prejudice + measures no greater than necessary, or (2) intent to deprive + adverse inference / dismissal / default.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "On notice to other parties and all affected persons, a party may move for an order compelling disclosure or discovery. The motion must include a certification that the movant has in good faith conferred or attempted to confer with the person or party failing to make disclosure or discovery in an effort to obtain it without court action." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 37(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Routine discovery enforcement. Document meet-and-confer thoroughly. Pair with companion request for sanctions under Rule 37(a)(5) and Rule 37(b)(2) on order non-compliance.
