# Petition for Rehearing / Rehearing En Banc (FRAP 35, 40)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. App. P. 35](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frap/rule_35)
- [Fed. R. App. P. 40](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frap/rule_40)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRAP 35 (en banc) + FRAP 40 (panel rehearing).

## Currency Warning
2024 amendments consolidated Rule 35 and Rule 40 — verify circuit Local Rule procedure. En banc rehearing not favored; granted only for: (1) necessary to secure or maintain uniformity of decisions, or (2) proceeding involves a question of exceptional importance.

## Template Type
Appellate Motion.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption + Title** — "PETITION FOR REHEARING [AND/OR REHEARING EN BANC] OF [APPELLEE/APPELLANT]."
2. **Rule 35(b)(1) Statement** (front of petition, conspicuously) — based on counsel's professional judgment, en banc consideration is necessary because either:
   - "The panel decision conflicts with [decision of the Supreme Court / this Court / circuit precedent], and en banc consideration is necessary to secure and maintain uniformity of the Court's decisions"; OR
   - "This proceeding involves a question of exceptional importance: [statement]."
3. **Introduction** — preview of grounds.
4. **Statement of Facts.**
5. **Reasons for Granting Rehearing**:
   - **Panel rehearing under Rule 40** — points of fact or law overlooked or misapprehended.
   - **En banc rehearing under Rule 35** — uniformity / exceptional importance.
6. **Conclusion** — relief sought.
7. **Certificate of Compliance** — Rule 35(b)(2) length limit (3,900 words).
8. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Timing: 45 days from entry of judgment (in civil cases where U.S. is a party) / 14 days otherwise (Rule 40(a)(1)) — verify current rule; 2024 consolidation harmonized timing.
- Length: typically limited to 3,900 words for petition (Rule 35(b)(2)).
- En banc is exceedingly rare — typically requires circuit split or clear conflict with Supreme Court precedent.
- For circuit splits, frame the split with specificity (which courts, which authorities, what differs).
- Rule 40 panel rehearing useful for arithmetic error, clearly overlooked authority, or procedural issue.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "An en banc hearing or rehearing is not favored and ordinarily will not be ordered unless: (1) en banc consideration is necessary to secure or maintain uniformity of the court's decisions; or (2) the proceeding involves a question of exceptional importance." — Fed. R. App. P. 35(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Pre-cert vehicle for high-stakes appeals. En banc denial often surfaces dissent useful in cert petition.
