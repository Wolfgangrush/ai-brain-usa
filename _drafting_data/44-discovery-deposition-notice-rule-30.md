# Deposition Notice (Rule 30) — Including Rule 30(b)(6) Designated Witness

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 30](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_30)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 30.

## Currency Warning
Rule 30(a)(2)(A) limits depositions to 10 per side without leave of court or stipulation. Rule 30(d)(1) limits each deposition to one day of 7 hours unless stipulated or ordered. Rule 30(b)(6) requires meet-and-confer about topics and identity of designees BEFORE deposition.

## Template Type
Discovery Notice + Rule 45 Subpoena (for non-parties).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — "NOTICE OF DEPOSITION OF [WITNESS NAME] / RULE 30(B)(6) DEPOSITION OF [ENTITY]."
2. **Notice Statement** — "Please take notice that Plaintiff will take the deposition upon oral examination of [Witness] before a notary public on [date and time] at [location], or by remote means (Zoom / Teams / specified platform). The deposition will be recorded by stenographic and video means."
3. **Rule 30(b)(6) Topics** (if entity deposition):
   - List "matters for examination" with reasonable particularity.
   - Examples: facts supporting affirmative defense No. X; document retention policies; identity of decision-makers re [event]; data collection practices.
4. **Document Requests Attached** (if combined with Rule 30(b)(2)) — duces tecum.
5. **Court Reporter / Videographer Information.**
6. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- For Rule 30(b)(6), meet and confer about topics before deposition (2020 amendment).
- Combine with Rule 34 document requests if witness has unique documents (Rule 30(b)(2)).
- For non-parties, serve Rule 45 subpoena + witness fee ($40/day + mileage).
- For remote depositions, address technology, exhibit-handling, and confidentiality protocols.
- Apex doctrine — for senior executives, opposing party may seek protective order requiring less-intrusive discovery first.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "In its notice or subpoena, a party may name as the deponent a public or private corporation, a partnership, an association, a governmental agency, or other entity and must describe with reasonable particularity the matters for examination. The named organization must then designate one or more officers, directors, or managing agents, or designate other persons who consent to testify on its behalf…" — Fed. R. Civ. P. 30(b)(6).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Routine deposition instrument. Build a meet-and-confer log for Rule 30(b)(6) topic disputes.
