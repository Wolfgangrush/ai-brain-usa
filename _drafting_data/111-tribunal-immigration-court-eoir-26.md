## Template Type
Notice of Appeal (Form EOIR-26).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — Executive Office for Immigration Review · Board of Immigration Appeals · respondent.
2. **Identification** — A-number; respondent name; IJ name; hearing date(s).
3. **Decision Appealed** — date of IJ decision; outcome (removal order, denial of relief, etc.).
4. **Reason for Appeal** — specific legal and factual errors (initial statement; brief follows).
5. **Statement of Issues** — narrowly framed.
6. **Filing Fee** ($110 — verify; fee waiver Form EOIR-26A).
7. **Counsel Information** + Form EOIR-28 entry of appearance.
8. **Service** on DHS counsel + respondent.

## Brief Skeleton (Filed within Briefing Schedule after Transcript Issuance)
1. **Statement of the Case.**
2. **Statement of Facts.**
3. **Statement of Issues.**
4. **Argument** — each issue:
   - Legal standard (de novo for law / fact-finding by IJ reviewed for clear error).
   - Application to facts.
5. **Conclusion.**

## Drafting Conventions
- File Form EOIR-26 within 30 days of IJ decision — JURISDICTIONAL.
- Identify each ground for appeal at notice stage; failure may waive on review.
- Brief filed after transcript issued; typical 21-day briefing schedule by BIA.
- For motions to reopen / reconsider, separate forms (Motion to Reopen 8 C.F.R. § 1003.23).
- *Pereira v. Sessions*, 138 S. Ct. 2105 (2018), and *Niz-Chavez v. Garland*, 593 U.S. 155 (2021) — notice-to-appear specificity affects stop-time rule and reopening.
- For voluntary departure, 60-day timing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Pursuant to 8 CFR 1003.38, an appeal from a decision of an Immigration Judge in deportation, exclusion, or removal proceedings shall be filed directly with the Board of Immigration Appeals within 30 calendar days after the stating of an Immigration Judge's oral decision or the mailing of an Immigration Judge's written decision." — 8 C.F.R. § 1003.38(b).
