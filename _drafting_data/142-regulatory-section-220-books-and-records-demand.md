# Section 220 Books-and-Records Demand (Delaware)

## Source URLs
- [DGCL § 220](https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/sc07/index.html)
- *Lebanon Cnty. Emps.' Ret. Fund v. AmerisourceBergen Corp.*, 243 A.3d 417 (Del. 2020).

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — DGCL § 220 + Delaware Court of Chancery doctrine.

## Currency Warning
Court of Chancery emphasizes proper purpose + credible basis to suspect wrongdoing (*Cent. Laborers' Pension Fund v. News Corp.*, 45 A.3d 139 (Del. 2012)). 2024 amendments to § 220 affecting permissible categories of records subject to inspection.

## Template Type
Stockholder Demand Letter.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Letterhead and Date.**
2. **Recipient** — Company; care of Corporate Secretary; registered agent or principal office.
3. **Identification of Demanding Stockholder** — name; record-holder status (or beneficial-owner with documentary proof); date of acquisition; current ownership.
4. **Statement of Demand** — "Pursuant to Section 220 of the Delaware General Corporation Law, [Stockholder] hereby demands the right to inspect and copy the following books and records of the Company…"
5. **Proper Purpose Statement** (REQUIRED — § 220(b)):
   - Identify the proper purpose reasonably related to such person's interest as a stockholder.
   - Typical proper purposes: investigation of mismanagement / wrongdoing; valuation of shares; communication with other stockholders; assessment of voting recommendations.
6. **Categories of Documents Demanded** — specific list:
   - Books and records demonstrating the alleged conduct.
   - Stock ledger and stockholder list.
   - Board meeting minutes and resolutions concerning [topic].
   - Committee meeting minutes.
   - Materials provided to the board concerning [topic].
   - Officer-board communications concerning [topic].
7. **Credible Basis** — concrete facts supporting suspicion of wrongdoing (typically required where investigative purpose).
8. **Confidentiality Undertaking** — willingness to enter reasonable confidentiality stipulation.
9. **Verification under Oath** (§ 220(b)(1)) — verified statement.
10. **Demand for Response** — typically 5 business days under DGCL.
11. **Notice of Section 220 Action** — if non-response, intent to file § 220 action.
12. **Signature** by stockholder or sworn-counsel.

## Drafting Conventions
- Verification under oath is jurisdictional.
- Proper purpose: investigation of corporate mismanagement is most common; must demonstrate "credible basis from which the Court of Chancery can infer wrongdoing" (*Seinfeld v. Verizon Commc'ns, Inc.*, 909 A.2d 117 (Del. 2006)).
- Books-and-records demand is the recommended PRE-COMPLAINT step before derivative action (*King v. VeriFone Holdings, Inc.*, 12 A.3d 1140 (Del. 2011)).
- Limit categories to specific topics; broad-and-burdensome demands invite rejection.
- "Necessary and essential" standard governs scope of inspection (*Saito v. McKesson HBOC, Inc.*, 806 A.2d 113 (Del. 2002)).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Any stockholder, in person or by attorney or other agent, shall, upon written demand under oath stating the purpose thereof, have the right during the usual hours for business to inspect for any proper purpose, and to make copies and extracts from: (1) The corporation's stock ledger, a list of its stockholders, and its other books and records…" — 8 Del. C. § 220(b).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Required first step for stockholder-derivative practice. Pair with credible-basis showing and confidentiality stipulation.
