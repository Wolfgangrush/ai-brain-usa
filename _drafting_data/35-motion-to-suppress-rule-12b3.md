## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCrP Rule 12 + Rule 41 + Fourth Amendment doctrine.

## Currency Warning
Fourth Amendment doctrine in flux: *Carpenter v. United States*, 585 U.S. 296 (2018) (CSLI third-party doctrine reframed); *Riley v. California*, 573 U.S. 373 (2014) (cell phone search incident to arrest); *Kentucky v. King*, 563 U.S. 452 (2011) (exigent circumstances doctrine). Good-faith exception (*United States v. Leon*, 468 U.S. 897 (1984)) and inevitable-discovery doctrines remain robust.

## Template Type
Pretrial Motion + Memorandum + Declarations / Affidavits + Proposed Findings.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — criminal case caption + "DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO SUPPRESS EVIDENCE."
2. **Introduction** — what evidence sought to be suppressed; constitutional basis.
3. **Factual Background** — defendant's standing (legitimate expectation of privacy in place/item searched); search/seizure circumstances; defendant supporting affidavit (typically required for evidentiary hearing — *United States v. Cazares-Olivas*, 515 F.3d 726 (7th Cir. 2008)).
4. **Argument**:
   - **Fourth Amendment threshold** — search or seizure; standing.
   - **Warrant analysis** — if warrant issued, attack: probable cause (Gates totality); particularity; staleness; nexus between probable cause and place searched.
   - **Warrantless search** — government bears burden to establish exception: consent; plain view; search incident to lawful arrest; protective sweep; exigent circumstances; automobile exception; Terry stop-and-frisk.
   - **Good-faith exception rebuttal** under *Leon*: warrant facially deficient; affidavit so lacking; magistrate not detached/neutral; affiant misled magistrate.
   - **Fruits of the poisonous tree** (*Wong Sun v. United States*, 371 U.S. 471 (1963)) — exclude derivative evidence.
   - **Statements** — *Miranda v. Arizona*, 384 U.S. 436 (1966) custodial-interrogation analysis; *Edwards v. Arizona*, 451 U.S. 477 (1981) post-counsel-request rule; voluntariness due-process challenge.
5. **Request for Evidentiary Hearing** — citing *Franks v. Delaware*, 438 U.S. 154 (1978), where alleging affidavit falsity.
6. **Proposed Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law.**
7. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Standing burden on defendant: legitimate expectation of privacy.
- Probable cause: totality of circumstances (*Illinois v. Gates*, 462 U.S. 213 (1983)) — fair probability that contraband or evidence will be found.
- Franks hearing requires substantial preliminary showing of false statement made knowingly or with reckless disregard.
- Carpenter trio (Carpenter, Riley, Jones) constrain digital-evidence searches.
- Pair with bail-related motions and discovery motions as part of unified pretrial strategy.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by Oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized." — U.S. Const. amend. IV.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Workhorse pretrial motion in federal criminal practice. Pair with Bivens / § 1983 civil rights theory where the conduct could give rise to parallel civil liability.
