# Security Agreement (UCC Article 9)

## Source URLs
- [UCC Article 9](https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/9)
- [UCC § 9-203 (attachment)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/9/9-203)
- [UCC § 9-310 (perfection by filing)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/9/9-310)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
State UCC Article 9 (enacting state's commercial code).

## Currency Warning
Article 12 (controllable electronic records / digital assets) — 2022 ULC amendments enacted in growing minority of states by 2026. CER perfection rules differ from traditional Article 9 collateral.

## Template Type
Secured Transaction Document.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Recitals + Effective Date.**
2. **Grant of Security Interest** — present grant of security interest in defined Collateral to secure all Obligations.
3. **Collateral Description** — by Article 9 category OR "all assets, whether now owned or hereafter acquired" (super-generic descriptions permitted in security agreement but not in UCC-1 financing statement).
4. **Obligations Secured** — all present and future obligations under [Loan Agreement] + protective advances.
5. **Representations and Warranties of Debtor** — debtor's name (exact match to state-organization record); jurisdiction of organization; chief executive office; locations of collateral; no other liens (other than permitted); good title.
6. **Covenants**:
   - Maintain insurance + name secured party as loss payee.
   - Pay taxes.
   - Permit inspection.
   - Notify of name / jurisdiction changes.
   - Account-debtor notification rights upon default.
7. **Perfection Provisions**:
   - UCC-1 filing authorization.
   - Possession of negotiable collateral / instruments.
   - Control of deposit accounts (Deposit Account Control Agreement); securities accounts (Securities Account Control Agreement); letters-of-credit rights.
   - Patent / Copyright / Trademark filings with USPTO + USCO.
8. **Events of Default + Remedies**:
   - Article 9 Part 6 remedies: take possession; collect proceeds; sell at public or private sale (commercially reasonable); apply proceeds in statutory order.
   - Notification under § 9-611.
   - Deficiency / surplus computations.
9. **Power of Attorney** — irrevocable; authorize secured party to sign UCC-1 amendments, endorse instruments, etc.
10. **Indemnification + Reimbursement + Costs of Enforcement.**
11. **Governing Law and Forum.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Debtor name on UCC-1 must EXACTLY match state organizational record (§ 9-503) — minor variations result in unperfection.
- "All assets" description in UCC-1 permitted (§ 9-504(2)); in security agreement, more particularity recommended.
- For deposit account perfection, control under § 9-104 — Deposit Account Control Agreement (3-party).
- For securities perfection, control under § 9-106 — Securities Account Control Agreement.
- For IP, file at USPTO (patents, trademarks) + USCO (copyrights) for federal-law perfection in addition to UCC-1.
- For investment property, customer's broker must agree to control (§ 9-106).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A security interest is enforceable against the debtor and third parties with respect to the collateral only if: (1) value has been given; (2) the debtor has rights in the collateral or the power to transfer rights in the collateral to a secured party; and (3) one of the following conditions is met: (A) the debtor has authenticated a security agreement that provides a description of the collateral…" — UCC § 9-203(b).
