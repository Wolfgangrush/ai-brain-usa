## Template Type
Compliance Policy / Internal Governance.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Scope** — covered AI systems (high-risk + GPAI + internal-tool).
2. **Governance Structure** — AI Governance Committee · accountable executive · CAIO/CPO · audit + compliance reporting line.
3. **NIST AI RMF Functions** — Govern · Map · Measure · Manage. For each, named controls and frequency.
4. **AI System Inventory** — register every covered system with: purpose, vendor, training data sources, intended user, output users, decision impact, refresh cadence.
5. **Risk Tiering** — Low / Limited / Substantial (analogous to EU AI Act risk classes for cross-jurisdictional consistency).
6. **Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA)** — required for substantial-risk; template form included.
7. **Pre-deployment review** — bias testing methodology + dataset documentation + evaluation set + acceptance criteria.
8. **Post-deployment monitoring** — drift detection · incident reporting · periodic re-evaluation.
9. **Human Oversight** — when and how a human is in / on / over the loop.
10. **Transparency Notices** — user-facing AI-interaction disclosure (Cal. SB 942 · CO AI Act consumer notice).
11. **Vendor Management** — diligence questionnaire + contractual flow-down + audit rights.
12. **Incident Response** — categorization + reporting line + remediation + retention of records.
13. **Training and Awareness** — periodicity + content + acknowledgement records.
14. **State-Specific Overlay** — Colorado consumer-rights and impact-assessment + Texas TRAIGA prohibited-use catalog + Utah disclosure rules.

## Drafting Conventions
- Cross-reference the NIST RMF Trustworthy Characteristics: Valid and Reliable · Safe · Secure and Resilient · Accountable and Transparent · Explainable and Interpretable · Privacy-Enhanced · Fair (Harmful Bias Managed).
- Where the client is also subject to EU AI Act (place market on EU), build a single combined-control set rather than parallel programmes.
- Document training-data sources to meet CA AB 2013 (generative AI training-data disclosure) requirements.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "AI risk management is a key component of responsible development and use of AI systems… This Framework is intended to be voluntary, rights-preserving, non-sector-specific, and use-case agnostic, providing flexibility to organizations of all sizes and in all sectors and throughout society to implement the approaches in the Framework." — NIST AI RMF 1.0 § 1.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Anchor product for AI-counsel engagements. Pair with vendor-due-diligence questionnaire and the firm's algorithmic-impact-assessment template. Most U.S. clients adopt NIST RMF as the framework even where they are EU AI Act subject.
