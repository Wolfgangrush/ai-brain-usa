# Bylaws — Delaware Corporation

## Source URLs
- [DGCL § 109 (bylaws)](https://delcode.delaware.gov/title8/c001/sc04/index.html)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — DGCL § 109.

## Currency Warning
Advance-notice bylaw provisions — *Coster v. UIP Cos.*, 300 A.3d 656 (Del. 2023); *Sears Hometown & Outlet Stores, Inc. v. Lampert*, 309 A.3d 474 (Del. Ch. 2024). Universal proxy (SEC Rule 14a-19) requirements for public-company contested elections.

## Template Type
Corporate Governance Document.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Offices** — registered office + principal office.
2. **Stockholders Meetings**:
   - Annual meeting.
   - Special meetings (who may call).
   - Notice.
   - Quorum.
   - Voting.
   - Action by written consent (if permitted by certificate).
   - Inspector of elections.
   - Advance-notice provisions for stockholder nominations and proposals (timing windows; required disclosures; ownership requirements).
3. **Board of Directors**:
   - Number, election, term.
   - Classified board (if applicable).
   - Vacancies.
   - Removal.
   - Resignation.
   - Compensation.
   - Meetings (regular, special).
   - Notice.
   - Quorum + voting.
   - Action by written consent.
   - Telephonic / video participation.
   - Committees — audit, compensation, nominating; charter incorporated by reference.
4. **Officers** — president, vice presidents, treasurer, secretary; titles; duties; appointment and removal.
5. **Indemnification + Advancement** — broadest authorized by DGCL § 145.
6. **Stock**:
   - Certificates / uncertificated.
   - Transfers.
   - Lost certificates.
   - Record dates.
7. **Records** — books and records location; inspection rights consistent with DGCL § 220.
8. **Forum Selection** — Delaware Court of Chancery (or DGCL-court alternative) for internal-affairs claims; federal forum for Securities Act claims.
9. **Amendment** — by board or by stockholders (per certificate / DGCL § 109).

## Drafting Conventions
- Bylaws may be more easily amended than certificate; board-amendment authority can be reserved by certificate.
- Advance-notice provisions: typical 90-120 day window before anniversary of prior year's meeting; verify *Coster / Sears Hometown* compliance.
- Universal proxy (Rule 14a-19) — covered companies must include all duly nominated candidates on universal proxy card in contested elections.
- For private companies, simpler bylaws acceptable; for public, additional Exchange Act / NYSE/Nasdaq listing rule overlays.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The bylaws may contain any provision, not inconsistent with law or with the certificate of incorporation, relating to the business of the corporation, the conduct of its affairs, and its rights or powers or the rights or powers of its stockholders, directors, officers or employees." — 8 Del. C. § 109(b).
