## Currency Warning
Twombly/Iqbal plausibility standard generally not applied to affirmative defenses in many circuits — but trend is mixed; check circuit law (Ninth Circuit: *Kohler v. Flava Enters.*, 779 F.3d 1016 (9th Cir. 2015), declined to apply Twombly to affirmative defenses; some district courts have applied heightened standard).

## Template Type
Pleading — Defendant's Initial Responsive Pleading.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — match plaintiff's caption; "ANSWER AND AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES TO COMPLAINT" + counterclaim subtitle if applicable + jury demand.
2. **Paragraph-by-Paragraph Responses** — mirror complaint paragraph numbers:
   - "Admitted."
   - "Denied."
   - "Defendant lacks knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief about the truth of the allegations…, and therefore denies them" (Rule 8(b)(5)).
   - "The allegations of paragraph X constitute legal conclusions to which no response is required; to the extent a response is required, denied."
3. **General Denial Statement** — "Defendant denies any allegation in the Complaint not expressly admitted herein."
4. **Affirmative Defenses** — listed and numbered. For each, plead facts showing entitlement (recommended to avoid waiver):
   - Failure to state a claim.
   - Statute of limitations.
   - Laches / estoppel / waiver.
   - Accord and satisfaction.
   - Payment / release.
   - Assumption of risk / contributory or comparative negligence.
   - Failure to mitigate damages.
   - Statute of frauds.
   - Frustration of purpose / impossibility / impracticability.
   - Privilege (qualified / absolute / fair report / fair use / etc.).
   - Lack of personal jurisdiction / improper venue / insufficient service (if not previously raised; otherwise waived under Rule 12(h)).
   - Failure to join an indispensable party (Rule 19).
   - Arbitration / forum-selection clause.
   - Preemption (federal / state).
   - First Amendment / constitutional defenses.
   - Sovereign immunity / governmental immunity (where applicable).
   - Reservation of additional defenses learned in discovery.
5. **Counterclaim (if any)** — caption · jurisdiction · parties · factual allegations · counts · prayer · jury demand.
6. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Rule 8(b)(1)(B): denials must fairly respond to substance of allegation; general denial inadequate unless intending to controvert all allegations including jurisdiction.
- Rule 12(h)(1): defenses under 12(b)(2), (3), (4), (5) are waived if not asserted in motion or first responsive pleading.
- Compulsory counterclaims (arising out of the same transaction or occurrence) must be pleaded under Rule 13(a) or are forever barred.
- Affirmative defenses should be pleaded with sufficient factual basis to put plaintiff on notice (better practice even where plausibility not applied).
- Reserve right to amend; many districts permit amendment without leave within 21 days under Rule 15(a)(1).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "In responding to a pleading, a party must affirmatively state any avoidance or affirmative defense, including: accord and satisfaction; arbitration and award; assumption of risk; contributory negligence; duress; estoppel; failure of consideration; fraud; illegality; injury by fellow servant; laches; license; payment; release; res judicata; statute of frauds; statute of limitations; and waiver." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 8(c)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Companion to every complaint defended. Pair with Rule 12 pre-answer motion analysis to decide whether to file Rule 12(b)(6) motion to dismiss first or answer with defenses raised.
