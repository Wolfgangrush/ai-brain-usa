## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 26(b)(5) + Federal Rule of Evidence 502.

## Currency Warning
A party withholding information on privilege grounds must (i) expressly make the claim and (ii) describe the nature of documents/communications in a manner that — without revealing privileged information — will enable other parties to assess the claim (Rule 26(b)(5)(A)). Categorical privilege logs may be permissible by stipulation; the alternative is individual document logs.

## Template Type
Discovery Compliance Schedule.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption / Title** — "[Producing Party]'s Privilege Log."
2. **Definitions / Methodology** — what is being logged (Attorney-Client / Work Product / Joint Defense / Common Interest / Spousal / Other); categorical vs. document-by-document approach.
3. **Log Columns** (per row, for each privileged document):
   - **Document ID / Bates Range** (if applicable).
   - **Date.**
   - **Document Type** (email · memorandum · attachment · etc.).
   - **Author / From.**
   - **Recipients / To · CC · BCC.**
   - **Subject / Topic / Title.**
   - **Privilege Asserted** (Attorney-Client · Work Product · Both · Other).
   - **Privilege Basis** — brief description (e.g., "Communication between in-house counsel J. Smith and CFO re defense strategy for [case]").
4. **Categorical Approach Alternative** — by stipulation, broad descriptions of categories of documents (e.g., "All communications between [Counsel] and [Client] from 1/1/2023 to present concerning [topic]").
5. **FRE 502(d) Order Cross-Reference** — non-waiver of privilege in this proceeding or others.
6. **Continuing Duty** — supplementation as further documents withheld.

## Drafting Conventions
- Privilege log timing: typically with document production response; check Local Rules for specific deadlines.
- Attorney-Client elements: communication between attorney and client + made in confidence + for purpose of obtaining/providing legal advice.
- Work Product (Rule 26(b)(3)) elements: prepared in anticipation of litigation by/for party or representative + fact work product (substantial need + undue hardship) vs. opinion work product (near-absolute protection).
- FRE 502(b) inadvertent disclosure non-waiver: holder took reasonable steps to prevent + promptly to rectify.
- FRE 502(d) court order: non-waiver across all proceedings, federal and state.
- *Upjohn Co. v. United States*, 449 U.S. 383 (1981), governs corporate attorney-client privilege.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "When a party withholds information otherwise discoverable by claiming that the information is privileged or subject to protection as trial-preparation material, the party must: (i) expressly make the claim; and (ii) describe the nature of the documents, communications, or tangible things not produced or disclosed — and do so in a manner that, without revealing information itself privileged or protected, will enable other parties to assess the claim." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(b)(5)(A).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard discovery deliverable. Negotiate categorical approach at Rule 26(f) conference where possible to reduce burden.
