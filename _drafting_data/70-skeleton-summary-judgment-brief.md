# Summary Judgment Brief — Skeleton

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 56](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_56)
- Celotex / Anderson / Matsushita trilogy.

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Skeleton — drafter's outline; not a fillable form.

## Currency Warning
Local Rules vary widely on briefing schedules, page limits (typically 25 pages for principal brief; 10-15 for reply), and Local Rule 56.1 statement of material facts requirements.

## Template Type
Brief Skeleton / Drafter Outline.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction (1 page)** — preview of why summary judgment appropriate; specific elements not satisfied by opposing party.
2. **Statement of Material Facts (Local Rule 56.1)** — numbered paragraphs; each fact supported by specific record citation; not narrative, not argumentative.
3. **Procedural Background (1 paragraph).**
4. **Argument** — each issue/claim:
   - **Claim X — Movant Entitled to Judgment as a Matter of Law**
     - **A. Legal Standard.** Rule 56(a) + Celotex burden-shifting.
     - **B. Elements of Claim X.** List statutory / common-law elements.
     - **C. Element 1 — [Element Name]. Undisputed Facts Foreclose Plaintiff's Showing.**
       - Record-cited fact.
       - Opposing party cannot produce admissible evidence.
       - Inference favored to opposing party still does not save claim.
     - **D. Element 2.** (Same structure.)
     - **E. Even Drawing All Inferences in Opposing Party's Favor, No Reasonable Jury Could Find for Plaintiff.**
5. **Conclusion.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Lead with the SMF — court reads facts before arguments.
- For each fact, cite specific record (deposition page:line, exhibit, declaration).
- Movant burden: identify portions of record showing absence of genuine issue OR opposing party cannot meet its trial burden.
- Use *Matsushita*: even when motive at issue, opposing party must produce more than scintilla; speculation insufficient.
- For affirmative defense summary judgment, identify each element of defense; show undisputed facts establish each.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The court shall grant summary judgment if the movant shows that there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard brief skeleton. Use this with statement of material facts schedule + declarations.
