# Settlement Agreement and Release

## Source URLs
- State contract law (governs interpretation).
- 26 U.S.C. § 104 (tax treatment of personal-injury settlements).
- 26 U.S.C. § 162(q) (no deduction for confidential sexual-harassment settlements — Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017).

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Common-law contract.

## Currency Warning
TCJA 2017 § 162(q): no business-expense deduction for settlements or payments related to sexual harassment / abuse if subject to NDA. Many states (NY, CA, IL, others) restrict NDAs in sexual-harassment / discrimination settlements; SPEAK OUT Act of 2022 (Pub. L. 117-224) renders pre-dispute NDAs unenforceable for sexual-assault and sexual-harassment claims.

## Template Type
Settlement Contract.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Recitals.**
2. **Settlement Consideration** — amount; payment timing; tax treatment representation (no tax advice rendered).
3. **Form of Payment** — wire / check; payee (settlement-trust account if attorney involved).
4. **Tax Allocation** — § 104 personal-injury exemption breakdown if applicable; W-2 / 1099 treatment.
5. **Release of Claims**:
   - Universe of claims released (broad: "all claims, known or unknown, arising from the beginning of time to the date hereof").
   - Carve-outs (e.g., enforcement of this agreement; non-waivable claims).
   - Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 waiver of unknown claims (California parties).
   - ADEA waivers (29 U.S.C. § 626(f)) — Older Workers Benefit Protection Act 21/45-day consideration + 7-day revocation requirements.
6. **No Admission of Liability** — boilerplate non-admission.
7. **Confidentiality** — with state-law limits (SPEAK OUT carve-out; whistleblower carve-out; state-law sexual-harassment limits).
8. **Non-Disparagement** — mutual; carve-outs.
9. **Cooperation** — for transition / future proceedings.
10. **Indemnification** — for prior released claims if asserted.
11. **Tax Indemnity** — if applicable.
12. **Governing Law · Forum · Notices · Entire Agreement · Counterparts · E-Sign.**
13. **Effective Date Language** — for ADEA waivers, after 7-day revocation period.

## Drafting Conventions
- For employment settlements, comply with OWBPA (21/45-day consideration; 7-day revocation; written advice to consult counsel) where releasing ADEA claims.
- Address §§ 162(q) and SPEAK OUT Act for sexual-harassment settlements — confidentiality is restricted.
- For non-disparagement and non-admission, ensure both sides bound symmetrically.
- For installment payments, address security / default consequences.
- 1099 vs. W-2: 1099 for non-wage payments; W-2 for back-pay and lost-wages portions.
- Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 waiver required for California parties to release unknown claims.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "It shall not be lawful, in the case of a settlement, judgment, or other resolution of an employment dispute that involves a sexual harassment dispute or a sexual assault dispute, to enforce a predispute nondisclosure clause or predispute nondisparagement clause with respect to a sexual assault dispute or a sexual harassment dispute filed under Federal, Tribal, or State law…" — Speak Out Act, Pub. L. 117-224.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard settlement-resolution instrument. Critical sequencing with ADEA / OWBPA waiver and state-specific confidentiality limits.
