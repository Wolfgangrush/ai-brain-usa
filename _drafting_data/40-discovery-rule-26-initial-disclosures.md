# Rule 26(a)(1) Initial Disclosures

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(a)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_26)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 26(a)(1).

## Currency Warning
Initial disclosures must be made at or within 14 days after the Rule 26(f) conference unless court orders otherwise (Rule 26(a)(1)(C)). Parties cannot rely on a contention that they have not fully investigated the case as basis for withholding disclosures (Rule 26(a)(1)(E)).

## Template Type
Discovery Disclosure Document.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — case caption + "PLAINTIFF'S / DEFENDANT'S INITIAL DISCLOSURES PURSUANT TO RULE 26(a)(1)."
2. **(A) Persons Likely to Have Discoverable Information** — name; address; subjects of information; party-controlled note where applicable.
3. **(B) Documents, ESI, and Tangible Things** — categories and locations sufficient for opposing party to make Rule 34 requests; identify categories (not necessarily every individual document); production may be deferred to discovery responses.
4. **(C) Computation of Damages** — for each category claimed (compensatory, special, punitive, statutory); making available documents on which computation based.
5. **(D) Insurance Agreements** — produce or make available any insurance agreement under which an insurance business may be liable to satisfy all or part of a possible judgment.
6. **Reservation of Right to Supplement** — under Rule 26(e) duty to supplement.
7. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Categories rather than exhaustive document lists for (B); production deferred to Rule 34.
- Damage computation in (C) must be "as much as is then known"; avoid both under- and over-disclosure.
- Even where insurance amount is confidential, the existence and limits must be disclosed under (D).
- Rule 26(e) imposes continuing duty to supplement if response incomplete or incorrect.
- Failure to disclose under Rule 26(a) triggers Rule 37(c)(1) exclusion sanction at trial unless substantially justified or harmless.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Except as exempted by Rule 26(a)(1)(B) or as otherwise stipulated or ordered by the court, a party must, without awaiting a discovery request, provide to the other parties: (i) the name and, if known, the address and telephone number of each individual likely to have discoverable information — along with the subjects of that information — that the disclosing party may use to support its claims or defenses…" — Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(a)(1)(A).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard kick-off discovery document. Tied to Rule 26(f) conference timing.
