# U.S. Tax Court Petition

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Tax Court Rules of Practice and Procedure + IRC § 6213.

## Currency Warning
Petition must be filed within 90 days of the date of the notice of deficiency (150 days if addressed to person outside the U.S.). Late filing = no Tax Court jurisdiction; remedy then is payment + refund-claim suit in district court / Court of Federal Claims.

## Template Type
Initiating Petition.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — "United States Tax Court · [Petitioner] v. Commissioner of Internal Revenue, Respondent. PETITION."
2. **Petitioner Information** — name; address; TIN (typically redacted in publicly filed copy); contact information.
3. **Notice of Deficiency** — date; tax year(s); amount(s); attach notice as exhibit.
4. **Errors Alleged** — paragraph-by-paragraph specification of errors in the Commissioner's determination.
5. **Facts** — supporting petitioner's position.
6. **Prayer for Relief** — redetermination of deficiency; finding of overpayment; etc.
7. **Signature** by petitioner (or counsel admitted to Tax Court bar).
8. **Filing Fee** ($60 — verify current).
9. **Statement of Taxpayer Identification Number** (Form 4 — filed separately due to privacy).
10. **Designation of Place of Trial** (Form 5).

## Drafting Conventions
- 90-day jurisdictional deadline is unforgiving — calendar at intake.
- Practitioners must be admitted to Tax Court bar (separate examination); non-admitted attorneys can prepare with petitioner pro se signature.
- Small-case procedure (§ 7463) — for deficiencies ≤ $50,000 per year; simplified procedures; non-precedential decision; no appeal.
- For collection due process (CDP) cases, separate petition from CDP notice of determination (§ 6320 / 6330).
- Innocent spouse (§ 6015) petitions — separate procedural requirements.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Within 90 days, or 150 days if the notice is addressed to a person outside the United States, after the notice of deficiency authorized in section 6212 is mailed (not counting Saturday, Sunday, or a legal holiday in the District of Columbia as the last day), the taxpayer may file a petition with the Tax Court for a redetermination of the deficiency." — 26 U.S.C. § 6213(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Tax-controversy practice anchor. Pair with Form 4 (TIN statement) and Form 5 (designation of trial location).
