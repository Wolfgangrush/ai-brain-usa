# DMCA Takedown Notice (17 U.S.C. § 512(c))

## Source URLs
- [17 U.S.C. § 512](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/17/512)
- [U.S. Copyright Office — DMCA Designated Agent Directory](https://www.copyright.gov/dmca-directory/)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Digital Millennium Copyright Act 1998 (codified at 17 U.S.C. § 512).

## Currency Warning
*Lenz v. Universal Music Corp.*, 815 F.3d 1145 (9th Cir. 2016) — copyright holders must consider fair use before issuing § 512(c)(3) takedown; failure may be § 512(f) misrepresentation.

## Template Type
Statutory Notice.

## Structural Skeleton (per § 512(c)(3)(A))
1. **Physical or Electronic Signature** of a person authorized to act on behalf of the owner.
2. **Identification of Copyrighted Work** — title, registration number, URL of original (if online).
3. **Identification of Allegedly Infringing Material** — specific URL where material is located; date of identification.
4. **Contact Information** — address, phone, email of complaining party.
5. **Statement of Good-Faith Belief** — "I have a good faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."
6. **Statement Under Penalty of Perjury** — "I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in this notification is accurate and that I am the copyright owner or am authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed."
7. **Designated Agent Recipient** — registered DMCA agent of the service provider (find at copyright.gov DMCA directory).

## Drafting Conventions
- Provide URL granular enough to permit removal — broad takedown of entire site / category likely invalid.
- Pre-issuance: conduct fair-use analysis per *Lenz*; document the analysis.
- For repeat-infringer cases, document a history with the platform.
- § 512(f) misrepresentation: knowing material misrepresentation may result in damages including attorney's fees.
- For counter-notification by alleged infringer (§ 512(g)(3)), platform reposts content unless copyright holder sues within 14 business days.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "To be effective under this subsection, a notification of claimed infringement must be a written communication provided to the designated agent of a service provider that includes substantially the following…" — 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3)(A).
