# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

- [Uniform Trade Secrets Act (UTSA)](https://www.uniformlaws.org/committees/community-home?CommunityKey=3a2538fb-e030-4e2d-a9e2-90373dc05792) — enacted in 48 states.

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Contract law + UTSA/DTSA trade-secret backstop + state UTSA enactment.

## Currency Warning
DTSA whistleblower-immunity notice (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)) is required to preserve enhanced damages and attorney's fees against employees/contractors disclosing trade secrets to a government official solely for the purpose of reporting a suspected violation of law, or filing under seal in a lawsuit alleging retaliation. NDA without this notice loses exemplary damages and attorney's fees against the employee/contractor.

## Template Type
Confidentiality / Trade-Secret Contract.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Effective Date + Purpose** — narrowly state the business purpose for which information will be exchanged (anti "all-purposes" usage).
2. **Definition of Confidential Information** — both prongs (a) information marked confidential, and (b) information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential; written-confirmation requirement for oral disclosures within agreed days.
3. **Exclusions** — already public · independently known to recipient before disclosure (documented) · received from third party without breach · independently developed without use of confidential information.
4. **Use Restrictions** — only for the stated Purpose; no reverse engineering, except where permitted by law (CA, MA exceptions in certain contexts).
5. **Standard of Care** — same degree of care recipient uses for its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care.
6. **Need-to-Know + Bound Recipients** — recipient personnel under written confidentiality obligations no less protective.
7. **Required Disclosures** — compelled by law / order: notice to disclosing party · seek protective order · limit disclosure.
8. **Term and Survival** — Confidentiality obligations: 3-5 years post-termination (longer for trade secrets — "for so long as the information qualifies as a trade secret").
9. **Return or Destruction** — on request or termination.
10. **No Licence Granted** — disclosure does not grant any rights in IP.
11. **Injunctive Relief** — recipient acknowledges breach causes irreparable harm; injunctive relief available without bond.
12. **Residuals Clause** — narrow if any (memory residuals only; not enforceable for trade secrets in many states); often excluded.
13. **DTSA Whistleblower Notice** — verbatim 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b) text.
14. **Governing Law · Forum · Notices · Counterparts · Electronic Signature.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Avoid "all our information" definitions; specify categories (technical, financial, customer-related, pricing, etc.).
- Reverse-engineering carve-outs invite trade-secret leakage; consider banning unless expressly permitted.
- DTSA notice should appear in any contract governing employee or contractor confidentiality — applies broadly.
- Equitable injunctive-relief clause expressly waiving bond requirement is enforceable in most U.S. jurisdictions but verify state-by-state.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that — (A) is made — (i) in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney; and (ii) solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law…" — 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)(1)(A).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Daily-use contract for any vendor/customer/M&A discussion. Pair with disclosure log and personnel-onboarding acknowledgement schedule.
