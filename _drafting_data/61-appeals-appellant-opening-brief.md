## Currency Warning
Word limits: principal brief 13,000 words; reply 6,500 (Rule 32(a)(7)). Some circuits have shorter local-rule limits. Bluebook 21st citation; certificate of compliance with type-volume limitation required (Rule 32(g)).

## Template Type
Appellate Brief.

## Structural Skeleton (Rule 28(a))
1. **Corporate Disclosure Statement** (Rule 26.1).
2. **Table of Contents.**
3. **Table of Authorities** — cases (alphabetical) · statutes · rules · other authorities.
4. **Jurisdictional Statement** — basis for district court jurisdiction; basis for appellate jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1291 final judgment; § 1292 interlocutory); date of judgment + date of notice of appeal demonstrating timeliness.
5. **Statement of Issues Presented.**
6. **Statement of the Case** — procedural history + facts relevant to issues; record citations.
7. **Standard of Review** — for each issue.
8. **Summary of Argument** — one or two pages.
9. **Argument** — substantive; record-cited; with applicable standard of review identified for each subsection.
10. **Conclusion** — precise relief sought (reverse · vacate · remand · affirm).
11. **Certificate of Compliance** with type-volume (Rule 32(g)).
12. **Certificate of Service.**
13. **Addendum** with statutes / regulations / rules (circuit-specific).

## Drafting Conventions
- Issue framing: neutral, leading-question-style; 1-3 issues maximum unless complexity demands more.
- Standard of review: identify for each issue — de novo · clear error · abuse of discretion · sufficiency of evidence (substantial evidence / no rational fact-finder).
- Cite record by joint appendix page references (Rule 30); circuit-specific record-citation conventions (e.g., "JA__").
- Statement of facts: only facts relevant to issues; record-cited; non-argumentative tone.
- Lead each argument section with the rule, not the facts.
