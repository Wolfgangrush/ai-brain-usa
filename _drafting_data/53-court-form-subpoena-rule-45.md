# Subpoena (Rule 45) — Form AO 88, AO 88A, AO 88B

## Source URLs
- [Administrative Office — Subpoena Forms](https://www.uscourts.gov/forms-rules/forms/court-forms)
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 45](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_45)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 45.

## Currency Warning
Rule 45(c) geographic limits: testimony — within 100 miles of where person resides, is employed, or transacts business in person; for trial, within state where person resides. For productions, no 100-mile limit, but place of production must be within 100 miles. Notice to other parties before service (Rule 45(a)(4)).

## Template Type
Court Form (Rule 45 Subpoena to Non-Party).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **AO 88 — Subpoena to Testify at a Trial or Hearing.**
2. **AO 88A — Subpoena to Testify at a Deposition.**
3. **AO 88B — Subpoena to Produce Documents.**
4. **Issuing Court and Caption.**
5. **To: [Non-Party Name and Address].**
6. **Command** — attend and testify at place, date, time; OR produce documents specified in Attachment A.
7. **Attachment A — Document Requests** — categorical descriptions with reasonable particularity.
8. **Witness Fee + Mileage** — tendered with service (28 U.S.C. § 1821 — $40/day attendance + $0.67/mile mileage as of 2025; verify current rate).
9. **Notice to Parties** — file copy with court and serve all parties BEFORE serving the subpoena recipient (Rule 45(a)(4)).
10. **Rule 45(d) Protections** — quoted on subpoena: avoiding undue burden, sanctions for unreasonable demands; rights of objection within 14 days (Rule 45(d)(2)(B)).
11. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Distinguish subpoenas for testimony (AO 88, AO 88A) from subpoenas for documents (AO 88B); often combined.
- Tendering fees: required by Rule 45(b)(1); failure may invalidate service.
- For ESI from non-parties, address format and burden — courts apply Rule 45(d)(2)(B) protections.
- Notice to opposing parties under Rule 45(a)(4) MUST occur before serving the subpoena.
- For business records, consider Rule 45(c)(2) — production at place of business; combined with Rule 902(11)/(12) self-authentication for trial use.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A party or attorney responsible for issuing and serving a subpoena must take reasonable steps to avoid imposing undue burden or expense on a person subject to the subpoena. The court for the district where compliance is required must enforce this duty and impose an appropriate sanction — which may include lost earnings and reasonable attorney's fees — on a party or attorney who fails to comply." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 45(d)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Workhorse non-party discovery tool. Calendar 14-day objection window; coordinate logistics for compliance / production.
