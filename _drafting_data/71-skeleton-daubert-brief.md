## Currency Warning
2023 amendment to Rule 702 placed burden on proponent and tightened gatekeeping inquiry — "more likely than not" standard. Some lower courts had not consistently applied this standard pre-2023; the amendment clarifies that the four factors must be shown.

## Template Type
Brief Skeleton.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Introduction** — preview of why expert opinion(s) should be excluded.
2. **Background on the Expert and Proposed Opinion.**
3. **Legal Standard** — Rule 702 (post-2023); Daubert/Kumho/Joiner trilogy.
4. **Argument**:
   - **A. The Proponent Has Not Met Its Burden Under Rule 702.**
   - **B. Element (a) — The Expert's Knowledge Does Not Help the Trier of Fact.**
     - Subject matter ordinary jurors can evaluate without expert help.
     - Expert opinion intrudes on jury function (credibility, legal conclusions).
   - **C. Element (b) — The Testimony Is Not Based on Sufficient Facts or Data.**
     - Expert relied on unverified facts.
     - Sample size insufficient.
     - Failure to consider alternative explanations.
   - **D. Element (c) — The Testimony Is Not the Product of Reliable Principles and Methods.**
     - Methodology not generally accepted.
     - Not subjected to peer review.
     - Lack of testable hypothesis or error-rate.
   - **E. Element (d) — The Expert Did Not Reliably Apply the Principles and Methods to the Facts.**
     - Analytical gap between methodology and conclusions.
     - "Ipse dixit" — expert's conclusory assertion (*Joiner*).
5. **Conclusion.**

## Drafting Conventions
- For each Rule 702 prong, identify specific failures in the expert's report or deposition.
- Cite the expert's report and deposition by specific page/paragraph.
- For methodology challenges, cite peer-reviewed authority showing method unreliability.
- Where expert opinion is critical, summary judgment may follow exclusion.
- Daubert hearings typically requested for high-stakes challenges.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A witness who is qualified as an expert… may testify in the form of an opinion or otherwise if the proponent demonstrates to the court that it is more likely than not that: (a) the expert's scientific, technical, or other specialized knowledge will help the trier of fact…; (b) the testimony is based on sufficient facts or data; (c) the testimony is the product of reliable principles and methods; and (d) the expert's opinion reflects a reliable application of the principles and methods to the facts of the case." — Fed. R. Evid. 702.
