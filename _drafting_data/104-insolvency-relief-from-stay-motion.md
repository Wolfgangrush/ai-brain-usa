# Motion for Relief from Automatic Stay (§ 362(d))

## Source URLs
- [11 U.S.C. § 362](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/362)
- [Fed. R. Bankr. P. 4001](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frbp/rule_4001)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Bankruptcy Code § 362 + FRBP 4001.

## Currency Warning
Automatic stay terminates if hearing on motion concludes (or court extends preliminary) within 30 days of filing in absence of cause (§ 362(e)(1)). For individual debtors, 60-day requirement under § 362(e)(2). Bankruptcy courts apply local rules establishing specific timing.

## Template Type
Motion + Memorandum + Supporting Declaration.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — bankruptcy court + "MOTION FOR RELIEF FROM AUTOMATIC STAY PURSUANT TO 11 U.S.C. § 362(D)."
2. **Introduction.**
3. **Background** — petition filing; debtor's interest in collateral; movant's interest.
4. **Legal Standard** — § 362(d) grounds:
   - **(1) For cause, including lack of adequate protection.**
   - **(2) Property not necessary to effective reorganization + no equity in property** (for non-Chapter-7 cases).
   - **(3) Single-asset real estate special grounds.**
   - **(4) Repeat-filer for hindering / delay / fraud.**
5. **Argument**:
   - **Cause / Lack of Adequate Protection** — collateral depreciating; no insurance; debtor failure to make post-petition payments.
   - **No Equity + Not Necessary** — fair-market valuation; lack of reorganization viability.
6. **Relief Requested** — lift / annul / condition stay; permit foreclosure / collection action.
7. **Proposed Order.**
8. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- For lack of adequate protection (§ 362(d)(1)), demonstrate collateral value declining or debtor failing to maintain insurance.
- For § 362(d)(2), establish: no equity (value less than liens) + not necessary for effective reorganization (no realistic reorganization prospect in reasonable time).
- *In re Briggs Transp. Co.*, 780 F.2d 1339 (8th Cir. 1985), articulates "effective reorganization" test.
- Single-asset real estate (§ 362(d)(3)) — 90 days to file plan or pay interest; stricter timing.
- Repeat filers (§ 362(d)(4)) — limit on automatic stay for repeat filings within 1 year.
- Use preliminary hearing within 30 days; final hearing within 30 days after preliminary.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "On request of a party in interest and after notice and a hearing, the court shall grant relief from the stay provided under subsection (a) of this section, such as by terminating, annulling, modifying, or conditioning such stay — (1) for cause, including the lack of adequate protection of an interest in property of such party in interest; or (2) with respect to a stay of an act against property under subsection (a) of this section, if — (A) the debtor does not have an equity in such property; and (B) such property is not necessary to an effective reorganization." — 11 U.S.C. § 362(d).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard secured-creditor motion in bankruptcy. Coordinate with valuation analysis and post-petition payment history.
