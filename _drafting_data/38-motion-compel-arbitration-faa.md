# Motion to Compel Arbitration (FAA § 4)

## Source URLs
- [9 U.S.C. § 4](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/9/4)
- [9 U.S.C. § 3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/9/3)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Federal Arbitration Act §§ 2, 3, 4.

## Currency Warning
*Smith v. Spizzirri*, 601 U.S. 472 (2024) — § 3 requires district court to STAY, not dismiss, when arbitration ordered. *Coinbase, Inc. v. Bielski*, 599 U.S. 736 (2023) — interlocutory appeal of arbitration denial automatically stays district-court proceedings. Sexual harassment / assault carve-out under 9 U.S.C. § 402 (EFAA 2022) — predispute arbitration unenforceable at claimant's election for those claims.

## Template Type
Motion + Memorandum + Declarations + Proposed Order.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — case caption + "DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO COMPEL ARBITRATION AND TO STAY PROCEEDINGS."
2. **Introduction** — arbitration agreement; FAA basis; relief sought (compel + stay).
3. **Factual Background** — formation of arbitration agreement; scope; delegation clause (if any).
4. **Argument**:
   - **Existence of valid agreement** under state contract law (*First Options of Chicago, Inc. v. Kaplan*, 514 U.S. 938 (1995)).
   - **Agreement covers the dispute** — under contract interpretation; resolve ambiguities in favor of arbitration (*Moses H. Cone Memorial Hosp. v. Mercury Constr. Corp.*, 460 U.S. 1 (1983)).
   - **No applicable defense** under § 2 savings clause — only generally applicable contract-law defenses (unconscionability, duress, fraud) — not arbitration-specific (*AT&T Mobility LLC v. Concepcion*, 563 U.S. 333 (2011)).
   - **Delegation clause** — if clear-and-unmistakable, arbitrability questions for arbitrator (*Henry Schein, Inc. v. Archer & White Sales, Inc.*, 586 U.S. 63 (2019)).
   - **EFAA carve-out check** — confirm no sexual harassment / assault claim being arbitrated against claimant's election.
   - **§ 1 transportation worker exclusion** — confirm not within (*Bissonnette v. LePage Bakeries Park St., LLC*, 601 U.S. 246 (2024)).
5. **Relief Requested** — order compelling arbitration; STAY (not dismiss) per *Spizzirri*; pending arbitration award.
6. **Proposed Order.**
7. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Identify the arbitration provider (AAA, JAMS, ICDR) and applicable rules.
- For class-action claims, address class-waiver enforceability post-*Concepcion* + *Epic Sys. Corp. v. Lewis*, 584 U.S. 497 (2018).
- For employment claims, address EFAA election possibility.
- Move PROMPTLY to avoid waiver argument; courts vary on what constitutes waiver but participation in merits litigation generally waives (*Morgan v. Sundance, Inc.*, 596 U.S. 411 (2022)) — abolished prejudice requirement.
- Pair with declaration attaching arbitration agreement + signed acknowledgment if any.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A party aggrieved by the alleged failure, neglect, or refusal of another to arbitrate under a written agreement for arbitration may petition any United States district court… for an order directing that such arbitration proceed in the manner provided for in such agreement." — 9 U.S.C. § 4.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard defensive motion. Move at first opportunity — *Morgan v. Sundance* abolished prejudice requirement so any merits engagement risks waiver finding.
