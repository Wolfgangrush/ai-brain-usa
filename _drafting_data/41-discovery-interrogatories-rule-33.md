# Interrogatories (Rule 33)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 33](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_33)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 33.

## Currency Warning
Rule 33(a)(1): a party may serve no more than 25 written interrogatories, including all discrete subparts, on any other party unless the court orders or parties stipulate otherwise. Boilerplate objections insufficient; must state objection with specificity (Rule 33(b)(4)).

## Template Type
Discovery Request Set.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — case caption + "PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT."
2. **Preliminary Instructions** — definitions (incorporating Local Rule definitions); time period; continuing-obligation reminder.
3. **Definitions** — "You," "Defendant," "Document," "Person," "Communication," "Identify," "Concerning," "Date of Loss."
4. **Instructions** — answer separately, fully, in writing, under oath (Rule 33(b)(3)); time period covered; privilege log requirements.
5. **Interrogatories** — numbered, narrowly scoped to elements of claims and defenses:
   - Identify witnesses to the events of [date].
   - Describe the basis for affirmative defense No. [X].
   - State all facts supporting your denial of paragraph [X] of the Complaint.
   - Identify all documents reviewed in answering the foregoing interrogatories.
6. **Verification Page** — for use on response (Rule 33(b)(5)).
7. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- 25-limit includes "all discrete subparts" — courts split on what constitutes discrete subpart; generally subparts that "stand alone" count.
- "Compound" or "open-ended" interrogatories invite objections — narrow each one.
- Pair interrogatories with Rule 34 RFPs; interrogatories establish theory, RFPs document the foundation.
- Contention interrogatories (asking about facts supporting positions) typically deferred until later in discovery (Rule 33(a)(2)).
- Rule 33(d) option to produce business records in lieu of answer requires specifying records with sufficient detail.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Unless otherwise stipulated or ordered by the court, a party may serve on any other party no more than 25 written interrogatories, including all discrete subparts. Leave to serve additional interrogatories may be granted to the extent consistent with Rule 26(b)(1) and (2)." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 33(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard early-stage discovery instrument. Reserve high-value interrogatories for contention-shape questions after document discovery.
