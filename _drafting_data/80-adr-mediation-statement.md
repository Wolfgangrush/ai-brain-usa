# Mediation Statement / Position Statement

## Source URLs
- ADR Act 1998 — 28 U.S.C. § 651 et seq.
- Court / mediator-specific procedures.

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Procedural — court-ordered or contractual ADR.

## Currency Warning
Federal Mediation Privilege common-law analog under Rule 501 — communications during mediation generally confidential; specific protection varies by jurisdiction. Uniform Mediation Act adopted in some states.

## Template Type
Pre-Mediation Brief Submitted to Mediator.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Cover Page** — identification; mediator name; date.
2. **Confidentiality Designation** — typically submitted only to mediator (ex parte) unless joint submission directed.
3. **Procedural Background** — case posture; key dates.
4. **Statement of Claims and Defenses** — concise.
5. **Legal Analysis** — key issues; controlling authority; likely outcome on merits.
6. **Damages Analysis** — itemization; expert reports referenced; insurance coverage discussed.
7. **Settlement History** — prior offers and demands.
8. **Position for Mediation** — opening demand; bottom line (often shared with mediator confidentially).
9. **Non-Monetary Considerations** — injunctive relief; restrictive covenants; releases; confidentiality.
10. **Attendees and Authority** — identify decision-makers with settlement authority required at mediation.
11. **Exhibits** — key documents; expert reports.

## Drafting Conventions
- Tone: candid with the mediator; arguments persuasive but credible.
- Ex parte: include strategic context the other side won't see.
- Address weak points honestly; the mediator will spot them.
- For evaluative mediation, include detailed legal analysis; for facilitative, focus on interests.
- For business clients, include relationship considerations + reputational concerns.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The procedures established for ADR will not be relevant to the merits of the underlying dispute and will not be admissible at trial." — *typical ADR confidentiality framing under court ADR rules*.

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard pre-mediation deliverable. Pair with settlement spreadsheet (damages model) and authority confirmation from client.
