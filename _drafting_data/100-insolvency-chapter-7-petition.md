## Currency Warning
BAPCPA 2005 means test (11 U.S.C. § 707(b)(2)) — individuals with above-median income subject to abuse presumption based on means-test calculation; verify current median income / IRS Local Standards / Statement of Currently Monthly Income.

## Template Type
Petition Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Form 101 (Individual) or Form 201 (Non-Individual) Petition.**
2. **Schedules A-J** — assets, liabilities, income, expenses, executory contracts, codebtors, equity security holders.
3. **Statement of Financial Affairs** (Form 107 or 207).
4. **Means Test** (Form 122A-1, A-2 — individual cases).
5. **Schedule of Assets and Liabilities** — detail of each asset and claim.
6. **Credit Counseling Certificate** — required pre-filing for individuals (§ 109(h)).
7. **Statement of Intention** (Form 108) — secured-creditor treatment.
8. **Disclosure of Compensation of Attorney** (Form 2030).
9. **Mailing Matrix** — creditor address list.
10. **Filing Fee or Application to Pay in Installments / Waiver** ($338 chapter 7 fee — verify).
11. **List of 20 Largest Unsecured Creditors** (non-individual cases).

## Drafting Conventions
- Pre-filing: bankruptcy-petition preparer / attorney must comply with § 110 disclosure obligations.
- Means test: above-median income presumption of abuse; may rebut with special circumstances.
- Schedule values: fair market value, not purchase price.
- Statement of Financial Affairs: 2 years' look-back on transfers, insider payments, account closures.
- Automatic stay (§ 362) effective on filing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A case under a chapter of this title… is commenced by the filing with the bankruptcy court of a petition under such chapter by an entity that may be a debtor under such chapter." — 11 U.S.C. § 301(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foundation of consumer bankruptcy practice. Coordinate with means-test calculation and required pre-filing credit counseling.
