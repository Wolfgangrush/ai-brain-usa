## Currency Warning
Plausibility standard governs under *Bell Atl. Corp. v. Twombly*, 550 U.S. 544 (2007), and *Ashcroft v. Iqbal*, 556 U.S. 662 (2009) — labels and conclusions insufficient; factual allegations must plausibly entitle plaintiff to relief. Check the District's Local Rules for chambers-copy requirements, page limits, and ECF filing protocols.

## Template Type
Pleading — Plaintiff's Initial Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — court name · case number (left blank pre-filing) · plaintiff · v. · defendant · COMPLAINT · JURY DEMAND (Fed. R. Civ. P. 38).
2. **Preliminary Statement / Introduction** — 1-3 paragraphs summarising the case (optional but increasingly common).
3. **Parties** — name · citizenship · principal place of business · place where claims arise.
4. **Jurisdiction and Venue**:
   - Subject-matter jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1331 federal question OR § 1332 diversity with amount-in-controversy > $75,000);
   - Personal jurisdiction over each defendant (general / specific + due-process minimum-contacts analysis);
   - Venue under 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b)(2) — substantial events / property in district.
5. **Factual Allegations** — numbered paragraphs (Rule 10(b)). Plead specific dates, conduct, communications, and the alleged unlawful acts. Avoid conclusory characterizations; show causation and harm.
6. **Causes of Action / Counts** — each cause of action in separate count with heading; for each: elements; facts satisfying each element; relief sought for that count.
7. **Prayer for Relief** — itemize: compensatory damages · punitive damages (where pleadable) · pre/post-judgment interest · attorney's fees and costs (specify statutory basis if any) · injunctive or declaratory relief · jury demand · "such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper."
8. **Signature Block** — counsel name · bar number · address · phone · email · "Attorney for Plaintiff."

## Drafting Conventions
- Twombly/Iqbal plausibility: allege specific facts, not legal conclusions. "On information and belief" is acceptable only where supported by reasonable basis.
- For § 1332 diversity: plead citizenship of each party with specificity; for LLCs, allege citizenship of every member.
- For fraud-based claims: comply with Rule 9(b) heightened-pleading (who, what, when, where, how).
- For class actions: include Rule 23 requirements (numerosity, commonality, typicality, adequacy + class definition + class-action allegations + subclasses if applicable).
- Local Rules: many districts require Civil Cover Sheet (JS-44) and corporate-disclosure statement (Rule 7.1) at filing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A pleading that states a claim for relief must contain: (1) a short and plain statement of the grounds for the court's jurisdiction…; (2) a short and plain statement of the claim showing that the pleader is entitled to relief; and (3) a demand for the relief sought…" — Fed. R. Civ. P. 8(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
The opening pleading of every federal civil matter. Pair with Civil Cover Sheet (§ 51), Summons (§ 52), Corporate Disclosure (§ 56), and Local-Rule chambers-copy delivery.
