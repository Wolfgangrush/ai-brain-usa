# Amended Complaint (Rule 15)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 15](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_15)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 15.

## Currency Warning
Rule 15(a)(1) permits one amendment as a matter of course within 21 days after service OR 21 days after service of responsive pleading or Rule 12 motion. After that period, leave of court or written consent required (Rule 15(a)(2)); leave "freely given when justice so requires." Relation-back under Rule 15(c) is critical for statute-of-limitations preservation.

## Template Type
Pleading — Amended Initial Pleading.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — main case caption · "FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT" (or higher number).
2. **Preliminary Note** — "Plaintiff [name], by and through counsel, hereby files this First Amended Complaint pursuant to Rule 15(a)(1) [or with consent of Defendant or with leave of Court granted by Order dated [date]]."
3. **Complete Restated Pleading** — file the complete amended complaint as one document; the amended complaint supersedes the original (*Loux v. Rhay*, 375 F.2d 55 (9th Cir. 1967)).
4. **All Required Components** of a complaint — caption · parties · jurisdiction · venue · factual allegations · counts · prayer · jury demand · signature.
5. **Redline / Comparison Document** — attach as exhibit if required by Local Rule.
6. **Certificate of Service** (Rule 5) — for parties already served.
7. **Summons + Service** — for new parties added under Rule 15 (Rule 4 service).

## Drafting Conventions
- File complete amended complaint — partial amendments not permitted; the amended complaint becomes the operative pleading.
- For relation-back under Rule 15(c)(1)(B), the amendment must arise out of the conduct, transaction, or occurrence set out in the original pleading.
- For relation-back under Rule 15(c)(1)(C) for changing defendant identity: (i) new party received notice within Rule 4(m) period; (ii) new party knew or should have known action would have been brought against it but for a mistake concerning identity.
- *Krupski v. Costa Crociere S.p.A.*, 560 U.S. 538 (2010), focuses Rule 15(c)(1)(C) on what new party knew/should have known.
- Local Rules often require lodging proposed amended complaint as exhibit to motion for leave with redline showing changes.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A party may amend its pleading once as a matter of course no later than: (A) 21 days after serving it, or (B) if the pleading is one to which a responsive pleading is required, 21 days after service of a responsive pleading or 21 days after service of a motion under Rule 12(b), (e), or (f), whichever is earlier." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 15(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical motion practice tool to cure pleading defects identified by Rule 12 motion or in discovery. Time amendment to preserve statute-of-limitations relation-back protections.
