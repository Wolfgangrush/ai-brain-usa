# Notice of Appearance

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 5 (service)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_5)
- Local Rules of each U.S. District Court (varies — e.g., S.D.N.Y. Local Civil Rule 1.3).

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Procedural — Local Rules + court-specific CM/ECF practices.

## Currency Warning
Verify Local Rule requirements (admitted status, sponsor for pro hac vice, address in district). Most districts now register attorneys via PACER/CM/ECF; e-filing with verified electronic signature.

## Template Type
Procedural Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption.**
2. **Statement** — "The undersigned hereby enters an appearance in the above-captioned matter as counsel for [Party Name]. All future correspondence and pleadings should be directed to the undersigned at the address below."
3. **Bar Admission** — admitted to practice in this District; bar number; admission date.
4. **Pro Hac Vice** (if applicable) — motion accompanying.
5. **Contact Information** — address; phone; email; fax (if required).
6. **Certificate of Service.**
7. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- File at first engagement; necessary for CM/ECF notifications.
- Multiple counsel may file separate notices; lead-counsel designation per Local Rule.
- For pro hac vice, file motion concurrently with local-counsel sponsorship.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "If a party is represented by an attorney, service under this rule must be made on the attorney unless the court orders service on the party." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 5(b)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
First filing in any case where counsel enters appearance.
