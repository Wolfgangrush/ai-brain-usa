# Writ of Execution / Judgment Enforcement (FRCP Rule 69)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 69 + state-law enforcement (federal courts apply state-law procedure under Rule 69(a)(1)) + FDCPA for government creditor.

## Currency Warning
Rule 69(a)(1) — federal court enforces money judgment by writ of execution UNLESS court directs otherwise; procedure governed by procedure of state where court located; federal statute on subject controls if any. State-by-state variation in execution procedure is substantial.

## Template Type
Court Form — Enforcement of Money Judgment.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — federal court + "WRIT OF EXECUTION."
2. **Judgment Information** — date of judgment; amount; post-judgment interest accrual (28 U.S.C. § 1961 rate).
3. **Judgment Debtor Information.**
4. **Direction to U.S. Marshal or other Officer** — "You are hereby directed to satisfy the judgment by levying on the property of the judgment debtor located in [district / county]."
5. **Property Subject to Levy** — identify specific assets if known; general levy if not.
6. **Exemptions** — note state-law and federal exemptions (homestead, tools-of-trade, wages, retirement accounts, etc.) — debtor's burden to claim.
7. **Return Date.**
8. **Companion Tools**:
   - **Writ of Garnishment** — for wages or third-party-held assets.
   - **Charging Order** — for partnership / LLC distributions.
   - **Judgment Debtor Examination** under Rule 69(a)(2) — depose judgment debtor about assets.
   - **Lien Registration** — register judgment in other federal districts under 28 U.S.C. § 1963.

## Drafting Conventions
- Identify the debtor's assets through Rule 69(a)(2) debtor examinations — interrogatories and depositions to discover hidden or transferred assets.
- For corporate / LLC judgment debtors, pierce-the-corporate-veil and alter-ego theories may extend liability to owners.
- For fraudulent-transfer claims (debtor transferred assets to thwart collection), use state Uniform Voidable Transactions Act (UVTA) or federal § 3304 (FDCPA).
- Post-judgment interest under 28 U.S.C. § 1961 — coupon equivalent to weekly average 1-year T-bill rate (verify current).
- Register judgment in other districts where debtor has assets (28 U.S.C. § 1963).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A money judgment is enforced by a writ of execution, unless the court directs otherwise. The procedure on execution — and in proceedings supplementary to and in aid of judgment or execution — must accord with the procedure of the state where the court is located, but a federal statute governs to the extent it applies." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 69(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Post-judgment enforcement. Pair with asset investigation, debtor examination, and lien-recording across all states where debtor has assets.
