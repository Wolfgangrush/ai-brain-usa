## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — California Civil Code + binding CCPA Regulations.

## Currency Warning
CPRA-amended CCPA in force since 1 January 2023 — adds new categories of "sensitive personal information," correction rights, and limits on retention. Multiple companion state acts (Colorado CPA · Connecticut CTDPA · Virginia VCDPA · Utah UCPA · Texas TDPSA · Oregon OCPA · others) follow similar but not identical disclosure architecture — combined policies need cross-walked language.

## Template Type
Compliance Notice / Privacy Policy.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Effective Date and Last Updated.**
2. **Scope** — entities, products, geographies covered.
3. **Categories of Personal Information collected** (mapped to Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(v)(1)(A)–(K)).
4. **Categories of Sensitive Personal Information** (§ 1798.140(ae) — government IDs, financial account log-in, precise geolocation, racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, union membership, genetic data, biometric ID, health, sex life or sexual orientation, communications content).
5. **Sources of collection** — direct from consumer, automated, third parties.
6. **Business / commercial purposes** for use (§ 1798.140(e)).
7. **Categories of third parties** with whom shared, and whether sale/sharing for cross-context behavioural advertising.
8. **Consumer Rights** — Right to Know · Right to Delete · Right to Correct · Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing · Right to Limit Use of Sensitive PI · Right to Non-Discrimination.
9. **How to exercise rights** — toll-free number (if applicable) + webform + email + designated method for opt-out and limit (incl. universal opt-out signals — GPC).
10. **Retention** — categories with retention periods (§ 1798.100(a)(3)).
11. **Children's Information** — § 1798.120(c) opt-in for under-16.
12. **Financial Incentives** disclosure if any (§ 1798.125(b)).
13. **Authorized Agent** process.
14. **Updates** clause.
15. **Contact information** + designated privacy contact.

## Drafting Conventions
- "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link required on every page where collection occurs.
- "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link required if SPI is used beyond § 1798.121(a) limited purposes.
- Cross-walk this notice with companion state-act notices (Colorado / Virginia / Connecticut) where the business is multi-state.
- Honour Global Privacy Control (GPC) browser signal as opt-out under 11 CCR § 7025(b).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A consumer shall have the right, at any time, to direct a business that sells or shares personal information about the consumer to third parties not to sell or share the consumer's personal information." — Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.120(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Anchor product for compliance counsel serving startups, SaaS vendors, and e-commerce clients. Pair with intake-form mapping to the 11 enumerated PI categories so the policy actually reflects what the client does, not boilerplate.
