## Structural Skeleton
1. **Policy Statement** — zero tolerance for bribery; commitment to compliance with FCPA + UK Bribery Act + applicable foreign anti-bribery laws.
2. **Scope** — all employees, officers, directors; third-party agents, consultants, distributors, joint-venture partners under flow-down provisions.
3. **Prohibited Conduct**:
   - Bribery — direct or indirect offers, payments, gifts, or anything of value to foreign officials to obtain or retain business or improper advantage.
   - Facilitation payments — discouraged or prohibited (post-UK Bribery Act 2010 — strict ban).
   - Books-and-records violations — accurate financial recording of all transactions.
4. **Definitions**:
   - "Foreign Official" — broad: government employees; state-owned enterprise employees; international organization officials; political parties; candidates.
   - "Thing of Value" — broad: cash, gifts, hospitality, travel, employment, charitable contributions, internships, donations.
   - "Anything of Value to a Foreign Official" — includes payments to relatives or third parties on the official's direction.
5. **Permitted Conduct**:
   - Reasonable bona fide expenditures (travel + meals + lodging directly related to the promotion of products or services or to the execution of a contract with a foreign government).
   - Charitable donations subject to due-diligence approval process.
6. **Third-Party Engagement**:
   - Due-diligence questionnaire for all agents/consultants/distributors.
   - Reference checks; background checks; sanctions screening.
   - Contractual flow-down — FCPA compliance representations; right of audit; right to terminate; cooperation.
7. **Gifts and Hospitality** — pre-approval matrix by value, recipient, occasion.
8. **Books and Records (15 U.S.C. § 78m(b)(2))** — accurate; timely; in reasonable detail; reflective of substance; internal accounting controls.
9. **Training** — annual; role-targeted (sales, supply chain, finance).
10. **Reporting** — hotline; non-retaliation; whistleblower protection (SOX § 1514A + Dodd-Frank § 922).
11. **Investigations** — internal investigation protocol; cooperation with DOJ/SEC.
12. **Discipline + Consequences for Non-Compliance.**
13. **Compliance Officer + Reporting Structure.**
14. **Risk Assessment** — periodic; integration with M&A due diligence + post-merger integration.

## Drafting Conventions
- DOJ "Evaluation of Corporate Compliance Programs" benchmarks: risk assessment + policies/procedures + training + investigations/discipline + third-party risk management + M&A diligence + monitoring/auditing + management commitment + reporting + remediation.
- For voluntary self-disclosure, follow Justice Manual § 9-47.120 four prongs.
- Maintain documentation: training records, due-diligence files, gift-and-hospitality logs, hotline reports, investigation files.
- Coordinate with UK Bribery Act Section 7 "failure to prevent" defense — requires "adequate procedures" — overlap with FCPA program.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "It shall be unlawful for any issuer which has a class of securities registered pursuant to section 78l of this title or which is required to file reports under section 78o(d) of this title… to make use of the mails or any means or instrumentality of interstate commerce corruptly in furtherance of an offer, payment, promise to pay, or authorization of the payment of any money, or offer, gift, promise to give, or authorization of the giving of anything of value to — (1) any foreign official…" — 15 U.S.C. § 78dd-1(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Anchor compliance product for any multinational client. Pair with third-party-agent due-diligence questionnaire + gift-and-hospitality pre-approval matrix.
