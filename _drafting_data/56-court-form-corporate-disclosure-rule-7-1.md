# Corporate Disclosure Statement (Rule 7.1)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 7.1](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_7.1)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 7.1.

## Currency Warning
2022 amendments to Rule 7.1 added disclosure of citizenship for diversity / supplemental jurisdiction cases — for parties whose citizenship matters under 28 U.S.C. § 1332(a) or § 1335, identify all parents and members. Filed with first appearance / pleading / motion.

## Template Type
Procedural Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption.**
2. **Title** — "Corporate Disclosure Statement Pursuant to Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 7.1."
3. **Identifying Statement** — "Pursuant to Rule 7.1, undersigned counsel for [Party] certifies that:"
4. **(a)(1) Parent Corporation and Publicly Held Stock** — for any nongovernmental corporate party:
   - "Defendant [Name] is a wholly-owned subsidiary of [Parent], a [State]-incorporated corporation, with no public stockholder owning 10% or more of stock." OR
   - "Defendant [Name] has no parent corporation, and no publicly held corporation owns 10% or more of its stock."
5. **(a)(2) Citizenship Disclosure** (when applicable to § 1332(a) or § 1335 jurisdiction) — disclose citizenship of every individual or entity whose citizenship is attributed to the party.
6. **Supplementation** — "If circumstances change, the undersigned will promptly file a supplemental disclosure statement."
7. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- File at "the party's first appearance, pleading, petition, motion, response, or other request addressed to the court" (Rule 7.1(b)(1)).
- Supplemental filing required if information changes (Rule 7.1(b)(2)).
- For LLCs in diversity cases, citizenship of each member (recursive through tiered structures).
- For partnerships, citizenship of each partner.
- Local Rules in some districts have additional disclosure requirements; check before filing.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A nongovernmental corporate party and a nongovernmental corporation that seeks to intervene must file a statement that: (A) identifies any parent corporation and any publicly held corporation owning 10% or more of its stock; or (B) states that there is no such corporation." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 7.1(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Routine first-filing companion for corporate parties. Critical for judge-recusal screening.
