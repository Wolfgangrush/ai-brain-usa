# Rule 26(f) Report / Joint Discovery Plan

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(f)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_26)
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 16(b)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_16)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rules 16(b) + 26(f).

## Currency Warning
Rule 26(f) conference must occur at least 21 days before scheduling conference under Rule 16(b) or scheduling order is due. Report due 14 days after conference. Court typically issues Rule 16 Scheduling Order based on parties' Rule 26(f) report.

## Template Type
Joint Pretrial Report.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption + Title** — "Joint Rule 26(f) Report and Proposed Discovery Plan."
2. **Conference Date and Participants.**
3. **Brief Summary of Claims and Defenses.**
4. **Jurisdiction and Service** — affirm; identify any unresolved jurisdictional or service issues.
5. **Likely Issues for Resolution** — claims, defenses, relief.
6. **Initial Disclosures Schedule** — date for Rule 26(a)(1) exchange.
7. **Discovery Plan**:
   - Subject matter of discovery.
   - Phased discovery if any (e.g., bifurcation of liability and damages).
   - Date for completion of fact discovery.
   - Date for expert disclosures (opening, rebuttal, depositions).
   - Date for close of all discovery.
   - Limits on number of depositions, interrogatories, RFAs.
   - Protective order plan.
8. **ESI Protocol** — sources of ESI, search methodology, production format, preservation, Rule 502(d) order request.
9. **Privilege Log Protocol** — categorical vs. document-level; timing.
10. **Pretrial Motions Schedule** — Rule 12 motions, summary judgment, motions in limine.
11. **Settlement / ADR** — early-neutral evaluation, mediation, settlement conference timing.
12. **Trial** — anticipated length; jury demand status; pretrial conference date.
13. **Joint Signature Block** — counsel for each party.

## Drafting Conventions
- Stipulate to ESI protocol and protective order early; reduces later motion practice.
- Set discovery cutoffs realistic — Rule 16 modifications require good cause.
- Identify bifurcation issues (liability/damages, class certification/merits).
- Address contention-interrogatory timing (Rule 33(a)(2)).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "[T]he parties must confer as soon as practicable — and in any event at least 21 days before a scheduling conference is to be held or a scheduling order is due under Rule 16(b). … In conferring, the parties must consider the nature and basis of their claims and defenses and the possibilities for promptly settling or resolving the case; make or arrange for the disclosures required by Rule 26(a)(1); discuss any issues about preserving discoverable information; and develop a proposed discovery plan." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(f)(1)–(2).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foundation of case management. Drives Rule 16 Scheduling Order. Locks ESI protocol, protective order, discovery limits — all the levers for the case.
