# Notice of Appeal (FRAP 3)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. App. P. 3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frap/rule_3)
- [Fed. R. App. P. 4](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frap/rule_4)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure 3 + 4.

## Currency Warning
Filing of notice of appeal is JURISDICTIONAL — *Bowles v. Russell*, 551 U.S. 205 (2007). Late filing cannot be cured. Time: 30 days civil (60 if U.S. party); 14 days criminal post-judgment; 60 days appellate brief.

## Template Type
Court Form — Filed with District Court Clerk.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — district court caption + "NOTICE OF APPEAL."
2. **Identification of Party Appealing** — "[Name], plaintiff/defendant in the above-captioned action, hereby appeals…"
3. **Order or Judgment Appealed From** — date of entry; specific order or judgment.
4. **Court of Appeals Designation** — "to the United States Court of Appeals for the [Circuit] Circuit."
5. **Representation** — counsel name + bar admission + address + phone + email.
6. **Filing Fee** — $605 (verify current docket fee at uscourts.gov) paid to district clerk concurrently.
7. **Service** — Federal Rule 25 service on all parties.
8. **Companion** — Civil Appeals Statement (CAS-1) or Local Rule equivalent within 14 days of notice of appeal (varies by circuit).
9. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Be precise about what is appealed (judgment / specific order); ambiguity may waive review.
- Timing: 30 days for civil cases (60 if U.S. or U.S. officer/agency is a party) — Fed. R. App. P. 4(a)(1); 14 days for criminal — Fed. R. App. P. 4(b)(1); tolled by post-judgment motions enumerated in Rule 4(a)(4) (Rule 59/60 motions filed within 28 days).
- File in DISTRICT court, not Court of Appeals (counter-intuitive).
- Pre-judgment appealability: interlocutory appeals under 28 U.S.C. § 1292(a)(1) (injunctions) or § 1292(b) (certified question); pendent appellate jurisdiction limited.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The notice of appeal must: (A) specify the party or parties taking the appeal by naming each one in the caption or body of the notice…; (B) designate the judgment, order, or part thereof being appealed; and (C) name the court to which the appeal is taken." — Fed. R. App. P. 3(c)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Time-critical filing. Calendar 30-day clock at judgment entry. File promptly with filing fee.
