# Proof of Claim (FRBP 3001 / Form 410)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Bankr. P. 3001](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frbp/rule_3001)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Bankruptcy Code §§ 501–502 + FRBP 3001.

## Currency Warning
Bar date: set by court order in Chapter 11 / 13 cases; for Chapter 7, often only required if asset case (notice provided). Filing after bar date may bar payment except for late-filed unsecured (§ 502(b)(9)) treatment depending on circumstances.

## Template Type
Claims-Filing Form (Form 410).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Creditor Information** — name; address; account number.
2. **Debtor Information** — name as filed; case number; chapter.
3. **Amount of Claim** — total amount; secured portion; priority portion; unsecured portion.
4. **Basis for Claim** — money loaned; services performed; goods sold; wages, salaries, commissions; tax; other.
5. **Secured Claim Information** — collateral description; value; basis for valuation; amount of arrearage.
6. **Priority Claim Information** — § 507 priority category.
7. **Supporting Documents** — promissory note; security agreement; invoice; etc. (FRBP 3001(c) requires writing for claims based on writing).
8. **Signature** under penalty of perjury.

## Drafting Conventions
- FRBP 3001(c)(1) requires attachment of writing for claims based on writing; failure undermines presumed-valid status.
- FRBP 3001(c)(2) — for open-end / revolving consumer credit, additional disclosures (account-opening statement, last invoice).
- For secured claims, attach security agreement + UCC-1 financing statement + lien documents.
- For wage / employee claims, identify priority category (§ 507(a)(4) — $15,150 wage cap as of 2024, verify).
- Filing electronically via court's CM/ECF system or paper if not registered.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A proof of claim is a written statement setting forth a creditor's claim. A proof of claim shall conform substantially to the appropriate Official Form." — Fed. R. Bankr. P. 3001(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard creditor-side filing in every bankruptcy. Critical bar-date calendar discipline.
