# Counsel Engagement Letter

## Source URLs
- [ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct r. 1.2 (scope) and r. 1.5 (fees)](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/professional_responsibility/publications/model_rules_of_professional_conduct/)
- State rules of professional conduct (binding).

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — ABA Model Rules 1.2 + 1.5 + 1.16 + state analogs.

## Currency Warning
Some states require contingency-fee agreements in writing (CA, NY, others); some require prior written disclosure of fee arrangement for any new client. Verify state-specific requirements.

## Template Type
Client Contract / Engagement Letter.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Date + Client Name + Salutation.**
2. **Scope of Engagement (Rule 1.2)** — specific matter; included services; excluded services.
3. **Identity of Client** — natural person · entity (identify constituents and clarify entity, not constituents, is the client per Upjohn).
4. **Fees and Billing (Rule 1.5)**:
   - Hourly · flat · contingency · hybrid.
   - Rate(s) for attorneys and paralegals.
   - Billing frequency (monthly).
   - Late fees.
   - Travel and expense policy.
   - Retainer (advance fee) — held in trust account until earned.
5. **Costs and Expenses** — out-of-pocket; advances; reimbursement.
6. **Conflicts and Waivers** — disclosed conflicts; advance waivers of future conflicts (narrow consent required; many state bar opinions limit enforceability).
7. **Confidentiality and Privilege** — Rule 1.6 obligations; cybersecurity reasonable measures; cloud / AI use disclosure.
8. **File Retention** — retention period (typically 7-10 years); return of client property on termination.
9. **Termination** — by either party; effect (fees due; file return).
10. **Dispute Resolution** — mediation, arbitration (if state-law permits in attorney-fee context).
11. **Governing Law.**
12. **Engagement Becomes Effective on Signature** — counter-signature line for client.

## Drafting Conventions
- Be explicit about scope — Rule 1.2 limited representation requires informed consent and reasonableness.
- For contingency fees, state required percentage, calculation of net recovery, and treatment of expenses — written agreement required under Rule 1.5(c).
- For multi-party engagement, address joint representation (Rule 1.7) and waiver.
- Use of generative AI: per ABA Formal Opinion 512 (2024), disclose AI tools used and obtain consent where appropriate.
- For corporate clients, identify the actual client (the entity, not management — *Upjohn*).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The scope of the representation and the basis or rate of the fee and expenses for which the client will be responsible shall be communicated to the client, preferably in writing, before or within a reasonable time after commencing the representation, except when the lawyer will charge a regularly represented client on the same basis or rate." — Model Rule 1.5(b).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical first-engagement document. Sets fee arrangement, scope, conflicts framework. Foundation of client relationship.
