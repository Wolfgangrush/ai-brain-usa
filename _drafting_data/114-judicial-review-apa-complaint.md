# APA Judicial Review Complaint

## Source URLs
- [5 U.S.C. §§ 701–706 (APA judicial review)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/part-I/chapter-7)
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 8](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_8)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Administrative Procedure Act + Article III standing doctrine.

## Currency Warning
Post-*Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo*, 603 U.S. 369 (2024), no *Chevron* deference; courts exercise independent judgment using traditional tools of statutory construction. *Corner Post, Inc. v. Bd. of Governors*, 603 U.S. 799 (2024) — APA 6-year statute of limitations runs from plaintiff's injury, not rule promulgation.

## Template Type
Civil Complaint.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — district court + civil action number + APA challenge.
2. **Nature of Action** — challenge to agency action.
3. **Parties** — plaintiff (with concrete injury for Article III); defendant agency / Secretary in official capacity.
4. **Jurisdiction and Venue** — 28 U.S.C. § 1331; APA § 702 right of review; venue under 28 U.S.C. § 1391(e) (sue United States or officer in district where defendant resides, substantial part of events occurred, plaintiff resides if no real property in action).
5. **Statutory and Regulatory Framework** — agency's organic statute; rules being challenged.
6. **Factual Allegations** — agency action; plaintiff's injury (concrete, particularized, actual or imminent; traceable to action; redressable).
7. **Final Agency Action** — meets *Bennett v. Spear*, 520 U.S. 154 (1997), two-prong test (consummation + legal consequences).
8. **Zone of Interests** — plaintiff's interests within zone protected by relevant statute.
9. **Causes of Action** — for each, identify § 706 ground:
   - **§ 706(2)(A)** — arbitrary and capricious / abuse of discretion.
   - **§ 706(2)(B)** — contrary to constitutional right.
   - **§ 706(2)(C)** — in excess of statutory jurisdiction.
   - **§ 706(2)(D)** — without observance of procedure required by law.
   - **§ 706(1)** — agency action unreasonably delayed / unlawfully withheld.
10. **Prayer for Relief** — set aside agency action; declaratory relief; injunctive relief; remand to agency.

## Drafting Conventions
- Identify final agency action with particularity; non-final actions generally not reviewable.
- Standing analysis: identify concrete injury; traceability to challenged action; redressability.
- Post-*Loper Bright*: argue statutory text + structure + history + traditional construction tools; do not rely on *Chevron* deference framework.
- Arbitrary-and-capricious review under § 706(2)(A) requires "hard look" — *Motor Vehicle Mfrs. Ass'n v. State Farm Mut. Auto. Ins. Co.*, 463 U.S. 29 (1983).
- For pre-enforcement challenges, *Abbott Labs. v. Gardner*, 387 U.S. 136 (1967), ripeness framework.
- Record review limited to administrative record — *Camp v. Pitts*, 411 U.S. 138 (1973); supplementation rare.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The reviewing court shall — (2) hold unlawful and set aside agency action, findings, and conclusions found to be — (A) arbitrary, capricious, an abuse of discretion, or otherwise not in accordance with law; (B) contrary to constitutional right, power, privilege, or immunity; (C) in excess of statutory jurisdiction, authority, or limitations, or short of statutory right; (D) without observance of procedure required by law…" — 5 U.S.C. § 706(2).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foundation of federal administrative-law litigation. Pair with motion for summary judgment on the administrative record.
