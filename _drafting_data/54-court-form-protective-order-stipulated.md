# Stipulated Protective Order

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(c)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_26)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 26(c) + court-approved order.

## Currency Warning
*Pansy v. Borough of Stroudsburg*, 23 F.3d 772 (3d Cir. 1994) — court must independently determine good cause; rubber-stamping insufficient. Press-/public-access challenges to protective orders sometimes succeed. Sealing common-law and First Amendment rights of access apply.

## Template Type
Stipulated Discovery Order.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Stipulated Caption + Title** — "Stipulated Protective Order."
2. **Recitals** — parties recognize discovery may include confidential information; need protections.
3. **Definitions** — "Confidential Information"; "Highly Confidential — Attorneys' Eyes Only" (AEO); "Source Code"; "Confidential — Outside Counsel Only" tier.
4. **Designation Procedure** — written designation by producing party; legend "CONFIDENTIAL" or "HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL — AEO" on each page; deposition designation on the record + 30 days after transcript receipt.
5. **Access Restrictions**:
   - **Confidential** — parties, counsel, experts, court personnel, mediators.
   - **AEO** — outside counsel + retained experts only (with Acknowledgment of Protective Order); no in-house counsel except as agreed.
   - **Source Code** — locked-down inspection room at producing party's office; no copies; auditing logs.
6. **Acknowledgment Form** — Annex A signed by every person receiving Confidential / AEO information.
7. **Challenge Procedure** — meet-and-confer; motion to maintain or de-designate; producing party bears burden of confidentiality.
8. **Use Limitations** — designated material used only for this case.
9. **Inadvertent Disclosure** — Rule 502(d) clawback procedure.
10. **Termination** — return or destruction within 60 days of case closure; certification.
11. **Court Approval** — proposed order signature line.

## Drafting Conventions
- Two-tier (Confidential / AEO) is standard; three-tier (with source code) for IP and competitive cases.
- AEO restrictions must serve genuine competitive concern; courts increasingly scrutinize.
- For sealing motions, *Brown v. Maxwell*, 929 F.3d 41 (2d Cir. 2019), and Ninth Circuit "compelling reasons" standard apply at dispositive-motion stage.
- Include Rule 502(d) clawback to insulate inadvertent privilege disclosure.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The court may, for good cause, issue an order to protect a party or person from annoyance, embarrassment, oppression, or undue burden or expense, including… (G) requiring that a trade secret or other confidential research, development, or commercial information not be revealed or be revealed only in a specified way…" — Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(c)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard early-case filing. Negotiate at Rule 26(f) conference; file as stipulation to avoid contested motion.
