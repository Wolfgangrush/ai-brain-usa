# Motion to Dismiss for Failure to State a Claim (Rule 12(b)(6))

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 12](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_12)
- [*Bell Atl. Corp. v. Twombly*, 550 U.S. 544 (2007)](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/550/544/)
- [*Ashcroft v. Iqbal*, 556 U.S. 662 (2009)](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/556/662/)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 12(b)(6) + Twombly/Iqbal plausibility doctrine.

## Currency Warning
Court accepts well-pleaded factual allegations as true and draws inferences in plaintiff's favor — but legal conclusions are not entitled to that assumption (Iqbal). Conversion to summary judgment if matters outside the pleadings are presented (Rule 12(d)), unless those matters are integral to the complaint or judicially noticeable.

## Template Type
Motion + Memorandum in Support.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — case caption + "DEFENDANT [NAME]'S MOTION TO DISMISS PURSUANT TO FEDERAL RULE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE 12(B)(6)" + accompanying memorandum.
2. **Notice of Motion / Motion Document** (per Local Rule) — relief sought; hearing date.
3. **Memorandum of Law in Support**:
   a. **Introduction** — preview of why the complaint fails.
   b. **Statement of Facts** — allegations of the complaint, taken as true for purposes of the motion.
   c. **Legal Standard** — Rule 12(b)(6); Twombly/Iqbal plausibility; legal conclusions not assumed.
   d. **Argument** — count-by-count attack:
      - Identify each element of the claim.
      - Show absent allegation of one or more elements.
      - Address conclusory allegations vs. factual allegations.
      - Heightened standards where applicable: Rule 9(b) fraud / mistake; PSLRA § 21D for securities.
      - Affirmative defenses appearing on face of complaint (statute of limitations).
   e. **Request for Relief** — dismissal with prejudice (no amendment can cure); alternatively, leave to amend should be denied as futile.
4. **Proposed Order.**
5. **Exhibits** — documents integral to the complaint (contract sued upon); judicially noticeable documents (SEC filings; public records).
6. **Certificate of Service.**
7. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Twombly: claim must be "plausible on its face" — sufficient factual matter to allow reasonable inference of liability.
- Iqbal: two-step approach — (i) identify legal conclusions to disregard; (ii) determine whether remaining factual allegations plausibly entitle plaintiff to relief.
- Rule 9(b) fraud particularity: who, what, when, where, how.
- For securities fraud: PSLRA "strong inference" of scienter under § 21D(b)(2); *Tellabs, Inc. v. Makor Issues & Rights, Ltd.*, 551 U.S. 308 (2007).
- Attach contract sued upon (integral to complaint, no conversion); SEC filings (judicial notice).
- Address standing as Rule 12(b)(1) jurisdictional challenge separately if applicable; consider combined Rule 12(b)(1) + (6) motion.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Every defense to a claim for relief in any pleading must be asserted in the responsive pleading… But a party may assert the following defenses by motion: (6) failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted…" — Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Single most-used dispositive motion in federal practice. Pair with optional Rule 12(b)(1) standing challenge; consider whether to move pre-answer (delays answer) or answer-and-move.
