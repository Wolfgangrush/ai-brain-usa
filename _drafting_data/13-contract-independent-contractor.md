2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Common-law right-of-control + DOL FLSA economic-realities test (2024 final rule) + state ABC tests (CA, MA, NJ, others) + IRS 20-factor test (Rev. Rul. 87-41).

## Currency Warning
DOL 2024 Final Rule (effective 11 March 2024) replaced 2021 rule with traditional totality-of-the-circumstances economic-realities test (six factors, none dispositive). California's ABC test (AB 5 / AB 2257) is far stricter; misclassification penalties severe. Track *Atlanta Opera, Inc.*, 372 N.L.R.B. No. 95 (June 13, 2023), NLRB independent-contractor standard.

## Template Type
Independent Contractor Services Contract.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Effective Date + Recitals.**
2. **Services** — Scope (specific deliverables, not open-ended); Contractor controls means, methods, schedule (consistent with independent-contractor status).
3. **Fees** — fixed-fee · project-based · hourly · milestone-based; expenses (typically Contractor bears its own).
4. **Term and Termination** — definite term or project-completion; termination for convenience and for cause.
5. **Independent-Contractor Status** — Contractor not an employee; not entitled to employee benefits; responsible for own taxes (Form 1099-NEC); provides own tools / equipment / workspace (where possible).
6. **No Authority to Bind** — Contractor cannot bind Company contractually or in dealings with third parties.
7. **Confidentiality** — Company Confidential Information protections; survival post-termination.
8. **Intellectual Property** — Work-for-hire to extent permitted under 17 U.S.C. § 101; otherwise assignment of all right, title, and interest; moral-rights waiver where permitted; pre-existing IP carve-out (Schedule).
9. **Representations and Warranties** — authority · non-infringement · no conflicting obligation · compliance with law.
10. **Indemnification** — Contractor indemnifies for breach + IP infringement + tax/withholding shortfall flowing from misclassification claim.
11. **Insurance** — Contractor's own GL · professional · workers comp (where applicable).
12. **Subcontracting** — only with prior written consent of Company.
13. **Restrictive Covenants** — non-solicitation of Company employees + customers (limited); non-compete to extent enforceable.
14. **Limitation of Liability** — exclude consequential; cap typically fees paid.
15. **Governing Law · Forum · Notices · Assignment · Counterparts · Electronic Signature.**

## Drafting Conventions
- The contract terms alone do NOT determine classification — the actual working relationship governs (IRS, DOL, state agencies, and courts).
- Avoid: providing employee-style benefits, performance reviews, mandatory training similar to employee training, integration into the workforce hierarchy, exclusive engagement provisions.
- Require Contractor's representation that it operates as an independent business (LLC / corporation / sole proprietorship), maintains its own insurance, and has other clients.
- For California-resident contractors providing services to California businesses, ABC test gating is determinative; document each prong (no control · outside usual course · independent trade).
- For IP, work-for-hire only applies to specifically enumerated categories under § 101 plus written-instrument requirement — pair with assignment fallback in every contract.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A worker is an employee unless the hiring entity demonstrates that all of the following conditions are satisfied: (A) The person is free from the control and direction of the hiring entity… (B) The person performs work that is outside the usual course of the hiring entity's business; and (C) The person is customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business…" — Cal. Lab. Code § 2775(b)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Highest classification-risk contract type. Always pair with a memorandum to client on misclassification risk and the specific tests applicable. Decline engagements where facts will not satisfy applicable tests.
