## Currency Warning
Four-level review: (1) initial · (2) reconsideration · (3) ALJ hearing · (4) Appeals Council. Court appeal to federal district court under 42 U.S.C. § 405(g). 60-day deadline at each level. SSA listings of impairments revised periodically; verify current Listing of Impairments (Pub. No. 64-039).

## Template Type
Administrative Appeal Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption + Identification** — SSN; claimant name; address; type of claim (Title II disability / Title XVI SSI).
2. **Statement of Appeal** — date of decision being appealed; reason for appeal.
3. **Request Form**:
   - **Form SSA-561** — Request for Reconsideration.
   - **Form HA-501** — Request for Hearing by ALJ.
   - **Form HA-520** — Request for Review by Appeals Council.
4. **Disability Report Updates** — current medical conditions, treatments, providers.
5. **Pre-Hearing Memorandum / Brief** — analysis of five-step sequential evaluation:
   - **Step 1.** Substantial gainful activity (SGA).
   - **Step 2.** Severe medically determinable impairment.
   - **Step 3.** Listing-level impairment (presumed disabled).
   - **Step 4.** Past relevant work — RFC vs. past work demands.
   - **Step 5.** Other work in national economy — RFC vs. vocational profile.
6. **Medical Evidence Submissions** — treatment records; medical-source statements; consulting examiner reports.
7. **Vocational Expert Cross-Examination Outline** for hearing.

## Drafting Conventions
- File at each level within 60 days of adverse decision (Sunday/holiday extends).
- For ALJ hearing, request via Form HA-501 with grounds for appeal.
- Pre-hearing brief: 5-step framework with medical-evidence and vocational analysis.
- *Biestek v. Berryhill*, 587 U.S. 97 (2019), addresses vocational expert testimony evidentiary weight.
- For Appeals Council, identify ALJ error of law or new and material evidence.
- For district court review under § 405(g), substantial-evidence standard; reviewing record evidence developed at agency level.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The Commissioner of Social Security is directed to make findings of fact, and decisions as to the rights of any individual applying for a payment under this subchapter. Any such decision by the Commissioner of Social Security which involves a determination of disability… which is in whole or in part unfavorable to such individual shall contain a statement of the case, in understandable language, setting forth a discussion of the evidence, and stating the Commissioner's determination and the reason or reasons upon which it is based." — 42 U.S.C. § 405(b)(1).
