## Authority Tier
Primary — DGCL + federal securities law + HSR.

## Currency Warning
HSR thresholds revised annually; verify current size-of-transaction. § 251(h) two-step medium-form merger for back-end without stockholder vote when threshold ownership reached via tender offer.

## Template Type
M&A Transaction Contract — Merger.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Recitals + Effective Date.**
2. **Definitions** — Merger Sub; Effective Time; Material Adverse Effect; Company Material Adverse Effect; Knowledge.
3. **The Merger**:
   - Merger Sub merges with and into Target (reverse triangular for tax + liability reasons).
   - Surviving corporation continues as wholly-owned subsidiary.
   - Cancellation of Target stock and conversion to merger consideration.
4. **Merger Consideration** — cash / stock / mix; treatment of equity awards (vesting acceleration; cashout; rollover); fractional shares.
5. **Closing**:
   - Conditions to closing.
   - Closing date and place.
   - Closing deliveries.
6. **Representations and Warranties**:
   - **Target** — organization; authorization; capitalization; subsidiaries; financial statements; SEC filings (public); absence of changes; litigation; tax; employees; benefits; material contracts; IP; privacy / cyber; environmental; real property; permits; compliance with law (FCPA / OFAC / antitrust); related-party transactions; brokers; opinion of financial advisor.
   - **Acquiror** — organization; authorization; financing; sufficient capital.
7. **Covenants between Signing and Closing**:
   - Operate Target in ordinary course.
   - Negative covenants (no dividend; no new indebtedness; no material contracts).
   - Stockholder approval — proxy statement / Schedule 14A.
   - Regulatory filings (HSR; CFIUS; foreign merger control).
   - Anti-trust / state-foreign-investment.
   - No-shop / fiduciary-out / matching rights / superior-proposal definition.
   - "Don't ask, don't waive" standstills addressed.
8. **Conditions Precedent** — accuracy of reps (bring-down); performance of covenants; no MAE; regulatory approvals; stockholder vote; no injunction.
9. **Termination** — drop-dead date; for-cause; for-no-vote; for-superior-proposal.
10. **Termination Fees and Reverse Termination Fees** — payable on superior proposal / financing failure / regulatory failure.
11. **Specific Performance and Damages** — specific performance available; damages cap; sole remedy clauses.
12. **Indemnification** — public-company target: typically no surviving indemnification by stockholders; private target: indemnification escrow + R&W insurance.
13. **D&O Insurance Tail / Indemnification of Target Directors and Officers** — 6-year tail policy.
14. **Miscellaneous.**

## Drafting Conventions
- For public-target, fiduciary-out + no-shop / superior-proposal mechanics — *Omnicare* legacy issues + post-*Williams* framework.
- For private-target, indemnification + escrow + R&W insurance + sandbagging clause Delaware default favors buyer.
- § 251(h) eligibility: Target stock listed on national exchange; tender offer; receipt of majority; back-end merger without vote.
- Reverse termination fees: negotiated; typically 1-3% of equity value; tied to financing failure or antitrust failure.
- *Atl. Marine* forum-selection enforceable in M&A litigation; specify Court of Chancery as exclusive.
