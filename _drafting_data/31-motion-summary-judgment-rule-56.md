# Motion for Summary Judgment (Rule 56)

- [*Celotex Corp. v. Catrett*, 477 U.S. 317 (1986)](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/477/317/)
- [*Anderson v. Liberty Lobby, Inc.*, 477 U.S. 242 (1986)](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/477/242/)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 56 + Celotex/Anderson/Matsushita summary-judgment trilogy.

## Currency Warning
Court grants summary judgment "if the movant shows that there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." Materials must be admissible in substance (Rule 56(c)(2)); declarations on personal knowledge (Rule 56(c)(4)).

## Template Type
Motion + Memorandum + Statement of Material Facts + Declaration(s) + Exhibits.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Notice of Motion** — relief sought; hearing date per Local Rule.
2. **Memorandum of Law in Support**:
   a. **Introduction** — preview of why no genuine issue exists.
   b. **Statement of Undisputed Material Facts** — numbered paragraphs each citing record evidence (deposition, document, declaration); per Local Rule (e.g., S.D.N.Y. Local Civil Rule 56.1).
   c. **Legal Standard** — Rule 56 + Celotex burden-shifting.
   d. **Argument** — claim-by-claim:
      - Identify each element of the claim/defense.
      - Identify undisputed facts foreclosing essential element OR establishing affirmative defense.
      - Show that opposing party cannot produce admissible evidence creating a genuine issue.
3. **Statement of Material Facts** (separate document per Local Rule) — numbered paragraphs.
4. **Declarations** under 28 U.S.C. § 1746 — personal knowledge; foundation for documents; setting the evidentiary stage.
5. **Exhibits** — deposition excerpts (with full deposition lodged); authenticated documents.
6. **Proposed Order.**
7. **Certificate of Service.**
8. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Movant's initial burden under *Celotex*: identify portions of the record showing absence of genuine issue OR show opposing party cannot meet its trial burden.
- Once shouldered, burden shifts to opposing party to designate specific facts showing genuine issue (Rule 56(c)(1)(A)).
- "Genuine" issue: reasonable jury could return verdict for non-movant (Anderson).
- "Material" fact: would affect outcome under governing substantive law.
- *Matsushita Elec. Indus. Co. v. Zenith Radio Corp.*, 475 U.S. 574 (1986): even where motive/intent at issue, opposing party must produce more than scintilla.
- Affidavits / declarations from non-experts must be based on personal knowledge; expert opinions admissible only if Rule 702 standards met.
- For partial summary judgment, identify each issue separately (Rule 56(a)).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A party may move for summary judgment, identifying each claim or defense — or the part of each claim or defense — on which summary judgment is sought. The court shall grant summary judgment if the movant shows that there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 56(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Dispositive motion most cases trail toward. Timing per Rule 56(b): may be filed any time until 30 days after close of discovery, unless court orders otherwise. Local Rules typically set summary-judgment briefing schedule.
