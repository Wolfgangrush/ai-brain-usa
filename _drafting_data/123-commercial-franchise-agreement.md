## Currency Warning
FTC Franchise Rule revision in progress; verify current 23-Item Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD) requirements. State registration / disclosure laws supplement federal floor. 14-day prospectus / receipt acknowledgment requirement before signing.

## Template Type
Long-Form Franchise Contract.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Recitals + Term.**
2. **Grant of Franchise** — name; system; territory.
3. **Term and Renewal** — initial term; renewal conditions; transfer rights.
4. **Initial Fees and Royalties** — initial franchise fee; royalty (% of gross sales); marketing-fund contribution; technology fees.
5. **System Standards** — operations manual incorporated by reference; quality standards; brand-protection requirements.
6. **Training and Support** — initial training; ongoing operations support.
7. **Trademark and Trade Dress License** — quality control; termination revoking license.
8. **Confidentiality and Trade Secrets** — proprietary information; non-disclosure during and post-termination.
9. **Non-Compete and Non-Solicit** — during term + post-termination; subject to state law enforceability.
10. **Insurance** — minimum coverage; franchisor as additional insured.
11. **Site Selection / Construction** — franchisor approval rights; deadlines.
12. **Termination**:
   - For cause (default + cure).
   - State franchise relationship law overlay (CA Franchise Investment Law; NJ Franchise Practices Act; PA Franchise Termination Law; others) requiring good cause + notice.
13. **Transfer and Right of First Refusal.**
14. **Dispute Resolution + Governing Law + Forum** — note state-law restrictions on forum selection / arbitration (CA Franchise Bill of Rights).
15. **FDD Receipt Acknowledgment** — signed and dated by franchisee.

## Drafting Conventions
- 14-day cooling-off period: FDD must be delivered 14 calendar days before franchisee signs binding agreement or pays consideration.
- State registration: 13 states require registration before offering or selling franchise; verify state-by-state.
- State franchise relationship laws (often anti-termination, anti-encroachment) may override contractual termination rights.
- Compliance with State Franchise Acts may require state-specific addenda to FDD.
- Item 7 estimated initial investment must be substantiated; Item 19 financial performance representations must comply with FTC Rule.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "It is an unfair or deceptive act or practice in violation of Section 5 of the Federal Trade Commission Act… for any franchisor or franchise broker to fail to furnish a prospective franchisee with a copy of the franchisor's current disclosure document, as described in subparts C and D of this part, at least 14 calendar-days before the prospective franchisee signs a binding agreement with, or makes any payment to, the franchisor or an affiliate in connection with the proposed franchise sale." — 16 C.F.R. § 436.2(a).
