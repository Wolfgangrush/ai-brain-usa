# Requests for Admission (Rule 36)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 36](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_36)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 36.

## Currency Warning
Rule 36(a)(3): matter deemed admitted if no response within 30 days (or as court orders / parties stipulate). Withdrawal or amendment of admission permitted under Rule 36(b) only if it would promote presentation of the merits AND opposing party would not be prejudiced.

## Template Type
Discovery Request Set.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — "PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO DEFENDANT."
2. **Definitions and Instructions** — Local Rule definitions; obligation to admit, deny, or state with particularity why party cannot truthfully admit or deny (Rule 36(a)(4)).
3. **Requests** — discrete, simple statements:
   - **Authentication** — "Admit that Exhibit A is a true and correct copy of the contract executed by Defendant on [date]."
   - **Foundational facts** — "Admit that Defendant employed John Doe between [date] and [date]."
   - **Application of law to facts** — "Admit that Defendant did not provide written notice to Plaintiff under Section [X] of the Contract."
4. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Each request should be narrow and unambiguous; compound requests invite denial.
- Authentication RFAs are particularly cost-effective — eliminate need for foundation testimony at trial.
- For each request: admit, deny, state inability after reasonable inquiry. Qualifications or partial denials must specify what is admitted and denied (Rule 36(a)(4)).
- Rule 37(c)(2): party may move for sanctions if opposing party fails to admit and proof at trial requires further expense.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A matter is admitted unless, within 30 days after being served, the party to whom the request is directed serves on the requesting party a written answer or objection addressed to the matter and signed by the party or its attorney." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 36(a)(3).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Most underused discovery tool. Front-load authentication and foundational facts to streamline trial.
