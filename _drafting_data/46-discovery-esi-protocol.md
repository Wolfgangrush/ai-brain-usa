## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rules 26(f), 34(b), 37(e).

## Currency Warning
Rule 37(e) — failure to preserve ESI that should have been preserved may result in measures no greater than necessary to cure prejudice OR (with intent to deprive) adverse inference instructions / dismissal / default judgment. Reasonable steps to preserve are the threshold inquiry.

## Template Type
Stipulated Discovery Order / Protocol.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties' Stipulation and Joint Proposed Order.**
2. **Definitions** — Electronically Stored Information; Custodian; Production Format; Native Files; Load File; Privilege Log; Source Code.
3. **Preservation** — categories of data subject to litigation hold; preservation methodology; ephemeral data treatment (Slack messages, chat platforms).
4. **Sources of ESI** — custodial vs. non-custodial; databases; cloud services; backup tapes (cost-shift if accessed); personal devices (BYOD); third-party providers.
5. **Search Methodology** — search terms (negotiated list); custodians (negotiated list); date range; sampling and validation; predictive coding / TAR election.
6. **Production Format** — generally:
   - **TIFF/PDF** images with searchable text + load file (DAT + OPT/LFP).
   - **Native** for Excel, PowerPoint, video, audio, source code.
   - **Email** with parent-child relationship preserved.
   - **Metadata fields** to be produced (Bates, custodian, sent date, received date, file extension, parent ID, attachment counts).
7. **Privilege and Work-Product** — Rule 502(d) order — non-waiver across this and any other proceeding; clawback procedure.
8. **Confidentiality Designations** — pair with Protective Order.
9. **Cost Allocation** — default is producing party bears cost; cost-shifting for inaccessible sources (e.g., backup tapes) per *Zubulake v. UBS Warburg LLC*, 217 F.R.D. 309 (S.D.N.Y. 2003) framework.
10. **Dispute Resolution** — meet-and-confer; informal letter motion; court intervention.

## Drafting Conventions
- Address modern data sources: Slack, Teams, ephemeral messaging, mobile devices, cloud storage, social media.
- Rule 502(d) order to insulate inadvertent disclosure from waiver — critical for large ESI productions.
- TAR (Technology Assisted Review) elections: party producing may elect TAR; opposing party may demand transparency about training set.
- For Source Code, separate protocol — typically inspection at counsel's office or producing party's office, no copies.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "If electronically stored information that should have been preserved in the anticipation or conduct of litigation is lost because a party failed to take reasonable steps to preserve it, and it cannot be restored or replaced through additional discovery, the court: (1) upon finding prejudice to another party from loss of the information, may order measures no greater than necessary to cure the prejudice; or (2) only upon finding that the party acted with the intent to deprive another party of the information's use in the litigation may: (A) presume that the lost information was unfavorable to the party; (B) instruct the jury that it may or must presume the information was unfavorable to the party; or (C) dismiss the action or enter a default judgment." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 37(e).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foundation of modern federal discovery. Negotiate ESI protocol at Rule 26(f) conference; lock in by stipulated order.
