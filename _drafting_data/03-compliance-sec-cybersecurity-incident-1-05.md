# SEC Form 8-K Item 1.05 — Cybersecurity Incident Disclosure

## Source URLs
- [SEC Final Rule — Cybersecurity Risk Management, Strategy, Governance, and Incident Disclosure (Release No. 33-11216)](https://www.sec.gov/files/rules/final/2023/33-11216.pdf)
- [Form 8-K — General Instructions](https://www.sec.gov/files/form8-k.pdf)
- [17 C.F.R. § 229.106 (Reg S-K Item 106)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-17/section-229.106)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — SEC Final Rule + Exchange Act § 13(a) + Form 8-K General Instruction B.1.

## Currency Warning
Item 1.05 effective 18 December 2023 (smaller reporting companies effective 15 June 2024). The 4-business-day clock starts from the **materiality determination**, not from incident discovery. Delay permitted only if U.S. Attorney General determines disclosure would pose substantial risk to national security or public safety (delegated to FBI under Department of Justice procedures).

## Template Type
Periodic Filing / Statutory Disclosure.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Item 1.05 Heading** — "Material Cybersecurity Incidents."
2. **Incident description** — material aspects of:
   - The nature, scope, and timing of the incident.
   - The material impact or reasonably likely material impact, including on financial condition and results of operations.
3. **Specific information NOT required by the rule** — technical specifics of vulnerability, response, remediation plan that would impede investigation.
4. **Amendment trigger** — if information was not determined or was unavailable at original filing, amend within 4 business days after determination.
5. **Forward-looking statements disclaimer** under PSLRA.
6. **Signature block** — officer signature pursuant to Form 8-K signature requirements.

## Drafting Conventions
- Item 1.05 is NOT triggered by discovery alone; the rule turns on materiality determination, which must be made "without unreasonable delay" after discovery.
- A "material" incident is one a reasonable investor would consider important to investment decision (*TSC Industries, Inc. v. Northway, Inc.*, 426 U.S. 438 (1976)).
- Series-of-related-incidents aggregated into one materiality analysis.
- Form 8-K Item 1.05 disclosures DO NOT satisfy state breach-notification obligations; they run in parallel.
- Companion annual disclosures under Reg S-K Item 106 (cybersecurity risk management, strategy, governance) live in the 10-K.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "If the registrant experiences a cybersecurity incident that is determined by the registrant to be material, describe the material aspects of the nature, scope, and timing of the incident, and the material impact or reasonably likely material impact on the registrant, including its financial condition and results of operations." — Form 8-K, Item 1.05(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Engagement shape: incident-response counsel + securities counsel coordination on Day 1. Materiality memo within 72 hours. 8-K drafting parallel to forensic investigation. Coordinate with state breach notifications, HIPAA OCR notification (if applicable), CISA voluntary reporting (and mandatory under CIRCIA when rules effective).
