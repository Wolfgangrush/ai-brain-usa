# Chapter 13 Bankruptcy Petition (Wage-Earner Repayment Plan)

## Source URLs
- [11 U.S.C. ch. 13](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/11/chapter-13)
- [Form 101 (Voluntary Petition — Individual)](https://www.uscourts.gov/sites/default/files/form_b_101.pdf)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Bankruptcy Code Chapter 13.

## Currency Warning
Chapter 13 available only to individuals with regular income and debts within statutory limits — verify current debt limits (periodically adjusted). 3-5 year plan duration based on income test relative to state median.

## Template Type
Petition + Plan Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Voluntary Petition (Form 101).**
2. **Schedules A-J + Statement of Financial Affairs.**
3. **Chapter 13 Plan** (Form 113) — debtor's proposed repayment plan.
4. **Credit Counseling Certificate.**
5. **Means Test** (Form 122C-1, C-2) — disposable income calculation.
6. **Codebtor List + Codebtor Stay (§ 1301).**
7. **Mailing Matrix.**
8. **Filing Fee** ($313 — verify current).

## Plan Elements (per § 1322)
- Treatment of secured claims — cramdown / lien strip / mortgage cure / arrears.
- Treatment of priority claims — must be paid in full.
- Treatment of general unsecured claims — disposable income devoted (best-efforts test).
- Duration — 3 years (below-median income) or 5 years (above-median income); maximum 5 years.
- 1325(a)(5) — secured-creditor treatment requires: full payment, surrender, or value-of-collateral with present value.

## Drafting Conventions
- Pre-filing credit counseling required (§ 109(h)).
- Calculate disposable income carefully — drives unsecured-creditor recovery.
- For mortgage cure under § 1322(b)(5), Statement of Cure required at filing.
- Codebtor stay extends to consumer debt codebtors (§ 1301).
- Confirmation hearing typically 30-45 days after § 341(a) meeting.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "Only an individual with regular income that owes, on the date of the filing of the petition, noncontingent, liquidated, unsecured debts of less than $400,000 and noncontingent, liquidated, secured debts of less than $1,257,850 [verify current limits], or an individual with regular income and such individual's spouse… may be a debtor under chapter 13 of this title." — 11 U.S.C. § 109(e) (approximate; verify current debt limits).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard consumer-debt reorganization path for wage-earners with regular income.
