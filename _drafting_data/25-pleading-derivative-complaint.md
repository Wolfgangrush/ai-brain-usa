## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 23.1 + state-of-incorporation corporate law (DGCL for Delaware corporations).

## Currency Warning
Demand-futility analysis post-*United Food & Commercial Workers Union v. Zuckerberg*, 262 A.3d 1034 (Del. 2021), uses a three-part test (replaced Rales/Aronson): for each director, (i) received a material personal benefit from the alleged misconduct; (ii) faces a substantial likelihood of liability on any of the claims that are subject of the litigation demand; (iii) lacks independence from someone who received a material personal benefit or faces substantial likelihood of liability. Demand futile if majority of board has any of these defects.

## Template Type
Pleading — Derivative Action by Stockholder on Behalf of Corporation.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — "[Plaintiff], derivatively on behalf of [Company], Plaintiff, v. [Director defendants and officers] · Defendants, and [Company] · Nominal Defendant. VERIFIED STOCKHOLDER DERIVATIVE COMPLAINT."
2. **Nature of the Action.**
3. **Jurisdiction and Venue** — supplemental + diversity analysis; Delaware Court of Chancery exclusively for fiduciary-duty derivative claims for Delaware corporations.
4. **Parties** — Plaintiff stockholder (continuous-ownership allegations); Director / Officer Defendants; Nominal Defendant Company.
5. **Standing Allegations under Rule 23.1(b)(1)** — Plaintiff was a stockholder at the time of the transaction complained of and continuously since.
6. **Demand Futility under Rule 23.1(b)(3)** — particularized facts demonstrating Zuckerberg three-prong futility for majority of board.
7. **Section 220 Books-and-Records Predicate** (best practice) — if Plaintiff conducted a § 220 demand and received documents, allege the demand process and resulting basis for particularized pleading.
8. **Factual Allegations** — board / officer conduct constituting fiduciary breach (loyalty / care / good faith / *Caremark* oversight).
9. **Counts** — Breach of Fiduciary Duty (Loyalty / Care / Good Faith) · Aiding and Abetting · Unjust Enrichment · Corporate Waste · Insider Trading (Brophy claim) where applicable.
10. **Prayer for Relief** — damages payable to Company · injunctive relief · disgorgement · attorney's fees and costs from corporate-benefit doctrine.
11. **Verification** — Rule 23.1(b)(4): Plaintiff verifies under oath.
12. **Jury Demand** — derivative claims typically equitable; jury demand consider Court of Chancery vs. federal court vs. state Superior Court.

## Drafting Conventions
- § 220 books-and-records first — *King v. VeriFone Holdings, Inc.*, 12 A.3d 1140 (Del. 2011), routes plaintiff to § 220 before filing.
- Continuous-ownership requirement (Rule 23.1 + state law) is strictly enforced.
- For demand-required cases: allege demand made, board's response, and rejection bad faith / not informed.
- Settlement requires court approval after notice to all stockholders (Rule 23.1(c)).
- For Caremark oversight claims, allege specific red flags ignored — *Marchand v. Barnhill*, 212 A.3d 805 (Del. 2019); *In re Boeing Co. Derivative Litig.*, 2021 WL 4059934 (Del. Ch.).

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "The complaint must be verified and must: (1) allege that the plaintiff was a shareholder or member at the time of the transaction complained of, or that the plaintiff's share or membership later devolved on it by operation of law; (2) allege that the action is not a collusive one to confer jurisdiction… and (3) state with particularity: (A) any effort by the plaintiff to obtain the desired action from the directors or comparable authority…; and (B) the reasons for not obtaining the action or not making the effort." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 23.1(b).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
High-stakes plaintiff-side litigation. Always begin with § 220 demand and document inspection. For nominal-Delaware-corporation defendants, Court of Chancery is the standard forum.
