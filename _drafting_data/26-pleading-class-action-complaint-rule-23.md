# Class Action Complaint (Rule 23)

2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 23 + CAFA.

## Currency Warning
*TransUnion LLC v. Ramirez*, 594 U.S. 413 (2021) — class members must have Article III standing for damages; *Olean Wholesale Grocery Coop. v. Bumble Bee Foods LLC*, 31 F.4th 651 (9th Cir. 2022) — addresses uninjured class members. Track FRCP Advisory Committee discussion of MDL practice and class-action settlement reforms.

## Template Type
Pleading — Class Representative Initial Filing.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — "[Named Plaintiff], on behalf of [himself / herself / themselves] and all others similarly situated, Plaintiff, v. [Defendant], Defendant. CLASS ACTION COMPLAINT."
2. **Nature of the Action.**
3. **Jurisdiction and Venue** — CAFA jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1332(d) (minimal diversity + $5M aggregate + 100+ class members) or federal-question + supplemental.
4. **Parties** — Named Plaintiff (with standing-anchoring injury) + Defendant.
5. **Factual Allegations** — common conduct affecting the class.
6. **Class Allegations** — class definition (precise, ascertainable); proposed subclasses; Rule 23(a) factors:
   - **Numerosity** — joinder impracticable.
   - **Commonality** — questions of law or fact common to the class.
   - **Typicality** — Named Plaintiff's claims typical.
   - **Adequacy** — Named Plaintiff and counsel will fairly and adequately represent.
   Plus Rule 23(b)(1), (2), or (3) basis:
   - **(b)(1)** — risk of inconsistent or impairing adjudications.
   - **(b)(2)** — injunctive / declaratory relief appropriate respecting class as a whole.
   - **(b)(3)** — common questions predominate + class action superior.
7. **Counts** — each cause of action; for (b)(3) classes, common-issue predominance per count.
8. **Prayer for Relief** — class certification · damages · injunctive / declaratory relief · attorney's fees and costs · class notice costs.
9. **Jury Demand.**
10. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Class definition: avoid fail-safe (defining class by reference to merits); ascertainable from objective records.
- Standing post-*TransUnion*: every class member seeking damages must have suffered a concrete injury — plead class-wide injury basis.
- Rule 23(b)(3) predominance: identify common questions and how they will be answered with class-wide proof.
- Pre-certification: file as class action complaint; defer motion for class certification until discovery permits.
- *Wal-Mart Stores, Inc. v. Dukes*, 564 U.S. 338 (2011), heightened commonality requirements.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "One or more members of a class may sue or be sued as representative parties on behalf of all members only if: (1) the class is so numerous that joinder of all members is impracticable; (2) there are questions of law or fact common to the class; (3) the claims or defenses of the representative parties are typical of the claims or defenses of the class; and (4) the representative parties will fairly and adequately protect the interests of the class." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 23(a).
