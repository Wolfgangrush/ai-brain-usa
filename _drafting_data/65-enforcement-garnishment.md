## Authority Tier
Primary — Rule 69 + state garnishment procedure + CCPA Title III wage limits.

## Currency Warning
Federal CCPA caps wage garnishment at lesser of (a) 25% of disposable earnings, or (b) amount by which disposable earnings exceed 30 × federal minimum wage (15 U.S.C. § 1673). States may impose lower caps. Child support / spousal support exceptions allow higher percentages.

## Template Type
Court Form — Garnishment of Earnings or Third-Party-Held Property.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — federal court + "WRIT OF GARNISHMENT — [WAGE / BANK / NONWAGE]."
2. **Identification of Garnishee** — third party holding property of judgment debtor (employer · bank · debtor's debtor).
3. **Amount of Judgment + Post-Judgment Interest.**
4. **Direction to Garnishee** — withhold and remit to court / counsel up to amount owed; calculate per CCPA / state wage caps for wage garnishments.
5. **Garnishee Answer / Disclosure** — required period (typically 7-30 days depending on jurisdiction) — identify property held / wages owed; assert exemptions claimed by debtor.
6. **Notice to Judgment Debtor** — claim of exemption period (typically 14-30 days).
7. **Service** — on garnishee (often statutorily prescribed); on debtor (notice + claim form).
8. **Continuing Lien** — for wage garnishments, often continuing each pay period until satisfied.

## Drafting Conventions
- CCPA Title III caps wage garnishment at the lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or 30 × federal minimum wage threshold.
- For child support / alimony, 50-65% caps apply under § 1673(b).
- Bank-account garnishment typically reaches funds present at moment of service; subsequent deposits require new writ.
- Some states require judgment creditor to first attempt to collect from debtor before issuing wage garnishment.
- Federal benefits (Social Security, VA, federal retirement) are generally exempt from garnishment under 42 U.S.C. § 407 and similar provisions.
- For government as creditor (FDCPA), § 3205 procedure controls.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "[T]he maximum part of the aggregate disposable earnings of an individual for any workweek which is subjected to garnishment may not exceed — (1) 25 per centum of his disposable earnings for that week, or (2) the amount by which his disposable earnings for that week exceed thirty times the Federal minimum hourly wage…" — 15 U.S.C. § 1673(a).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Post-judgment recovery instrument. Pair with garnishee disclosure form and continuing lien for wage garnishments.
