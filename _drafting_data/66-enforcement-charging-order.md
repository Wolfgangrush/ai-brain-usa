# Charging Order Against LLC / Partnership Interest

## Source URLs
- [Delaware LLC Act § 18-703 (charging order)](https://delcode.delaware.gov/title6/c018/sc07/index.html)
- [Uniform Partnership Act § 504; Revised Uniform Limited Liability Company Act § 503](https://www.uniformlaws.org)
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 69](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_69)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — state LLC / partnership acts + FRCP Rule 69(a) (state procedure governs).

## Currency Warning
Charging order is the EXCLUSIVE remedy of a judgment creditor against a debtor-member's LLC interest in most states (Delaware, Nevada, Wyoming, Texas, and most adopters of Revised ULLCA). Single-member LLC analysis differs in some states (FTC v. Olmstead, 528 F.3d 1284 (11th Cir. 2008), held charging-order-only protection does not apply to single-member LLC under Florida law).

## Template Type
Post-Judgment Motion + Order.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption + Motion** — "Motion for Charging Order Pursuant to [State LLC Act § X-XXX]."
2. **Procedural Background** — judgment entered; debtor's identified LLC/partnership interest.
3. **Statement of Law** — charging-order procedure under applicable state act.
4. **Identification of LLC / Partnership** — entity name; jurisdiction; debtor's interest.
5. **Relief Requested** — order charging the debtor's transferable interest with payment of the unsatisfied amount of the judgment + post-judgment interest + costs.
6. **Effect of Charging Order** — judgment creditor receives debtor's distributions; no management rights; no right to compel distributions.
7. **Service** — on debtor + on the LLC / partnership.
8. **Proposed Order.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Charging order is a lien on the debtor's transferable interest; creditor steps into debtor's economic rights only.
- No foreclosure of interest in most charging-order-only jurisdictions (Delaware, Nevada, Wyoming, Texas).
- For multi-member LLC with non-distributing income, creditor may face "starvation strategy" — no distributions; income passes through but tax burden not necessarily extends.
- Some states permit foreclosure on charging-order interest (Florida — Olmstead reach for single-member; varies for multi-member).
- For receivership remedy, separate motion to appoint receiver typically required.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "On application by a judgment creditor of a member or of a member's assignee, a court having jurisdiction may charge the limited liability company interest of the judgment debtor to satisfy the judgment. To the extent so charged, the judgment creditor has only the right to receive any distribution or distributions to which the judgment debtor would otherwise have been entitled in respect of such limited liability company interest." — 6 Del. C. § 18-703(a).
