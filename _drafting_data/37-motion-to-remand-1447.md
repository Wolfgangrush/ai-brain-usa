# Motion to Remand (28 U.S.C. § 1447)

## Source URLs
- [28 U.S.C. § 1447](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1447)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — 28 U.S.C. § 1447.

## Currency Warning
For procedural defects (improper removal not amounting to lack of subject-matter jurisdiction), motion must be filed within 30 days of removal (§ 1447(c)). For lack of subject-matter jurisdiction, motion may be filed any time before final judgment, and court must remand sua sponte if no jurisdiction. Order remanding for lack of subject-matter jurisdiction or procedural defect generally not reviewable on appeal (§ 1447(d)) except for civil-rights and federal-officer removal cases.

## Template Type
Procedural Motion — Plaintiff's Challenge to Removal.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — federal court caption + "PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO REMAND."
2. **Introduction** — basis for remand: lack of subject-matter jurisdiction or procedural defect.
3. **Procedural Background** — state court action filing; removal date; this motion's timeliness (within 30 days for procedural; any time for jurisdictional).
4. **Argument**:
   - **No federal-question jurisdiction** — well-pleaded complaint lacks federal claim; federal defense or counterclaim insufficient (*Holmes Group, Inc. v. Vornado Air Circulation Sys., Inc.*, 535 U.S. 826 (2002)).
   - **No complete diversity** — identify shared citizenship; or amount-in-controversy below $75,000.
   - **Forum-defendant rule** (§ 1441(b)(2)) — a defendant properly served and a citizen of the state where action was brought defeats removal.
   - **CAFA exceptions** (§ 1332(d)(3)–(5)) — home-state · local-controversy · government action exclusions.
   - **Procedural defect** — late removal (>30 days after service); not all defendants consented (§ 1446(b)(2)(A)).
5. **Request for Fees** — § 1447(c) permits award of just costs and actual expenses, including attorney's fees, incurred as a result of the removal; reserved for cases where removal lacked objectively reasonable basis (*Martin v. Franklin Capital Corp.*, 546 U.S. 132 (2005)).
6. **Proposed Order.**
7. **Certificate of Service.**

## Drafting Conventions
- Plead with specificity any factual citizenship dispute — for corporations, plead state of incorporation + principal place of business; for LLCs, citizenship of every member.
- Amount-in-controversy on removal: defendant must show by preponderance (*Dart Cherokee*, 574 U.S. 81 (2014)); plaintiff can move to remand with evidence.
- Forum-defendant rule applies only to "properly joined and served" defendants — snap removal pre-service is split among circuits.
- Pre-emptive remand motions on the day of removal common in plaintiff-friendly fora.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A motion to remand the case on the basis of any defect other than lack of subject matter jurisdiction must be made within 30 days after the filing of the notice of removal under section 1446(a). If at any time before final judgment it appears that the district court lacks subject matter jurisdiction, the case shall be remanded." — 28 U.S.C. § 1447(c).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Critical plaintiff-side response to defense removal. Calendar 30-day clock from removal date; if jurisdictional, may file any time.
