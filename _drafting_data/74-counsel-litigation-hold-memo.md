# Litigation Hold Memo

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 37(e) (ESI loss sanctions)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_37)
- *Zubulake v. UBS Warburg LLC*, 220 F.R.D. 212 (S.D.N.Y. 2003).

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — Federal Rule 37(e) + duty-to-preserve common-law foundation.

## Currency Warning
Duty to preserve attaches when litigation is reasonably anticipated, not just commenced (*Zubulake*). Failure to issue and follow up on litigation hold can result in Rule 37(e) sanctions including adverse inference, default, or dismissal upon intent-to-deprive finding.

## Template Type
Internal Client Memo / Litigation Hold Notice.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **To: [Designated Recipients]** — broad — anyone potentially in possession of relevant materials.
2. **From: [In-House or Outside Counsel].**
3. **Date.**
4. **Re: Litigation Hold — Preservation of Documents and ESI in [Matter].**
5. **Notice of Pending or Anticipated Litigation** — generally describe the matter, parties, and subject matter.
6. **Duty to Preserve** — explanation of legal obligation; consequences of failure.
7. **Categories of Materials Subject to Hold**:
   - Documents (paper).
   - Emails, including drafts, sent items, deleted items.
   - Other electronic communications (Slack, Teams, SMS, mobile apps).
   - Files on shared drives, personal drives, OneDrive, SharePoint, Dropbox.
   - Backup tapes and snapshots.
   - Databases and SaaS systems.
   - Voicemails, video conferences, recorded calls.
   - Mobile device data.
   - Social media posts and communications.
   - Calendar entries.
   - Notebooks and notes.
7. **Custodians** — list of individuals required to preserve (or "all employees in the [department/function]").
8. **Specific Hold Categories** — by subject matter relevance.
9. **Suspension of Routine Destruction** — pause document-destruction schedules, automatic email deletion, system retention policies.
10. **Custodian Acknowledgment** — sign and return acknowledgment of receipt and understanding.
11. **Periodic Reissuance** — at intervals to refresh consciousness.
12. **Counsel Contact** — questions to specific attorney.

## Drafting Conventions
- Issue promptly when litigation anticipated; document the timing.
- Reissue periodically (every 6-12 months) and on major case events.
- Track recipient acknowledgments; non-acknowledgment is a red flag.
- For corporate clients, work with IT/InfoSec to disable automatic-purge systems and document the disabling.
- Modern ephemeral messaging (Slack, Teams) requires affirmative configuration to preserve.
- For BYOD devices, address phone-imaging vs. targeted-collection protocols.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A party's discovery obligations do not end with the implementation of a 'litigation hold' — to the contrary, that's only the beginning. Counsel must oversee compliance with the litigation hold, monitoring the party's efforts to retain and produce the relevant documents." — *Zubulake v. UBS Warburg LLC*, 229 F.R.D. 422, 432 (S.D.N.Y. 2004).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Foundation of every litigation engagement. Issue at intake.
