# Supply Agreement

## Source URLs
- [UCC Article 2 (Sales)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/ucc/2)
- [Hart-Scott-Rodino Act — 15 U.S.C. § 18a (where supplier-customer relationship triggers HSR)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/18a)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Common-law contract + UCC Article 2.

## Currency Warning
For requirements / output contracts (UCC § 2-306), quantity term must be made in good faith and not unreasonably disproportionate to any stated estimate or to any normal or otherwise comparable prior output / requirements.

## Template Type
Long-Term Supply Contract.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties + Recitals + Effective Date + Term.**
2. **Definitions** — Product; Specifications; Forecast; Minimum Order; Reasonable Notice.
3. **Supply Obligation**:
   - Exclusive / non-exclusive.
   - Requirements / output / fixed-quantity / take-or-pay structure.
   - Geographic scope.
4. **Forecasting and Ordering** — rolling forecast (binding vs. non-binding portions); order acceptance criteria; lead times.
5. **Pricing and Payment** — list prices; volume discounts; price escalators (CPI / cost-input indexing); most-favored-customer clause if any; currency.
6. **Quality and Specifications** — incorporation of Spec Schedule; right of inspection; testing; quality manuals.
7. **Delivery** — INCOTERMS; title and risk; force majeure; allocation in shortage (good-faith allocation under UCC § 2-615).
8. **Warranties** — conformity; non-infringement; performance.
9. **Indemnification** — IP; product-liability; tort.
10. **Insurance** — product liability + general liability + cyber.
11. **Limitation of Liability** — exclude consequential; cap; carve-outs (IP, indemnity, confidentiality).
12. **Confidentiality + IP.**
13. **Compliance** — FCPA; OFAC; export control; modern slavery; conflict minerals.
14. **Change Orders** — engineering change notices; vendor lead time for change implementation.
15. **Term, Renewal, Termination** — auto-renewal; for-cause termination; for-convenience (often vendor-unfriendly); transition assistance.
16. **Dispute Resolution + Governing Law + Forum.**
17. **Antitrust Considerations** — most-favored-nation / exclusivity / tying scrutiny (Sherman § 1, Clayton § 3).

## Drafting Conventions
- For requirements contracts, define minimum and maximum reasonably; courts apply good-faith and proportional-to-prior-output tests under UCC § 2-306.
- For exclusivity, document pro-competitive justifications; address Clayton § 3 risk for substantial market share.
- For HSR implications: long-term supply may be re-characterized as acquisition if exclusive + indefinite + with rights — verify under FTC HSR rules.
- For force majeure, define triggers narrowly; address pandemic / supply-chain disruption explicitly post-2020.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A term which measures the quantity by the output of the seller or the requirements of the buyer means such actual output or requirements as may occur in good faith, except that no quantity unreasonably disproportionate to any stated estimate or in the absence of a stated estimate to any normal or otherwise comparable prior output or requirements may be tendered or demanded." — UCC § 2-306(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard B2B supply contract. Pair with quality manual schedule + INCOTERMS schedule + compliance schedule.
