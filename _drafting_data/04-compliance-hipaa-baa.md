## Authority Tier
Primary — 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e) (required BAA provisions) + HITECH 2009 amendments.

## Currency Warning
Pending HHS-proposed 2024 Security Rule modernization (multi-factor authentication, encryption-by-default, vulnerability scanning, asset inventory) — verify final rule status before relying. Reproductive-health PHI special protections under HHS Final Rule April 2024.

## Template Type
Contract / Regulatory Compliance Annex.

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Parties** — Covered Entity (CE) and Business Associate (BA). Subcontractor BAs covered in flow-down clause.
2. **Definitions** — incorporate by reference 45 C.F.R. §§ 160.103, 164.103, 164.304, 164.501; "PHI" and "ePHI" defined.
3. **Permitted Uses and Disclosures** — BA may use/disclose PHI only:
   - As permitted/required by the BAA;
   - As required by law; and
   - For the proper management and administration of the BA / to carry out its legal responsibilities (with confidentiality assurances).
4. **Obligations of Business Associate** — § 164.504(e)(2)(ii):
   - Not use/disclose PHI other than as permitted.
   - Implement Security Rule safeguards for ePHI.
   - Report any use/disclosure not provided for + Security Incidents + breaches.
   - Ensure subcontractors agree to same restrictions (flow-down).
   - Provide access to individuals (§ 164.524) and amend (§ 164.526) on CE direction.
   - Provide accounting of disclosures (§ 164.528).
   - Make internal practices, books, records available to HHS Secretary.
   - On termination, return or destroy all PHI.
5. **Permitted Uses by BA for BA's Own Operations** — narrow.
6. **Breach Notification** — within reasonable time (the BAA may specify a number of days shorter than 60).
7. **Term and Termination** — CE may terminate on material breach; cure period optional.
8. **Indemnification + Insurance** — beyond HIPAA-required minima; cyber-liability coverage minima.
9. **Subcontractor Flow-Down.**
10. **Governing Law + Choice of Forum.**
11. **State-Law Overlay** — note that state laws more stringent than HIPAA are not preempted (45 C.F.R. § 160.203).

## Drafting Conventions
- The BAA is REQUIRED before BA receives PHI. CE faces direct enforcement if no BAA in place.
- HITECH made BAs directly liable to HHS for Security Rule violations and certain Privacy Rule violations.
- Breach notification timing may be shorter than 60 days under contract; default is 60 days under § 164.410.
- Avoid "all-purpose" BAAs; tie use-and-disclosure permissions to the actual services performed.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A covered entity may disclose protected health information to a business associate and may allow a business associate to create, receive, maintain, or transmit protected health information on its behalf, if the covered entity obtains satisfactory assurances that the business associate will appropriately safeguard the information." — 45 C.F.R. § 164.502(e)(1)(i).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard intake document for any vendor engagement with a healthcare-sector client (provider, plan, clearinghouse). Verify the vendor's actual services; the BAA's "permitted uses" should mirror the SOW.
