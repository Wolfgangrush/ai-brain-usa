# Third-Party Complaint (Rule 14 Impleader)

## Source URLs
- [Fed. R. Civ. P. 14](https://www.law.cornell.edu/rules/frcp/rule_14)

## Last Verified
2026-05-29

## Authority Tier
Primary — FRCP Rule 14.

## Currency Warning
Within 14 days after serving original answer, defendant may serve third-party summons and complaint without leave of court; thereafter requires leave. Third-party claims must allege derivative liability — "is or may be liable to it for all or part of the claim against it" — not joint or independent liability.

## Template Type
Pleading — Third-Party Complaint by Defendant (Third-Party Plaintiff).

## Structural Skeleton
1. **Caption** — main case caption + "DEFENDANT AND THIRD-PARTY PLAINTIFF'S THIRD-PARTY COMPLAINT AGAINST [Third-Party Defendant]."
2. **Parties to Third-Party Action** — Defendant/Third-Party Plaintiff v. Third-Party Defendant.
3. **Jurisdiction and Venue** — supplemental jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1367 + venue consideration.
4. **Factual Allegations** — incorporate by reference relevant paragraphs from plaintiff's complaint; allege the factual basis for derivative liability (indemnity, contribution, subrogation, breach of warranty, contractual passthrough).
5. **Counts of Derivative Liability** — Contribution count · Indemnity (contractual + common law) · Subrogation · Breach of Warranty · Breach of Contract (passthrough).
6. **Prayer for Relief** — judgment over for all or part of any judgment plaintiff obtains; declaratory relief on duty to defend.
7. **Jury Demand.**
8. **Signature Block.**

## Drafting Conventions
- File and serve within 14 days of original Answer to avoid leave-of-court requirement (Rule 14(a)(1)).
- Allege derivative-only liability — Rule 14 will not support a claim that third-party is independently liable to plaintiff.
- Third-party defendant may assert defenses against third-party complaint AND against original plaintiff's complaint (Rule 14(a)(2)(C)).
- Original plaintiff may assert claims directly against third-party defendant where arising out of same transaction (Rule 14(a)(3)).
- Service: Rule 4 (summons) required for third-party defendant.

## Fair-Use Excerpt
> "A defending party may, as third-party plaintiff, serve a summons and complaint on a nonparty who is or may be liable to it for all or part of the claim against it. But the third-party plaintiff must, by motion, obtain the court's leave if it files the third-party complaint more than 14 days after serving its original answer." — Fed. R. Civ. P. 14(a)(1).

## Practical Use for Solo Advocate / Small Firm
Standard tool in construction, product-liability, insurance-coverage, and indemnity disputes. Coordinate with original answer timing to avoid leave requirement.
