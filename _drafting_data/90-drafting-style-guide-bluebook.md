## Structural Skeleton

### Cases
- **Supreme Court** — *Twombly v. Bell Atl. Corp.*, 550 U.S. 544 (2007). (Year, U.S. citation; parallel S. Ct. citation typically dropped for SCOTUS.)
- **Courts of Appeals** — *Pearson v. Callahan*, 555 F.3d 564 (10th Cir. 2009). (Volume, F.3d, page, court abbreviation, year.)
- **District Courts** — *In re Adelphia Commc'ns Corp.*, 298 F. Supp. 2d 50, 53 (S.D.N.Y. 2003).
- **State Courts** — depend on jurisdiction; common form for state-of-last-resort.
- **Pin cites** — required for any specific proposition.

### Statutes
- **U.S. Code** — 28 U.S.C. § 1331 (current code year understood).
- **U.S. Code with section symbol** — 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3)(A).
- **Public Laws (pre-codification or non-codified)** — Pub. L. No. 116-260, 134 Stat. 1182 (2020).

### Rules and Regulations
- **Federal Rules** — Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6). Fed. R. Evid. 702.
- **Code of Federal Regulations** — 17 C.F.R. § 240.10b-5.

### Constitutional Provisions
- U.S. Const. art. III, § 2, cl. 1.
- U.S. Const. amend. IV.

### Secondary Sources
- **Books** — John Roe, *Federal Practice Treatise* § 2.1, at 35 (3d ed. 2024).
- **Articles** — Jane Doe, *Rule 11 Reform*, 75 Stan. L. Rev. 100 (2023).

### Signals
- **No signal** — directly supports.
- ***See*** — supports.
- ***See, e.g.*** — multiple authorities support; representative cite.
- ***Cf.*** — analogous support.
- ***But see*** — directly contradicts.
- ***See generally*** — background support.

### Short Forms
- *Twombly*, 550 U.S. at 555.
- Once full cite given, may use shortened name + at + pin cite for subsequent references in same section.

### Internal Cross-References
- *See infra* Part III.A.
- *See supra* note 5.

## Drafting Conventions

### Quotation Practice
- Block quotations for 50+ words (indented, single-spaced, no quote marks).
- For inline quotations, [brackets] for alterations; ellipses (… ) for omitted words.
- *See* Bluebook Rule 5.

### Bluebook vs. ALWD
- Most federal courts accept either; verify Local Rules and journal preferences.

### Practitioner Format vs. Academic
- Practitioner format uses regular italics; academic uses underlines in some publications. Stick to italics for federal court filings.

## Formatting

### Federal Court Briefs
- 12-point font (some courts allow 13 or 14).
- 1.5 line spacing or double-spacing per Local Rule.
- 1-inch margins.
- Word limit per Local Rule or by court order.
- Certificate of Compliance with type-volume.

### SCOTUS Briefs (Booklet Format)
- 6⅛ × 9¼ inches.
- 12 point or larger.
- Cover-color requirements per Sup. Ct. R. 33.1(g):
  - White — cert petition.
  - Light red — opposition.
  - Light yellow — reply at cert.
  - Light blue — opening brief on merits.
  - Light red — opposition on merits.
